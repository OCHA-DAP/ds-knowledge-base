# IFRC — Niger drought

## Summary
The Niger Red Cross Society's drought Early Action Protocol (EAP2021NE02, validated July
2021, DREF Forecast-based Action pillar), targeting the agropastoral livelihood zone of
Zinder region — Niger's most populous province. On trigger, it replenishes subsidized
cereal banks and disburses cash/vouchers for up to 4,000 households (~28,000 people) in
Damagaram-Takaya and Gouré departments. Activated once, in April 2022, amid the broader
2021–2022 Sahel food crisis.

## Trigger
Two-phase design tied to the agricultural calendar: an earlier check (~3-month lead,
assessed April–June) on early-season rainfall, and a later check (~5-month lead, assessed
November–March) combining end-of-season production conditions, the Cadre Harmonisé
food-insecurity phase, and staple-food market prices (reported up to ~40% above the
five-year average at the 2022 activation). The 2022 activation was driven by the second
(Nov–Mar) check, met in March 2022; given the severity of the crisis both phases were
triggered together rather than sequentially.

## Funding & scope
CHF 349,989 (≈US$0.37M) from the IFRC DREF's Forecast-based Action mechanism: CHF 27,228
for immediate readiness/pre-positioning, CHF 322,761 released automatically once the
trigger was met. Target: up to 4,000 agropastoral households (~28,000 people) for
cereal-bank replenishment, with 1,000 of the most vulnerable also receiving cash/vouchers,
across Damagaram-Takaya and Gouré departments, Zinder region.

## Activations
- **April 2022 (activated 19 April)** — the only known activation. Triggered by the
  Nov–Mar check (met March 2022); both trigger phases fired simultaneously given the
  worsening Sahel food crisis (39% drop in national cereal production that season). Final
  report (2024-07-15) records ~3,300 households (~23,100 people) reached via cereal banks
  and cash/voucher support. This activation directly preceded a much larger, separate
  IFRC Federation-wide Emergency Appeal (~CHF 5M, launched 9 May 2022, ~300,000 people
  targeted) for the same crisis — related but not the same operation or funding envelope.
