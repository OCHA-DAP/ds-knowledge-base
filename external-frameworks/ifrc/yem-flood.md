# IFRC — Yemen flood

## Summary
The Yemen Red Crescent Society's (YRCS) Simplified Early Action Protocol (sEAP2023YE01,
IFRC operation MDRYE015) — the first sEAP developed in IFRC's Middle East and North Africa
region — is pre-financed through the IFRC Disaster Relief Emergency Fund's (DREF)
Anticipatory Pillar. Approved in 2024, it activates ahead of forecast heavy rainfall to
release early actions (drainage clearing, sandbag distribution, emergency shelter, cash
grants, early-warning messaging) for at-risk communities, primarily displaced people in
flood-hazard sites. Its only confirmed activation to date (21-25 August 2025) covered
~15,000 IDPs in Hajjah governorate's Abs district.

## Trigger
Activates when national meteorological forecasts (Yemen's Civil Aviation and Meteorology
Authority) indicate heavy/high-accumulation rainfall likely to cause flooding. The August
2025 activation followed a forecast of high rainfall accumulation for 18-25 August 2025 over
Hajjah governorate, with early actions triggered on 21 August. Public sources describe the
trigger only in this plain-language form — no numeric rainfall threshold (mm) or lead-time
window (days) was found (see `extra.schema_strain`).

## Funding & scope
IFRC DREF Anticipatory Pillar. The overall sEAP appeal (MDRYE015) is CHF 220,000, fully
funded, covering 15,785 people, with an operational window from 23 July 2024 to 30 November
2025. The August 2025 activation itself released CHF 129,338 (~USD 160,379) for ~15,000
at-risk IDPs at the Al-Mithaq and Al-Mashta/Mushta sites, Abs district, Hajjah governorate.
Earlier IFRC/Climate Centre reporting on the sEAP's launch describes piloting in Sana'a and
Hadramout governorates as well (see `extra.coverage_note`).

## Activations
- **21-25 August 2025** (sEAP2023YE01/MDRYE015) — first confirmed activation: forecast of
  high rainfall accumulation over Hajjah governorate; CHF 129,338 released from the DREF
  Anticipatory Pillar for drainage clearing, sandbags, shelter kits, cash grants and
  early-warning messaging, reaching ~15,000 at-risk IDPs in Abs district.
- No activation is confirmed prior to 2025; the protocol reportedly could not be activated
  in time for earlier flood events because it had only just been approved.
- A separate, larger DREF operation (MDRYE016) responded to further 2025 flooding
  (56,000 people, several governorates) as a standard reactive emergency appeal, not a
  sEAP trigger (see `extra.non_eap_event`).
