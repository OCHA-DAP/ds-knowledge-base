# IFRC — Bangladesh cyclone EAP

## Summary
The Bangladesh Red Crescent Society's cyclone Early Action Protocol (3rd generation,
validated by IFRC, pre-financed by the DREF anticipatory pillar) — one of the oldest EAPs
anywhere (first approved Dec 2018). Early warning to ~150,000 people and shelter-based
food/water/ORS for 50,000 across 14 coastal districts, with the Cyclone Preparedness
Programme and BMD.

## Trigger
Two-stage. **Pre-activation** (≥72h): BMD Signal 4+ AND at least two of BMD/IMD/ECMWF/
GFS/JTWC forecast ≥125 km/h winds in coastal districts with potential damage to 25% of
household assets in affected unions. **Activation** (≥30h): BMD Signal 7+ AND BMD/IMD
forecast ≥125 km/h with the same damage criterion.

## Funding & scope
CHF 499,987 pre-arranged (≈US$0.56M): readiness + prepositioning + a trigger-released
tranche. Target: ~150,000 people warned, 50,000 served at shelters, 14 coastal districts.

## Activations
- **May 2020, Cyclone Amphan** — the one full activation to date (~20,000 people).
- Notable *non*-activations: Fani 2019 (trigger formally not met — a decision-quality
  success story), Mocha/Hamoon 2023, Remal 2024.
