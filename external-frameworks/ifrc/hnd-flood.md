# IFRC — Honduras flood

## Summary
The Honduran Red Cross's Early Action Protocol for floods caused by tropical storms
(current generation EAP2023HN03, Operation MDRHN020), validated by IFRC and pre-financed
by the DREF, with technical support from the German Red Cross and the RCRC Climate
Centre. Targets ~13,500 people (2,700 households) in flood-prone municipalities along the
Aguán, Patuca and Ulúa river basins — Tocoa, La Lima, San Manuel, Pimienta and Sabá —
with cash assistance and WASH early actions. One of the first EAPs activated anywhere in
Central America (first generation, alongside Guatemala's, in 2022).

## Trigger
Two-stage, both evaluated within roughly a 3-5 day window: (1) a NOAA/National Hurricane
Center forecast of ≥50-60% probability that a system intensifies into a tropical storm
(34kt+ winds) affecting Honduras, AND (2) a GLOFAS/GEOGLOWS river-discharge forecast
showing >50% probability of exceeding a 10-year return period in the Aguán, Patuca and
Ulúa basins. Both must be met to activate.

## Funding & scope
Current generation: DREF allocation reported as CHF 549,023 (≈US$623,182) — CHF 348,133
for readiness/prepositioning plus CHF 200,891 released automatically on trigger. Target:
~2,700 households (~13,500 people), Multipurpose Cash Transfer and WASH interventions,
prioritizing Tocoa (Colón department) among the five at-risk municipalities. First
generation (EAP2022HN01, 2022) carried CHF 360,829 for the same ~2,700-household target.

## Activations
- **Oct 2022, Tropical Storm Julia** — first activation, under EAP2022HN01 (the
  first-generation protocol). CHF 360,829 released; final report 4 May 2023.
- **14 Nov 2024, Tropical Storm Sara** — second activation, under the current
  EAP2023HN03/MDRHN020. Both triggers met; ~2,500 families targeted, prioritizing Tocoa.
