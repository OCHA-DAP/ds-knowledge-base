# IFRC — Tajikistan heatwave

## Summary
The Red Crescent Society of Tajikistan's (RCST) heatwave Early Action Protocol
(EAP2021TJ01), developed with the German Red Cross and validated by IFRC, financed
through the DREF Forecast-based Action mechanism. Grew out of a 2019-2021
forecast-based-financing pilot; formalized November 2021. As of the 2024 annual
report the EAP has never been activated — a pending budget revision delayed
readiness through 2024, with the Hub now listing an expanded current scope of
16,740 targeted people and an $826,899 budget.

## Trigger
Tajik Hydromet forecast of a heatwave with an estimated return period of five years
or less, during the seasonal hot months, giving RCST a 3-5 day lead time to act
before the event. The earlier 2019-2021 pilot phase used explicit thresholds
(>40.5°C in northern Tajikistan, >43.5°C in the south, ~10-year return period,
sustained ≥3 days) — the formalized EAP2021TJ01 documentation available publicly
describes the trigger in the looser return-period terms above; no lead-time or
temperature detail beyond the pilot figures was found for the current version.

## Funding & scope
Originally CHF 249,971 (≈USD 261,603) via the DREF FbA mechanism — CHF 65,470
readiness + CHF 102,006 pre-positioning + CHF 82,494 released automatically on
trigger — registered as DREF operation MDRTJ034 (approved 2022-02-24, fully funded,
valid through 2026-12-31), targeting 5,580 people in remote, poverty-line
communities in Khatlon (south) and Sughd (north) provinces. The Anticipation Hub's
current country page lists a larger, revised scope: 16,740 people targeted and an
$826,899 budget; no public document with the revised budget's funding-source
breakdown was found.

## Activations
None. The Hub's Tajikistan country page states 0 activations across both of its
active frameworks (heatwave and cold wave). The 2024 EAP annual report (published
2025-10-30) confirms the heatwave EAP was not activated that year: approval of the
revised budget was delayed by internal RCST restructuring, pushing pre-positioning
work to Year 3, so no early actions were implemented and no funds were expended.
