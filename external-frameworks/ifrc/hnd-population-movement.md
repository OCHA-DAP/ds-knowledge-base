# IFRC — Honduras population movement

## Summary
The Honduran Red Cross (HRC) runs a 5-year Early Action Protocol (EAP2023HN04, operation
MDRHN021, approved 18 December 2023) targeting people in transit — irregular migrants
moving through Honduras, largely northbound toward the US/Mexico border. Designed with
German Red Cross technical support and pre-financed from the DREF's Anticipatory Pillar,
it is, per the Anticipation Hub, the first EAP submitted by any National Society for
population movement/displacement rather than a weather/climate hazard. It targets 10,000
people with health, WASH, migration/family-links and protection support at humanitarian
service points along transit routes.

## Trigger
Single trigger, declared by the Migration Roundtable's Steering Committee (National
President and General Director of HRC, IFRC and ICRC heads of mission, and Participating
National Society delegates including German Red Cross; quorum of 4 of 5). The Steering
Committee activates when: (1) two consecutive months show a positive migrant-flow trend;
(2) the projection for the following month exceeds 23,000 people crossing the border (5%
of the historical highest monthly flow); and (3) HRC's response capacity is assessed to
have dropped 40% below what the projected flow would require. Lead time is 30-90 days.
Monitoring draws on fortnightly reports from Honduras's National Migration Institute
(INM), monthly data from Panama's National Migration Service and the IFRC GO Central
America Population Movement dashboard, and quarterly IOM DTM reports; a Technical
Committee reviews these and prepares the report the Steering Committee acts on. The EAP
has national scope rather than a fixed department list, since migratory routes shift with
regional dynamics — but names the highest-transit border points (Corinto, El Florido,
Agua Caliente, El Poy, Las Manos, Guasaule, among others) as likely intervention sites.

## Funding & scope
CHF 549,995 total, entirely pre-arranged from the DREF Anticipatory Pillar (appeal
MDR00001): CHF 148,355 readiness + CHF 210,625 pre-positioned stock (the "CHF 358,980
immediate allocation" in the EAP summary) + CHF 191,016 released automatically on
trigger. Target: 10,000 people (5,000/5,000 roughly by sex across sectors), reached via
Health & Care (4,000 people, transit first-aid and psychosocial kits, a mobile health
unit), WASH (10,000 people, hygiene kits and water supply/purchase), Migration/Restoring
Family Links (10,000 people, connectivity equipment, protection referrals) and Community
Engagement & Accountability (anti-stigma communication). Operational timeframe once
triggered: 3 months.

## Activations
None confirmed as of this check. IFRC's GO platform lists appeal MDRHN021 as active and
fully funded from 2023-12-19 to 2028-12-31, but that is the standing readiness/
pre-positioning window for the EAP's full 5-year lifetime, not evidence the trigger has
fired. ReliefWeb hosts EAP annual-report updates from April 2025 and March 2026 that
likely record trigger-monitoring outcomes, but they returned HTTP 403 to the
unauthenticated fetcher used for this page — flagged for manual follow-up (see
