# IFRC — Mozambique cyclone EAP

## Summary
The Mozambique Red Cross (CVM) cyclone Early Action Protocol (2nd generation, IFRC
DREF anticipatory pillar), focused on house/school reinforcement and WASH ahead of
landfall — activated three times (once under the exceptional lower threshold, once
partially), which makes it one of the most exercised EAPs in the portfolio.

## Trigger
INAM 72-hour forecast of ≥120 km/h winds at landfall (~1-in-5-year event), conditional on
a government disaster alert (CTGD/INGD). An exceptional clause allows activation at
≥100 km/h by CVM–IFRC expert consensus where vulnerability is heightened (e.g. an area
recently hit) — used for both Chalane 2020 and Jude 2025.

## Funding & scope
CHF 549,957 pre-arranged (≈US$0.62M); 2,000 households / 10,000 people per activation,
max 3 districts simultaneously.

## Activations
- **Dec 2020, TS Chalane** — first activation (expert-consensus below-threshold call).
- **Mar 2025, Cyclone Jude** — full activation (100 km/h clause), 2,000 households in ~24h.
- **Feb 2026, Cyclone Gezani** — partial (no-regret actions only, scaled back with the forecast).
- Non-activation: Eloise (Jan 2021), trigger not met at decision time.
