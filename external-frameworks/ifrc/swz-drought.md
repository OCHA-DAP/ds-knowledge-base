# IFRC — Eswatini drought

## Summary
The Baphalali Eswatini Red Cross Society (BERCS) runs an IFRC-endorsed drought Early Action
Protocol (EAP2023SZ01, operation MDRSZ005), pre-financed from the DREF's Anticipatory
Pillar, covering the Lowveld region — Eswatini's most drought-prone area with the least
coping capacity. It targets up to 10,800 people and uses a two-step trigger built on a
seasonal rainfall forecast plus a purpose-built drought index. It activated for the first
time during the 2023/24 El Nino season, with an activation final report published
18 March 2024.

## Trigger
A two-step design for the October-March rainy season:
- **Step 1 (readiness, ~September)**: triggered by a "below-normal" seasonal rainfall
  forecast from Eswatini Meteorological Services, roughly a 3-month lead time.
- **Step 2 (early action, ~mid-January)**: triggered by a low seasonally-averaged Composite
  Drought Index for Eswatini (CDI-E) — a drought index NDMA developed and shared historical
  data for, with trigger design support from the IFRC/Red Cross Red Crescent Climate Centre,
  addressing gaps the National Society found in forecast data availability and data-sharing
  between agencies.

## Funding & scope
DREF's Anticipatory Pillar approved CHF 539,143 total: CHF 103,232 for readiness and
pre-positioning, plus CHF 435,910 to implement early actions once triggers are met. IFRC's
GO platform lists the MDRSZ005 appeal as fully funded at $546,685, running 2024-03-15 to
2029-03-31 (a roughly 5-year EAP lifetime) with 10,800 people targeted — those in the
Lowveld areas with the least coping capacity for drought impacts (livestock death, water
shortages, crop failure, income loss, and food insecurity).

## Activations
- **Activation final report published 2024-03-18** — the EAP's first known activation, during
  the strong 2023/24 El Nino drought season (the Government of Eswatini's NDMA requested
  humanitarian intervention in September 2023 amid an IPC estimate of ~238,500 people facing
  acute food insecurity). Per the two-step design, readiness likely triggered around
  September 2023 and the early-action step around mid-January 2024, though the exact
  trigger-crossing date, funds released, and specific actions delivered could not be
  confirmed — ReliefWeb blocks automated fetches of the full report (HTTP 403).
- A separate ReliefWeb entry, an "Early Action Protocol Annual Report" for MDRSZ005 dated
  December 2024, appears to be routine monitoring rather than a distinct second activation
  (unconfirmed).
- No activations known before EAP2023SZ01's approval (2023).
