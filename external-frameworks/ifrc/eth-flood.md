# IFRC — Ethiopia flood

## Summary
The Ethiopian Red Cross Society's (ERCS) flood Early Action Protocol (EAP2021ET01),
validated by IFRC and pre-financed through the DREF's Forecast-based Action (FbA)
mechanism, with technical support from the Netherlands Red Cross, 510 and the RCRC
Climate Centre under the IKEA Foundation-funded IARP programme. It covers riverine and
flash flooding across 31 woredas in Afar, Somali, Gambella, Amhara and other regions,
targeting pastoralist and agro-pastoralist communities settled along riverbanks, mountain
slopes and low-lying areas.

## Trigger
GLOFAS-forecast river discharge reaching or exceeding the 1-in-10-year return-period
level with at least 75% forecast probability, giving a 7-day lead time; corroborated by
the National Meteorological Agency's WRF model 10-day precipitation forecast, seasonal
forecasts and Basin Development Authority water-level readings. An automated system
alerts Technical Working Group members by email/mobile when the trigger is reached; the
TWG convenes within 24 hours to authorise implementation. Activation is called off if
discharge has not increased by day 4 and forecast confidence has dropped (below 85%
probability, false-alarm ratio above 0.3).

## Funding & scope
CHF 350,000 pre-arranged via the DREF FbA mechanism (≈US$397,276): CHF 79,731 readiness
+ CHF 22,046 pre-positioning stock + CHF 248,223 for early-action implementation once
triggered (sector breakdown: DRR CHF 133,865, livelihoods/basic needs CHF 112,187,
National Society capacity CHF 60,629, WASH CHF 26,210, shelter CHF 3,674). Target: 5,650
households (~28,250 people) across the 31 target woredas.

## Activations
No confirmed trigger activation found in public reporting. The Hub inventory lists 2022,
2023 and 2024 as active years for this framework, but this appears to reflect the EAP
being live/socialised rather than a documented activation event. The August 2024
Southern Ethiopia landslides/floods response (emergency appeal MDRET036, CHF 6M, ~430,000
people affected) was a standard post-event emergency appeal, not an EAP/FbA trigger. A
joint simulation exercise tested the Afar-region component of the EAP in Samara,
18-20 May 2026, under the DG ECHO-funded Disaster Preparedness Project (2004,
2024-2026) with the Netherlands and Danish Red Cross.
