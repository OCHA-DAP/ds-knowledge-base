# IFRC — Costa Rica volcanic ash

## Summary
The Costa Rican Red Cross's (CRRC) Early Action Protocol for volcanic ash dispersion and
fall (EAP2022CR01, approved June 2022), pre-financed by the IFRC DREF's Forecast-based
Action (FbA) mechanism. Designed with the German Red Cross, the RCRC Climate Centre, the
National Commission for Risk Prevention and Emergency Attention (CNE), and the volcano
observatory OVSICORI-UNA, it covers Costa Rica's most active volcanoes (historically
Arenal, Irazú, Poás, Rincón de la Vieja, Turrialba). It targets 10,000 people (2,000
families) with health, livelihoods (cash), and WASH early actions ahead of forecast
ashfall.

## Trigger
Two-stage, both issued by OVSICORI. **Trigger 1** (readiness): a rise in volcanological
activity level of ≥3 grades on OVSICORI's scale, generating a special report that
verifies pre-positioning and starts awareness activities. **Trigger 2** (early-action
release): OVSICORI reports an eruption column ≥6km above the crater sustained for ≥1
hour; the ASH3D numerical dispersion model (run by OVSICORI and cross-checked by LAQAT-
UNA, ~50% forecast skill) is then used to identify districts forecast to receive ≥2mm of
ashfall, which sets the priority areas for cash transfers, health/protection kits, and
WASH supplies. Impact thresholds for triggering scale with ash depth (2–5mm, 5–10mm,
10–300mm) calibrated against historical crop, livestock, and human-health damage; an
average per-volcano return period of ~200 years is cited for the underlying risk model.

## Funding & scope
CHF 383,103 pre-arranged via the DREF's anticipatory (FbA) pillar: CHF 254,568 for
readiness and pre-positioning (immediate allocation) plus CHF 128,534 released
automatically once Trigger 2 is met, split roughly CHF 157,067 livelihoods/cash, CHF
84,625 health, CHF 40,853 WASH, CHF 65,913 National Society strengthening. Targets
10,000 people / 2,000 families in high-exposure districts near active volcanoes. EAP
runs 5 years from its June 2022 approval (~through 2027), with a 3-month window to
execute early actions once triggered.

## Activations
No full EAP trigger activation is documented. The closest event is the **2024 Poás
volcano green alert**: the Costa Rican Red Cross and IFRC launched an imminent DREF
operation (MDRCR025, CHF/USD 149,498, targeting 11,850 people in six cantons of Alajuela)
in anticipation of possible ashfall, but publicly confirmed the EAP's own trigger
conditions were **not** met — it was run as a simulation of the ash protocol and a
lessons-learned exercise rather than a real activation.
