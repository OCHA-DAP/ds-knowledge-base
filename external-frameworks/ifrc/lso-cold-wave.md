# IFRC — Lesotho cold wave

## Summary
The Lesotho Red Cross Society's Early Action Protocol for cold wave and heavy snowfall
(EAP2023LS02/MDRLS010), approved by IFRC 1 October 2024 and funded through the DREF
Anticipatory Pillar. It pre-arranges CHF 550,000 (~US$567k) over an unusually long 5-year
window (Oct 2024-Oct 2029) to protect 20,000 people across five highland/mountain
districts from the impacts of extreme cold and prolonged snow cover.

## Trigger
A forecast-based trigger with a 4-day lead time to early action, covering extreme
cold/heavy snowfall conditions in the Lesotho highlands. The publicly accessible sources
(ReliefWeb summary metadata, Anticipation Hub country page, 510.global reporting on a 2025
technical review conducted with ECMWF) do not spell out the specific meteorological
indicator or numeric threshold — the authoritative EAP PDF could not be text-extracted by
available tooling and ReliefWeb blocks automated fetches. See `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
CHF 550,000 total (~US$567,144), split into readiness (259,600), prepositioning (83,480),
and early-action (206,920) tranches, funded via the DREF Anticipatory Pillar. Targets
20,000 people in Maseru, Mokhotlong, Qacha's Nek, Butha-Buthe, and Quthing — highland
districts most exposed to heavy snow and cold-related isolation. Prioritised groups:
elderly people, orphans/vulnerable children, people with disabilities, school-aged
children, and herders. Planned early actions centre on early-warning messaging and
unconditional cash grants to cover basic needs.

## Activations
None confirmed in accessible public sources as of 2026-07-15. A ReliefWeb-listed "Annual
Report, 17 April 2026" for the same EAP code exists but was not fetchable (403) — it may
contain activation detail for the 2025/26 winter; flagged for a follow-up check rather than
guessed at here.
