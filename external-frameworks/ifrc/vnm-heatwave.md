# IFRC — Vietnam heatwave

## Summary
The Vietnam Red Cross Society's (VNRC) heatwave Early Action Protocol (EAP2021VN01),
validated by IFRC with German Red Cross support, covering Hanoi (15 highest-risk wards),
Da Nang, and Hai Phong. Grew out of the 2018-2021 "FbF Ready" pilot — the first
forecast-based-financing project to target heat waves and the first in an urban setting.
Triggers cooling centres, mobile cooling tents/buses, and awareness campaigns for roughly
25,500 targeted street workers, informal-settlement residents, and other outdoor-exposed
people.

## Trigger
A 6-day-lead heat index forecast for the target cities exceeding the 99th percentile in Da
Nang (~47.13°C) or the 97th percentile in Hanoi/Hai Phong (~48.16°C) for two or more
consecutive days puts the protocol on readiness; if the forecast still holds at a 3-day
lead time, early actions activate (a stop mechanism halts them if the forecast changes).
Forecasts come from Vietnam's National Centre for Hydro-Meteorological Forecasting (NCHMF)
and a Hanoi-specific heat index developed with the Vietnam Institute of Meteorology,
Hydrology and Climate Change (IMHEN). This refines an earlier ("2x2") pilot-phase trigger —
forecast temperature >37°C AND heat index >99th percentile, both for ≥2 days — tested
informally in 2019 before the EAP's 2021 approval.

## Funding & scope
CHF 225,285 pre-arranged via the DREF (~US$225,286 per IFRC GO reporting), of which CHF
111,390 covers stock pre-positioning and annual readiness activities, the remainder
released on trigger. Target: ~25,525 people — street vendors, outdoor workers, and informal
settlement residents in Hanoi's 15 highest-risk wards (8 urban districts), extended to Da
Nang and Hai Phong. Early actions: community cooling centres and mobile cooling
tents/buses, plus awareness campaigns (slum visits, megaphone messaging, social media).

## Activations
None confirmed as a formal EAP activation. Two unofficial pilot activations occurred in
2019, before the EAP was approved (396 people sheltered in 2 cooling centres on the first
trigger-threshold reach; 1,787 people in 4 centres on the second) — these tested the design
but predate the protocol itself. Per the 2023 annual report, forecast temperatures
approached the trigger at least twice in both 2022 and 2023, but the EAP was not activated
(unexpected temperature fluctuations and internal VNRC approval delays). No activation was
found in public sources for 2024 or 2025 either, though the relevant ReliefWeb annual
reports could not be fully fetched for this page (see `extra.schema_strain`).
