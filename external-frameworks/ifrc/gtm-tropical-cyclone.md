# IFRC — Guatemala tropical cyclone

## Summary
The Guatemalan Red Cross's Early Action Protocol for floods associated with tropical
cyclones (2nd generation, EAP2024GT02, IFRC DREF anticipatory pillar), with technical
support from the German Red Cross and scientific support from the RCRC Climate Centre.
Targets 14,000 people across four flood-prone watersheds (Motagua, Salinas, Samalá, and
Suchitepéquez) with multi-purpose cash, health/first-aid readiness, and risk-reduction
messaging. Activated once, in 2022 under its first generation, for Hurricane Julia — the
first-ever EAP activation anywhere in Central America.

## Trigger
Two independent triggers, either sufficient to activate. **Trigger 1**: INSIVUMEH or the
NOAA National Hurricane Center forecasts ≥60% probability that a tropical storm (≥63 km/h
winds) or stronger will track into Guatemala's cyclone emergency zone, with a 3-5 day
lead time. **Trigger 2**: GLoFAS forecasts >50% probability of a flood at or above a
3-year return period in high-flood-risk areas, 3-day lead time. The hurricane forecast
comes from NOAA (relayed via INSIVUMEH); GLoFAS forecast skill is quoted at FAR 0.53.

## Funding & scope
CHF 547,206 pre-arranged from the DREF Anticipatory Pillar (≈US$613k; readiness CHF
225,021 + pre-positioning CHF 17,213 + early-action tranche CHF 304,972). Targets 14,000
people, dominated by multi-purpose cash (9,800 people) plus risk-reduction/climate
messaging (14,000) and first-aid readiness (100 volunteers). Coverage: Izabal, Alta
Verapaz, Retalhuleu, and Suchitepéquez departments, via the Guatemalan Red Cross branches
in Puerto Barrios, Cobán, Retalhuleu, and Mazatenango.

## Activations
- **October 2022, Hurricane/Tropical Storm Julia** — the only activation to date, under
  the 1st-generation EAP2022GT01. Trigger met in two stages (hurricane confirmation 6
  Oct, GLoFAS flood probability 7 Oct); targeted 3,000 families (~15,000 people) with
  CHF ~478,800 allocated, but the final report found only 708 families actually reached
  with cash, in Izabal department, since the activation covered just part of the
  planned high-risk area.
