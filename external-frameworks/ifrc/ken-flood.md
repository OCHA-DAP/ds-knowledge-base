# IFRC — Kenya flood

## Summary
The Kenya Red Cross Society's national riverine-flood Early Action Protocol (EAP2021KE01,
approved 4 October 2021), pre-financed through the IFRC DREF's Forecast-based Action (FbA)
pillar. Developed with a national FbF Technical Working Group chaired by the National
Disaster Operation Centre, it targets 210,240 people (35,040 households) across
flood-prone areas, initially prioritizing the Nzoia, Tana and Athi river basins. Activated
once to date, in November 2023.

## Trigger
Two-step process. **Pre-activation**: KMD and/or ICPAC WRF-model rainfall forecasts
indicating ≥150mm of rain over an upcoming 7-10 day period. **Activation** (7-day GloFAS
lead time, cross-checked against a 3-day KMD forecast): GloFAS forecasts an 85%-probability
exceedance of the basin's 5-year-return-period discharge at defined stations — Nzoia
(5.3m / 469.8 m³/s), Athi (469.236 m³/s) or Tana (5.1m / 1,191.6 m³/s). A **stop
mechanism** on day 4 cancels the activation if GloFAS shows <40% probability of flooding
in the next 3 days and KMD's forecast agrees the threshold won't be met.

## Funding & scope
CHF 332,278 (≈US$348,260) from the DREF FbA mechanism: CHF 33,019 readiness + CHF 106,561
pre-positioning (released on EAP approval) + CHF 192,698 released automatically once the
trigger is met. Early actions (7-day window once activated) include flood-marker
placement, early-warning dissemination, physical evacuation support (conditional cash/
vouchers), livestock vaccination, and prepositioned shelter/WASH NFIs. Target: 210,240
people overall; a shelter-specific tranche covers ~1,000 households (6,000 people) in
Tana River, Garissa, Kisumu, Busia, Homabay and Migori counties.

## Activations
- **11 November 2023** — El Niño-driven Tana River floods. Garissa Bridge gauge exceeded
  5m and the Hola gauge reached its extreme-flood threshold (4 November); CHF 192,698
  (≈US$214,000) released to support ~150,000 people (shelter, livelihoods/cash, mobile
  health, WASH, evacuation assistance). Reported as an *exceptional* activation approved
  by IFRC's EAP validation committee on observed river levels, rather than the pre-agreed
  GloFAS forecast trigger being formally met.
- No other activations known.
