# IFRC — Mozambique flood EAP

## Summary
The Mozambique Red Cross Society (CVM) flood Early Action Protocol (current generation
EAP2023MZ04, IFRC DREF anticipatory pillar, with German and Belgian Red Cross support),
covering four river basins prone to seasonal flooding. Activated three times since 2022
(once as a pre-generation DREF Imminent Crisis response, twice under the current EAP),
making it one of the more frequently exercised flood EAPs in the IFRC portfolio.

## Trigger
A 72-hour hydrological forecast, based on INAM meteorological input and DNGRH/ARA river
gauge monitoring, that water levels at a reference station in one of four basins —
Limpopo, Buzi, Zambeze, or Licungo — will reach the 1-in-5-year return-period discharge
level. Each basin has its own threshold and can trigger independently; a single
activation can therefore cover one or several basins at once (e.g. Licungo and Limpopo
both triggered in February 2025).

## Funding & scope
CHF 549,929 pre-arranged with DREF (≈US$624,210: 357,352 readiness/prepositioning +
192,578 released on trigger), targeting 10,000 people per activation across 12 priority
downstream districts in Gaza, Sofala and Zambezia provinces. Funded via the DREF
anticipatory pillar with German Red Cross (incl. German Foreign Office funds) and
Belgian Red Cross support; implemented by CVM in coordination with INAM, DNGRH and
regional ARA water authorities.

## Activations
- **Jan 2022, Tropical Storm Ana** — the cyclone-EAP wind trigger wasn't met, but a DREF
  Imminent Crisis allocation (CHF 249,327) funded early action as the storm caused
  flooding in four provinces; ~855 people reached. This predates EAP2023MZ04.
- **Feb 2025** — first activation of the current EAP2023MZ04/MDRMZ022: Licungo and
  Limpopo basin triggers both reached; ~10,000 people / ~1,933 households reached with
  evacuation support and NFIs.
- **Dec 2025** — Licungo basin trigger reached again (Maganja da Costa district); CHF
  178,853 released for a 5-day anticipatory-action window, ~37,033 people at risk
  in-basin.
