# IFRC — Colombia drought

## Summary
The Colombian Red Cross Society (CRCS) runs a Simplified Early Action Protocol (sEAP2023CO01,
operation MDRCO02) for drought, approved 19 November 2024 and pre-financed from the IFRC DREF's
Anticipatory Pillar — CRCS's first Early Action Protocol of any kind. It targets meteorological
drought in communities of Colombia's Caribbean and/or Andean regions, with a total budget of CHF
216,996 (~US$246,306) reaching up to 2,400 people via livelihoods support, multipurpose cash,
water/sanitation/hygiene assistance and community engagement. It triggered for the first time on
11 June 2026, in the departments of Tolima and Cesar, reported jointly with simultaneous EAP
activations in El Salvador and Guatemala.

## Trigger
IDEAM (Colombia's national hydrology/meteorology institute) is monitored continuously through
three products: drought-index monitoring bulletins, climate-prediction bulletins, and SPI
(Standardised Precipitation Index) forecasts. The EAP activates when IDEAM's rolling 3-month
precipitation forecast shows a **≥50% probability that rainfall will be below normal**, **and**
the SPI forecast for the same period indicates moderate drought (**SPI ≤ -1.4**), in departments
of the Caribbean and/or Andean regions. Lead time is approximately 3 months, with a 3-month
operational timeframe once triggered. On activation, IDEAM's official report goes to IFRC and
German Red Cross, national and sectional (branch) technical teams are briefed, departmental
disaster-risk councils are informed, and field teams deploy.

## Funding & scope
CHF 216,996 total (~US$246,306 at ~1.135 CHF/USD): CHF 54,010 readiness, CHF 48,846
prepositioning, CHF 114,140 early-action tranche, all from the DREF Anticipatory Pillar. Sector
allocations: Livelihoods CHF 30,219 (600 people — nutritional blocks for livestock, hydro-retainers
for crops), Multipurpose Cash CHF 46,587 (900 people — unconditional cash transfers of 480,000 COP
per family), Water, Sanitation & Hygiene CHF 58,912 (900 people — household water-treatment/storage
kits, water-source diagnostics, mobile treatment plant), and Community Engagement & Accountability
CHF 5,859 (480 people). Target population overall: 2,400 people, prioritizing small/medium farming
and livestock households, households with limited access to drinking water, and those with young
children, older adults, disabilities or no regular income. Priority geography is framework-wide
"communities in the Caribbean and/or Andean region"; the 2026 activation narrowed to Tolima and
Cesar departments.

## Activations
- **11 June 2026** — first-ever activation, roughly 19 months after EAP approval. IDEAM's
  forecast showed "more than 50 per cent probability of below-normal rainfall and a moderate
  drought index by August," meeting both trigger legs. Reported together with simultaneous EAP
  activations by the El Salvador and Guatemala Red Cross Societies ("at the first signs of
  drought"), assisting a combined ~22,000 people across the three countries from a CHF 1.2M
  (~US$1.5M) DREF Anticipatory Pillar envelope. Colombia's share targeted its full 2,400-person
  caseload in Tolima and Cesar, delivering cash transfers, safe drinking water, crop/livestock
  protection inputs, and training in adaptive farming, hygiene and heat-illness prevention. The
  exact CHF amount disbursed to Colombia specifically is not stated in the sources found.
- No prior activations known (approved Nov 2024, first fired Jun 2026).
