# IFRC — Kazakhstan cold wave

## Summary
The Red Crescent Society of the Republic of Kazakhstan's Simplified Early Action Protocol
for cold waves (EAP2022KZ01/MDRKZ012), approved by IFRC 29 September 2022 and funded
through the DREF Anticipatory Pillar. It pre-arranges CHF 219,998 (~US$220k) to protect
~82,000 people (2,000 direct) across seven northern/central/eastern regions from
hypothermia, cold-related injuries, and stranded-traveller risk during extreme cold snaps.

## Trigger
Two-stage, keyed to Kazhydromet (national meteorological service) forecasts. **Stage 1**:
the 7-day forecast shows minimum temperature below -40°C (-35°C in Karaganda region) with
precipitation and elevated wind, persisting at least two days in any targeted district —
"orange" warning level or higher — at which point the National Society begins
preparation. **Stage 2**: the 3-day Kazhydromet alert map confirms the same threshold,
initiating early actions. Lead time: 7 days.

## Funding & scope
CHF 219,998 total (~US$220k), split readiness / prepositioning / early-action tranches
funded via the DREF Anticipatory Pillar (Forecast-based Action by the DREF). Targets
homeless people (incl. street children), older people living alone, low-income single
mothers, people with special needs, and stranded drivers/passengers, across Northern
Kazakhstan, Pavlodar, Kostanay, Eastern Kazakhstan/Abay, Akmola, Karaganda and Astana
city — the regions with the lowest recorded absolute minimum temperatures. Target: 82,000
people (2,000 direct assistance — hot meals, NFIs, heaters; 80,000 via awareness-raising
and first-aid messaging).

## Activations
- **11 December 2023** — the only activation on record. Kazhydromet forecast minimum
  temperatures below -40°C (locally to -45°C) in North Kazakhstan region; CHF 67,978
  (≈US$78,000/€71,500) released from the DREF for hot meals, transport of homeless people
  to warm-up shelters, NFI distribution (winter clothes, blankets, heaters) and road-safety/
  first-aid awareness for stranded drivers. ~2,000 people directly assisted, ~80,000 reached
  through awareness activities.
