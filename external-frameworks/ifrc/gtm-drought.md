# IFRC — Guatemala drought

## Summary
The Guatemalan Red Cross's (GRC) drought Early Action Protocol, validated by IFRC and
pre-financed by the DREF Anticipatory Pillar (EAP2023GT03, operation MDRGT023, approved
14 May 2024, valid through May 2029). It targets extreme meteorological drought in the
Guatemalan Dry Corridor with a two-trigger, ENSO/precipitation-forecast design. This is a
separate, independently funded and triggered protocol from the OCHA/CERF collective AA
framework that also covers drought in Guatemala's Dry Corridor (see `extra.coordination`) —
the two are not the same mechanism, despite the geographic and hazard overlap.

## Trigger
Two triggers, both based on forecasts from INSIVUMEH (Guatemala's national meteorological
institute), with a combined 2–3 month lead time:
- **Trigger 1** — INSIVUMEH/IRI forecasts a ≥70% probability of an El Niño event in ENSO
  region 3.4 for the next 3 months, issued from June.
- **Trigger 2** — INSIVUMEH issues a seasonal precipitation bulletin (from June onward)
  forecasting ≥50% probability of rainfall below the first tercile (33rd percentile) over the
  next 3 months in the Dry Corridor.

Both triggers must be met to fully activate the EAP; a "stop mechanism" halts action if a
later forecast update no longer confirms Trigger 2. The intervention zone (prioritized by a
drought risk index combining exposure, vulnerability and lack of response capacity) covers
municipalities in Quetzaltenango, Chiquimula, Jalapa, Sacapulas (Quiché), Retalhuleu and San
Lorenzo (Suchitepéquez).

## Funding & scope
CHF 546,439 pre-arranged via the DREF Anticipatory Pillar (≈US$620,249): CHF 250,145 for
readiness and prepositioning, CHF 296,295 for the early-action tranche once triggers are met.
Overall EAP target is 14,000 people, delivered mainly through multi-purpose cash (7,700
people), WASH (3,500 people) and risk-reduction/climate-adaptation activities (14,000 people).

## Activations
- **June 2026 — full activation.** Reported together with simultaneous EAP activations by the
  El Salvador and Colombia Red Cross Societies "at the first signs of drought." Per IFRC,
  ~10,000 of the 14,000-person target were assisted in Chiquimula, Jalapa and Quetzaltenango
  via IFRC-DREF cash and other pre-agreed early actions. No activation is recorded for 2024 or
  2025.
