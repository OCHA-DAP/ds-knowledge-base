# IFRC — Honduras tropical cyclone

## Summary
The Honduran Red Cross Society's first-generation Early Action Protocol for flooding
associated with tropical storms (EAP2022HN01, validated by IFRC, pre-financed by the
DREF anticipatory/Forecast-based Action pillar), developed with German Red Cross and
RCRC Climate Centre support and coordinated with CENAOS/COPECO. Approved June 2022,
targeting ~2,700 families (~13,500 people) with multipurpose cash and WASH assistance
ahead of storm-associated flooding.

## Trigger
Two triggers, both required to release funds. **Trigger 1** (3-5 day lead): CENAOS or
NOAA forecast ≥60% probability that a tropical storm (34kt winds) or higher will reach
the alert zone. **Trigger 2** (3-day lead): GEOGLOWS/GloFAS forecast >50% probability of
river flooding exceeding the 10-year return period in high-risk areas. Prioritized
impacts addressed: loss of household/livelihood assets (multipurpose cash) and
gastrointestinal/skin disease risk from unsafe water (WASH).

## Funding & scope
CHF 360,829 pre-arranged from the DREF's Forecast-based Action mechanism (≈US$378,185):
CHF 232,476 immediate for readiness and pre-positioning, plus CHF 128,353 released
automatically on trigger. Target: ~2,700 families (~13,500 people).

## Activations
- **October 2022, Hurricane Julia** — the only activation under this generation
  (EAP2022HN01). Both triggers met (6-7 Oct); early actions (cash to 700 families, WASH
  support) implemented 7-13 Oct ahead of flood peaks. Final report 4 May 2023.

The program continued under later generations — EAP2023HN03 (Dec 2023, activated for
Tropical Storm Sara Nov 2024) and a further EAP2025HN05 (Oct 2025) — which the
Anticipation Hub catalogues under hazard=flood rather than tropical-cyclone; see
`external-frameworks/ifrc/hnd-flood.md`. Not an independent framework — same HRC
program, same operation number (MDRHN020) through the 2023 revision.
