# IFRC — Tajikistan cold wave

## Summary
The Red Crescent Society of Tajikistan's Early Action Protocol for cold waves
(EAP2022TJ02/MDRTJ033), approved by IFRC 24 November 2022 and funded through the DREF
Anticipatory Pillar. It pre-arranges CHF 274,969 (~US$244k after a later downward
revision) to protect 6,565 people — single-parent households, the elderly, and poor
households with disabled or chronically ill members — across Gorno-Badakhshan Autonomous
Oblast (GBAO), the Districts of Republican Subordination, Sughd and Khatlon from extreme
cold. Developed since 2019 with the German Red Cross and the Red Cross Red Crescent
Climate Centre.

## Trigger
Forecast-based, using the Hydromet Agency of Tajikistan (with a CoES — Committee of
Emergency Situations — alert). Activates when the forecast shows temperature at or below
-39.0°C (the 1st percentile of historical extremes) in GBAO, or at or below -21.1°C (2nd
percentile) in the Districts of Republican Subordination, Sughd or Khatlon, persisting 3
or more consecutive days. Lead time: 4 days before the cold wave strikes. Planned early
actions include NFI distribution (electric oil heaters, window/floor insulation materials,
thermos flasks) and a 7-day food ration for a family of five.

## Funding & scope
CHF 274,969 approved (readiness CHF 77,227 + prepositioning CHF 110,361 + early action CHF
87,381) via the DREF Anticipatory Pillar; later revised down to CHF 244,083. The IFRC GO
appeal (MDRTJ033) records US$244,083 requested and fully funded, open 13 December 2022
through 31 December 2027, targeting 6,565 people across the four provinces above.

## Activations
- **1 December 2022** — the only activation on record. DREF appeal MDRTJ033 opened
  following the EAP's 24 November 2022 approval. Implementation was severely delayed:
  per the operations update, "no activities were implemented until 31 December 2023" due
  to audit findings and procurement-system issues at the Red Crescent Society of
  Tajikistan; the operation was subsequently managed directly by IFRC, with Year-1
  activities pushed into Year 2. The Anticipation Hub lists both 2023 and 2024 for this
  framework, likely reflecting this single delayed activation/implementation cycle.
- A January 2022 test distribution (185 households, Hisorak village, Rasht District) took
  place before this EAP's approval and is a precursor dry-run, not a formal activation.
