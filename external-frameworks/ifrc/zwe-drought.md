# IFRC — Zimbabwe drought

## Summary
The Zimbabwe Red Cross Society (ZRCS), with IFRC support, runs a drought Early Action
Protocol (EAP2023ZW01, operation MDRZW020) approved 12 December 2022 and pre-financed
from the DREF's Anticipatory Pillar. It covers five wards of Binga District, targeting
21,000 people with a two-stage trigger built on seasonal forecasts and food-security
projections. It triggered for the first time on 8 September 2023, ahead of an El
Niño-driven poor rainfall season. The EAP formalizes and narrows (to Binga only) a
2019-2022 forecast-based-financing pilot that also covered Kariba District.

## Trigger
Stage 1 fires around October when Zimbabwe's Meteorological Services Department (MSD)
seasonal forecast confirms an El Niño / poor-rainfall signal at roughly once-in-six-year
severity, releasing early-warning communication, drought-tolerant seed distribution and
livestock vaccination ahead of the rains. A planned Stage 2 — cash/voucher assistance and
school feeding — is designed to trigger if FEWS NET food-security projections, checked at
the March and June updates, confirm IPC Phase 4 conditions as the season progresses;
public sources do not confirm Stage 2 has ever fired.

## Funding & scope
CHF 549,318 total budget (CHF 182,440 readiness + CHF 366,878 early-action tranche), all
under the DREF Anticipatory Pillar (appeal MDRZW020, appeal code MDR00001) and fully
funded. Target: 21,000 people in five wards of Binga District. The nominal 5-year EAP
lifetime (to ~2027-12) is separate from the DREF appeal itself, which the IFRC GO
platform shows as closed at end of 2024. On the September 2023 activation, CHF 366,878
(≈US$411,435/€383,422) was released.

## Activations
- **8 September 2023** — Stage 1 activation, the EAP's first known trigger, roughly nine
  months after approval. MSD's El Niño forecast met the once-in-six-year severity
  threshold; CHF 366,878 released; early actions (drought-early-warning messaging,
  drought-tolerant seed distribution, livestock vaccination) rolled out from October 2023
  across five wards of Binga District, reaching up to 21,000 people. No confirmation that
  the Stage 2 cash/voucher and school-feeding actions were triggered.
- No other activations known. 2024 activity reported by IFRC (seasonal-forecast
  training for 120 stakeholders, climate-smart-agriculture farmer training) reads as
  continued readiness/implementation work rather than a second trigger event.
