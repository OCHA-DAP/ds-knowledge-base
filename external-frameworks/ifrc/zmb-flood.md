# IFRC — Zambia flood

## Summary
The Zambia Red Cross Society's (ZRCS) national riverine-flood Early Action Protocol
(EAP2020ZM01, approved 8 October 2020), pre-financed through the IFRC DREF's
Forecast-based Action (FbA) mechanism with technical and financial support from the
Netherlands Red Cross, the RCRC Climate Centre and Netherlands Red Cross "510" data team.
It targets up to 6,000 people (1,000 households) in flood-prone communities, drawn from a
countrywide risk map of 31 very-high/high-risk districts along the Zambezi, Chambeshi and
Kafue floodplains. Activated once to date, in January 2023 (Kitwe and Kafue districts);
revised in October 2023 following that activation's lessons.

## Trigger
Countrywide riverine-flood trigger monitored jointly by the Disaster Management and
Mitigation Unit (DMMU), Zambia Meteorological Department (ZMD) and Water Resources
Management Authority (WARMA). The EAP fires when the daily GLOFAS (Global Flood
Awareness System) forecast reports water discharge exceeding the threshold for a
**10-year-return-period flood** at one or more monitoring stations. The activation runs on
a **7-day lead time**, staged day-by-day (stock pre-positioning, beneficiary verification,
distribution, evacuation-route/safe-haven checks) through day 6; a **stop mechanism** at
day 3 cancels the remaining response if GloFAS shows a river-flow reduction of at least
40%. GLOFAS was adopted because neither ZMD nor WARMA run their own hydrological flood
models; rainfall forecasts alone don't indicate riverine flood risk.

## Funding & scope
CHF 249,955 total from the DREF's Forecast-based Action mechanism: CHF 65,655 readiness +
CHF 98,160 pre-positioning of stock (released on EAP approval) + CHF 86,140 released
automatically once the trigger is met. Early actions span shelter (NFI pre-positioning,
evacuation-route/safe-haven assessment, water-diversion trenches), WASH/health (jerry
cans, water-purification chlorine, soap, hygiene messaging) and food security (early
harvest and food-storage messaging, storage bags). Target: up to 1,000 households (6,000
people) in wetland/riverbank communities across 31 flagged districts nationwide (see
`extra.scope_detail`).

## Activations
- **22 January 2023** — GLOFAS impact-based forecast predicted a 20-year-return-period
  flood likely to affect 10 districts and >100,000 people. ZRCS activated (published 24
  January) in Kitwe district (Wusakile ward: 16,468 people / 2,745 households), extending
  to Kafue district from 28 January (12,232 people / 2,039 households) as heavy rains
  continued. The CHF 86,140 automatic DREF-FbA tranche was allocated (CHF 80,882.75
  reported as disbursed) for community sensitization, evacuation-route and safe-haven
  checks, house-to-house early-warning visits, NFI/WASH distribution and water-diversion
  trenching. The activation period ran to 30 April 2023.
- No other confirmed activations. The Hub also lists 2022 and 2025 as active years; no
  public source confirms an EAP trigger or activation event for either (see
  `extra.schema_strain` on the 2025 "ACCORD" credit).
