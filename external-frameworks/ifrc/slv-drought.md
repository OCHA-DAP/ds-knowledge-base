# IFRC — El Salvador drought

## Summary
The Salvadorean Red Cross Society (SRC/CRS) runs a 5-year drought Early Action Protocol
(EAP2024SV01, operation MDRSV017), approved 13 November 2024 and pre-financed from the
IFRC DREF's Anticipatory Pillar (appeal MDR00001, CHF 544,464 fully funded). It targets
the Eastern Dry Corridor — primarily Morazán and La Unión departments — for
meteorological droughts linked to El Niño, delivering cash transfers and WASH support to
up to 10,000 people. It triggered for the first time in May 2026.

## Trigger
Two sequential triggers, both issued by the General Directorate for Threat Observatory
and Natural Resources (DGOA) and disseminated with the General Directorate of Civil
Protection:
- **Trigger 1** — DGOA/IRI-CPC forecast El Niño conditions in the Niño 3.4 region for
  the next 3-4 months with ≥60% probability (checked by April of the activation year, or
  earlier in the prior year). Releases low/no-cost early actions (drought-alert
  dissemination, hygiene/heatstroke awareness campaigns).
- **Trigger 2** — DGOA's quarterly precipitation outlook forecasts accumulated May-June-July
  rainfall below normal (33rd percentile). Releases the bulk of early actions (cash
  transfers, water storage/purification, household kits).

Forecast sources: the El Niño trigger draws on NMME-CFSv2 (documented skill: POD 0.55,
FAR 0.1, 3-month lead) and the official IRI-CPC ENSO forecast; the rainfall trigger uses
DGOA's own seasonal precipitation forecast (FAR 0.30, 3-month lead, Dry Corridor). The
framework doc cites ~8 severe Dry-Corridor droughts between 1972 and 2022 (~7-year
implied return period), concentrated in ENSO years 1977-78, 1982-83, 1994-95, 1997-98,
2002 and 2014-2016. Target municipalities are selected by overlaying a municipal
drought-risk index (INFORM methodology) with the seasonal forecast, prioritizing the
Eastern departments (Morazán, La Unión, Usulután, San Miguel), with secondary risk noted
in La Paz, San Vicente, Cabañas, Santa Ana and Chalatenango.

## Funding & scope
CHF 544,464 total budget, fully funded from the DREF's Anticipatory Pillar (appeal
MDR00001): CHF 219,245 readiness + CHF 87,659 pre-positioned stock + CHF 237,560
early-action tranche. Target: 10,000 people (1,200 households for unconditional/basic-needs
cash transfers). Sectors: livelihoods (conditional cash for drought-resistant
agricultural kits, CHF 201,979), multipurpose cash (CHF 126,836), WASH (community water
tanks, purification/hygiene kits, water trucking, CHF 121,053), plus secretariat and
national-society-strengthening support. Implemented by the Salvadorean Red Cross with
Civil Protection, DGOA, the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock (MAG), FAO, WFP and the
University of El Salvador as coordination partners.

## Activations
- **18 May 2026 (first-ever activation)** — Trigger 1 (El Niño ≥60% probability in Niño
  3.4) reached ~14 April 2026; the DGOA's subsequent seasonal outlook confirmed Trigger 2
  (May-June-July rainfall below the 33rd percentile). CHF 544,464 released (~US$692,000
  per IFRC's own reporting) — the EAP's full budget, not only the CHF 237,560 early-action
  tranche, an inconsistency not resolved in public sources. Target: 10,000+ people in the
  Eastern Dry Corridor (Morazán, La Unión; also Usulután, San Miguel). Actions: cash
  transfers for livelihoods and basic needs, safe water distribution and purification/hygiene
  kits, and hygiene/heatstroke-prevention awareness campaigns.
- No prior activations known (approved Nov 2024, first fired May 2026).
