# IFRC — Bangladesh heatwave EAP

## Summary
The Bangladesh Red Crescent Society's heatwave Early Action Protocol (EAP2023BD05,
validated by IFRC, financed by the DREF anticipatory pillar), covering Dhaka city.
Grew out of a 2020-21 feasibility study and simulation; published August 2023. Triggers
early warning, water distribution, cash grants, cooling stations, and ambulance support
for ~123,700 vulnerable people.

## Trigger
Bangladesh Meteorological Department (BMD) observation/forecast of maximum temperature
above 38°C combined with a heat index above 38 persisting for two or more consecutive
days in Dhaka, cross-checked against ECMWF and GFS global forecasts. Meeting the
threshold automatically releases the pre-arranged early-action funding tranche (no
separate lead-time figure stated in public sources).

## Funding & scope
CHF 499,930 pre-arranged via the DREF anticipatory pillar (≈US$547,000): CHF 206,061
readiness + CHF 44,599 pre-positioning + CHF 249,271 released on trigger. Target:
123,700 people in vulnerable Dhaka wards (Adabor, Gendaria, Kamrangir Char, Lalbagh,
Mohammadpur, Pallabi).

## Activations
- **21 April 2024, Dhaka heatwave** — the only activation to date, and the EAP's first
  ever. BMD forecast severe-to-very-severe heat (approaching 40°C) through 30 April;
  early actions ran ~13 days during what was reported as Dhaka's longest recorded
  heatwave. A separate, non-anticipatory DREF appeal (MDRBD034) covered the wider
  national heatwave that followed in May 2024 — not part of this EAP.
