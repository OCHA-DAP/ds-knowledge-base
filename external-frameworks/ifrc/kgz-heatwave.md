# IFRC — Kyrgyzstan heatwave

## Summary
The Red Crescent Society of Kyrgyzstan's heatwave Early Action Protocol, financed through
the IFRC DREF anticipatory pillar and developed with German Red Cross support (funded by
the German Federal Foreign Office). Covers the southern provinces of Osh, Jalal-Abad and
Batken. A first, pilot version was activated once in 2022; the protocol was then
formalized as EAP2023KG02 (approved August 2023, operation MDRKG016, 5-year validity to
2028), which has since been activated once, in 2025. Triggers cash/in-kind early actions
(cooling, water/hygiene, awareness, care-facility support) for ~16,200 targeted people.

## Trigger
Kyrgyzhydromet's 7-day forecast showing temperature exceeding the 85th percentile
(local climatological threshold) for 3 or more consecutive days during June-August in
Osh, Jalal-Abad and/or Batken provinces, with an early-action lead time of 4 days. The
earlier (2022) pilot version used an 80th-percentile threshold. Public secondary sources
describe the trigger in these terms; the primary EAP PDF could not be parsed directly for
this page (see `extra.schema_strain`).

## Funding & scope
CHF 499,151 pre-arranged under the current EAP2023KG02 via the DREF anticipatory pillar:
CHF 152,671 readiness + CHF 86,406 prepositioning + CHF 260,073 released on trigger.
Target: 16,200 people in Osh, Jalal-Abad and Batken provinces, including vulnerable
families below the poverty line, older persons in care homes, and children in
orphanages/residential institutions for children with disabilities. The 2022 pilot
version carried a smaller allocation (CHF/USD ~207,757) for 2,100 households (~10,500
people).

## Activations
- **19 July 2022** — first activation, under the pilot EAP (operation MDRKG014, 80th
  percentile trigger). Triggered by a Kyrgyzhydromet forecast of temperatures exceeding
  the threshold for 3+ consecutive days from 22 July in the three southern provinces;
  reached 2,100 households (~10,500 people) with air conditioners for care facilities,
  food/hygiene packs, awareness campaigns and first-aid training.
- **15 July 2025** — first activation of the current EAP2023KG02 (operation MDRKG016).
  Kyrgyzhydromet forecast extreme heat up to 40°C in the three southern provinces from
  20-22 July 2025; CHF 242,649 released for a heat-awareness campaign (~15,000 people),
  food/hygiene distributions, and 63 air-conditioning units for elderly-care facilities
  and orphanages.
- No independently confirmed activation for 2023 or 2024 despite those years appearing
  in the Hub's inventory listing for this framework (see `extra.schema_strain`).
