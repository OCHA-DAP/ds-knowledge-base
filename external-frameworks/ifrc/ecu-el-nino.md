# IFRC — Ecuador El Niño

## Summary
The Ecuadorian Red Cross's (ERC/CRE) Early Action Protocol for extreme rainfall/flooding
linked to El Niño (EAP2020EC02, operation MDREC023), validated by IFRC and pre-financed by
the DREF Anticipatory Pillar. First developed from 2017 with German Red Cross support
alongside a companion volcanic-ash EAP (see [[ecu-vulcano-ash]]), it uses a three-level
INAMHI/ERFEN forecast trigger to release WASH, cash and prepositioning assistance to
~1,000 families (~5,000 people) across seven coastal provinces. All three trigger levels
were reached during the 2023-24 El Niño season.

## Trigger
Three progressively shorter-lead-time triggers, all on INAMHI (Ecuador's National
Institute of Meteorology and Hydrology) and ERFEN (regional El Niño monitoring) forecasts:
- **Trigger 1** (3-month lead) — ERFEN confirms ≥70% probability of an El Niño event AND
  INAMHI's seasonal forecast gives >70% probability of above-normal precipitation.
- **Trigger 2** (1-month lead) — INAMHI sub-seasonal forecast of >70% precipitation
  probability.
- **Trigger 3** (5-7 day lead) — INAMHI medium-term forecast of daily rainfall exceeding
  80mm at a high-to-very-high meteorological threat level; the shortest-lead tranche funds
  immediate kit distribution and cash transfers.

Coverage: the coastal provinces of El Oro, Esmeraldas, Guayas, Los Ríos, Manabí, Santa
Elena and Santo Domingo.

## Funding & scope
CHF 249,983 total EAP budget; the early-action (DREF Anticipatory Pillar) tranche
disbursed on the August 2023 activation was CHF 114,418 (≈USD 130,263 / EUR 120,208 per
IFRC reporting; the GO API appeal MDREC023 records USD 114,418, fully funded). Target:
1,000 families (~5,000 people), with 300 of the most vulnerable prioritized for cash
transfers. Designed with the German Red Cross, INAMHI, ERFEN and Ecuador's Secretariat of
Risk Management. The MDREC023 appeal closed 2024-06-30.

## Activations
All three trigger levels were reached during the 2023-24 El Niño season:
- **5 Aug 2023 — Trigger 1.** Full EAP activation (reported/published 23 Aug 2023);
  3-month readiness phase begins: prepositioning humanitarian kits, community risk
  messaging, and PHAST (Participatory Hygiene and Sanitation Transformation) training.
- **~Nov-Dec 2023 — Trigger 2.** Sub-seasonal forecast confirmed; Ecuadorian Red Cross
  teams conducted water-quality analysis and worked with community brigades. Exact date
  not stated in sources found.
- **9 Feb 2024 — Trigger 3.** INAMHI forecast of >80mm daily rainfall at high-to-very-high
  threat (based on 3-11 Feb 2024 data). Final tranche: humanitarian kits (cleaning
  materials, safe water, mosquito nets) distributed to ~1,000 families; cash transfers via
  ATM withdrawal to 300 of the most vulnerable families.
