# IFRC — Peru cold wave

## Summary
The Peruvian Red Cross Society's Early Action Protocol for cold wave and snowfall,
developed from 2018 with support from the German Red Cross, German Federal Foreign
Office, IFRC and the Red Cross Red Crescent Climate Centre, funded through IFRC's
Forecast-based Action Fund (by the DREF). It pre-arranges CHF 249,743 (~US$264k) over a
5-year timeframe to protect alpaca-herding households in the southern Peruvian Andes
(Apurímac, Arequipa, Cusco, Moquegua, Puno, Tacna) from the impacts of extreme cold and
heavy snowfall — livestock losses, pneumonia and other cold-related illness.

## Trigger
SENAMHI (Peru's national meteorological/hydrological service) issues a 5-day forecast. A
"Level 4" warning — 60-80% probability of a high-impact snowfall event (~20mm) persisting
more than 4 consecutive days, or of minimum temperatures below the 5th percentile
persisting at least 4 consecutive days — in a targeted district triggers early action,
giving the Peruvian Red Cross roughly 4 days to act before the event. Which of the ~10
priority communities receive support is further narrowed by a composite risk index
combining altitude, poverty, alpaca population, elderly/under-5 population and historic
pneumonia rates. The exact numeric weighting of that index and the EAP's formal document
were not accessible (see `extra.schema_strain`).

## Funding & scope
CHF 249,743 total (~US$264,304), split CHF 142,267 readiness/pre-positioning and CHF
106,978 early action, from IFRC's Forecast-based Action Fund (by the DREF), over a 5-year
programme timeframe. Targets alpaca-herding households across Apurímac, Arequipa, Cusco,
Moquegua, Puno and Tacna departments, prioritising children under 5, people 65+, pregnant
women and people with chronic/infectious disease. Programme capacity is cited as up to 625
families (~3,125 people); planned early actions include veterinary/feeding kits for
alpacas, materials for temporary livestock shelters, herder protection gear (ponchos,
gloves, boots), warm clothing, and community early-warning messaging.

## Activations
- **31 May 2018** — the only activation documented in accessible sources. SENAMHI Warning
  No. 058 (Level 4) forecast a high-impact snowfall event; the Peruvian Red Cross
  distributed veterinary kits, shelter materials, herder protection kits and warm clothing
  to 430 households across 10 communities in Arequipa and Puno, beginning about two days
  ahead of the snowfall's onset. A peer-reviewed evaluation (Frontiers in Climate, 2021)
  found the intervention reduced alpaca mortality by roughly 30% versus non-recipient
  households. No activation after 2018 was found in the sources checked (Anticipation Hub,
  ReliefWeb search, IFRC news) — see `extra.schema_strain`.
