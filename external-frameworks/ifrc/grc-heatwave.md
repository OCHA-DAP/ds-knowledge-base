# IFRC — Greece heatwave

## Summary
The Hellenic Red Cross's (HRC) Simplified Early Action Protocol for extreme heat
(sEAP2023GR01), approved 22 September 2023 and financed through the IFRC DREF
anticipatory pillar. Covers the cities of Athens, Patras and Thessaloniki. Grew out of
a 2022 partnership between HRC, IFRC, the Municipality of Athens' Chief Heat Officer,
the Arsht-Rockefeller Foundation Resilience Center/Atlantic Council, and the National
Observatory of Athens (NOA). Triggers health/first-aid outreach, water and supply
distribution, and awareness campaigns for ~2,000 directly-assisted people (plus
~10,000 reached through awareness messaging). Activated once, in June 2024.

## Trigger
NOA forecast of Heat Alert **Category 3** — the top tier of a 0-3 impact-based scale
(the Arsht-Rockefeller/Atlantic Council Extreme Temperature Categorization system,
built on 20 years of correlated meteorological and excess-mortality data for Athens) —
corresponding to a predicted excess mortality of 45% or higher, for Athens, Patras
and/or Thessaloniki, with a 5-day lead time. NOA separately issues a 10-day national
temperature-threshold warning; the 5-day, city-level Category 0-3 forecast is what
actually triggers the sEAP. Categories 0-2 keep the Hellenic Red Cross within its
normal (non-anticipatory) response capacity rather than triggering pre-arranged
funding release. See `extra.trigger_detail` and `extra.schema_strain` for a
press-vs-document labeling discrepancy ("Danger Level 4" vs. "Category 3") not fully
reconciled in sources reviewed.

## Funding & scope
CHF 217,260 total pre-arranged via the DREF anticipatory pillar: CHF 101,708
readiness + CHF 51,653 prepositioning + CHF 63,900 early-action tranche (≈USD
242,000, see `extra.funding_chf` for the conversion caveat). Target: 2,000 people
reached directly (older people, especially 65+; young children, particularly under 12
months; pregnant women and caregivers of babies; people with chronic illness or
mobility issues; homeless people and undocumented migrants) across Athens, Patras
and Thessaloniki, plus ~10,000 reached indirectly through awareness campaigns.
Stated sEAP validity: 2 years from approval (to 2025-09-22, since passed — see
`extra.schema_strain`); operational timeframe per activation: 3 months.

## Activations
- **8 June 2024** (operation MDRGR005) — the only activation to date. NOA forecast a
  "heat overload — Danger Level 4" warning for 12-13 June 2024, reaching the sEAP's
  trigger threshold. CHF 63,900 (~USD 71,300 / EUR 66,200) released from the DREF
  anticipatory pillar. Early actions — bottled water, isotonic drinks, food and sun
  cream distribution; bike-based first-aid wellness checks for homeless people and
  house-to-house visits for elderly/chronically-ill residents; a helpline; and
  multi-language health messaging via TV, social media and community outreach — ran
  across Athens, Piraeus, Thessaloniki and Patra, reaching ~2,000 people directly and
  ~10,000 through awareness messaging. A tabletop simulation exercise had been run
  about a month earlier (~10 May 2024).
