# WFP — Dominican Republic flood

## Summary
WFP anticipatory action for flooding in the Dominican Republic: cash transfers to
flood-prone households ahead of forecast river floods, part of a wider WFP-DR anticipatory
action portfolio (alongside a separate tropical-cyclone framework) that has been integrated
into the country's National Strategic Communication for Risk Management and its social
protection system. The Hub inventory lists it active in 2023 and 2024, targeting up to
~19,565 people with ~$852,212 pre-arranged in the latest recorded year.

## Trigger
Public sources describe the mechanism only in general terms: cash assistance to households
in flood-prone areas, triggered by forecasts from the Dominican Republic's national
hydromet services — INDRHI (hydrology) and ONAMET (meteorology) — which jointly operate the
WMO-supported Integrated Riverine Flood Forecasting (IRFF) system (rainfall nowcasts +
HEC-HMS river modelling, piloted on the Yaque del Norte and Haina basins). No public
document states the specific rainfall/river-level threshold or lead time used to fire the
WFP cash trigger.

## Funding & scope
Hub inventory (latest year, 2024): ~$852,212 pre-arranged, targeting 19,565 people; an
earlier 2023 Hub record shows $800,000 / 16,000 people, and the Hub's own country page
displays a cumulative $1,652,212 / 35,565 people across the two years (see `extra`). WFP's
broader DR anticipatory action programme (flood + cyclone) is funded by Germany (GFFO) and
the EU (ECHO); no flood-specific donor breakdown is published.

## Activations
None known. The Anticipation Hub's Dominican Republic page lists no activations for this
flood framework. (Not to be confused with WFP's separate tropical-cyclone framework for the
Dominican Republic, `wfp-dom-tropical-cyclone`, which activated for Hurricane Fiona in
September 2022 — that is a different framework page.)
