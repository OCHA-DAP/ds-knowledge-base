# WFP — Madagascar tropical cyclone

## Summary
WFP's anticipatory action (AA) program for tropical cyclones in Madagascar, listed by
the Anticipation Hub since 2023 (US$3.4M pre-arranged, ~164,561 people targeted). On
forecast of an intense cyclone (≥166 km/h) within 100 km of the coast, pre-arranged
funding — named sources CERF and Germany — releases anticipatory cash assistance to
the most vulnerable households, delivered with the Government of Madagascar and the
Malagasy Red Cross. First activated for Cyclone Gezani in February 2026. Distinct from,
but coordinated with, WFP's role as one of six agencies in the separate OCHA/CERF
collective Madagascar-storms framework, which shares the same trigger threshold.

## Trigger
A Météo-Madagascar bulletin forecasting an intense tropical cyclone — sustained winds
≥166 km/h — passing within 100 km of the Malagasy coast (the national contingency
plan's "Scenario 3", the same threshold used by the OCHA/CERF collective framework and
the Start Network's Madagascar mechanism). Public WFP sources describing this program
do not state an exact lead-time window; in the 2026 activation, funds were released and
a WFP team deployed to eastern Madagascar roughly a day ahead of landfall.

## Funding & scope
US$3.4 million pre-arranged, targeting an estimated 164,561 people (Anticipation Hub
inventory, fetched 2026-07-10). Named funding sources for the 2026 activation are CERF
and Germany, but the public record does not break down amounts by source, or confirm
whether they constitute the full $3.4M or a single season's tranche of a larger,
multi-year commitment. A related but separate Feb 2025 MOU with the Malagasy Red Cross
extends integrated cyclone/flood anticipation, preparedness (72-hour emergency
response) and 3-month post-cyclone recovery assistance across six regions (Sava,
Diana, Sofia, Ambatosoa, Analanjirofo, Atsinanana).

## Activations
- **9 Feb 2026 — Cyclone Gezani**: the program's first confirmed activation. WFP
  released pre-arranged CERF + Germany funding as anticipatory cash assistance to
  ~11,000 of the most vulnerable households ahead of landfall near Toamasina
  (10 Feb 2026), alongside food assistance to a further 18,000 people and a WFP
  operational team deployed to the east. Ran in parallel with (but is a separate
  funding line from) WFP's share of the OCHA/CERF collective Madagascar-storms
  framework, activated for the same storm.
- No earlier WFP-specific AA activation is confirmed in public sources; the Hub has
  listed the program as active since 2023, but the 2023 Cyclone Freddy response found
  in public reporting was a Start Network/OCHA-led activation plus WFP post-cyclone
  recovery assistance (cash/food transfers), not a documented WFP anticipatory-cash
  trigger event.
