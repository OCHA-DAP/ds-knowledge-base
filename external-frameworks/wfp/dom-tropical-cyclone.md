# WFP — Dominican Republic tropical cyclone

## Summary
WFP runs forecast-based anticipatory cash assistance for hurricanes/tropical storms in
the Dominican Republic, delivered through the government's Supérate social-protection
system and funded by Germany (GFFO) and the EU (ECHO). It has activated once on record,
for Hurricane Fiona in September 2022 — WFP's account frames this as the first time cash
moved to households ahead of a storm based on forecasts in the country. The framework is
separate from WFP's own Dominican Republic flood framework (`wfp-dom-flood`) and from a
related but distinct government mechanism: CCRIF SPC's parametric wind-speed insurance
payouts, which WFP helped link to the same Supérate delivery channel.

## Trigger
WFP monitors hurricane/tropical-storm forecasts via ONAMET, the national meteorological
service, whose station network WFP helped expand to speed up data gathering. Activation
is delivered through Supérate using an emergency-voucher operational guide that WFP
co-designed with the government — described by WFP as the country's "first national ...
anticipatory action mechanism under ... forecast-based financing," first used after
Fiona. No public document states the specific forecast indicator, numeric threshold, or
lead time used to fire the cash trigger; in the one recorded activation, cash and
early-warning SMS reached households before landfall.

## Funding & scope
Anticipation Hub inventory (2022 record): ~US$101,538 pre-arranged, targeting 11,000
people; no WFP-published breakdown of that total was found. Funded by Germany (GFFO) and
the EU (ECHO) as part of WFP's wider Dominican Republic AA programme (which also covers
the separate flood framework). Separately, WFP partnered with the government and CCRIF
SPC to channel that facility's parametric wind-speed tropical-cyclone insurance payouts
through the same Supérate system — a government insurance payout, not part of WFP's own
pre-arranged AA budget above.

## Activations
- **19 September 2022 — Hurricane Fiona**: WFP's first recorded AA cash activation in
  the country. Wire transfers of ~US$150 reached over 400 vulnerable families, and
  early-warning SMS reached 1,300 families in the storm's projected path, ahead of
  Fiona's landfall near Boca de Yuma, La Altagracia. Delivered through Supérate.

Not counted as an activation: Tropical Storm Franklin (August 2023) — WFP prepositioned
food stocks and offered drone/AI impact-assessment support for ~125,000 people in the
projected path, but no cash disbursement was reported.
