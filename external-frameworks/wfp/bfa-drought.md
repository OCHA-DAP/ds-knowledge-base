# WFP — Burkina Faso drought

## Summary
WFP is one of several implementing agencies (alongside FAO, UNICEF, UNHCR and UNFPA)
delivering Burkina Faso's drought Anticipatory Action Framework — an OCHA-led,
CERF-financed collective framework, not a framework WFP owns or publishes independently.
The Anticipation Hub's global map lists WFP's 2023 role separately ($500,000 /
41,000 people); no distinct WFP-authored framework document for Burkina Faso drought was
found. WFP's own component is food-security and livelihoods support (cash transfers,
input distribution) within the regions the collective framework covers.

## Trigger
The collective framework's original (9 March 2022) pilot trigger, reviewed in a
13 February 2023 Centre for Humanitarian Data model report, used an IRI seasonal
rainfall forecast with two seasonal checkpoints: a March trigger on the forecast risk of
below-average June-August rainfall, and a July trigger on the forecast risk of
below-average August-October rainfall, each activating when a minimum share
(reported as ~10%) of the target area was forecast below-average. Each checkpoint
releases CERF funding into different sectors (food security/protection/WASH for the
March trigger; food security/nutrition for the July trigger). This IRI-based design has
since been superseded twice over (2024 and Feb 2025 revisions) and replaced in the
current (17 April 2026) version by an ECMWF SEAS5 forecast trigger plus a JRC ASAP
observational-biomass trigger — see frameworks/bfa-drought for that current, OCHA-held
version. WFP does not appear to operate a distinct trigger of its own; it implements
against whichever collective trigger is current.

## Funding & scope
The Anticipation Hub attributes $500,000 of pre-arranged funding and 41,000 targeted
people to WFP specifically for the 2023 listing — sourced from CERF, the framework's sole
funding channel. This is smaller than both the original 2022 pilot's overall envelope
(CERF up to $15,000,000 across ~800,000 people in Boucle du Mouhoun, Centre-Nord, Sahel
and Nord) and WFP's confirmed food-security allocation in the current 2026 version
($2,850,000 of a $8,000,000 total CERF disbursement — see frameworks/bfa-drought). The
provenance of the Hub's specific $500,000/41,000 figures could not be confirmed against a
WFP-published sub-budget; treat them as an approximate, Hub-attributed slice rather than
a confirmed WFP budget line.

## Activations
None known. No activation of the Burkina Faso drought AA framework (any version) turned
up in public reporting, and the OCHA-held frameworks/bfa-drought page likewise records
activations as unverified.
