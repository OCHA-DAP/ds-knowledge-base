# WFP — Bangladesh tropical cyclone

## Summary
WFP runs its own cyclone-triggered anticipatory cash assistance in Bangladesh, funded
through its Anticipatory Action Trust Fund and bilateral AA-earmarked donor contributions
(ECHO in 2023, Germany in 2024) — separate from, but coordinated alongside, the OCHA/CERF
collective Bangladesh cyclone framework in which WFP is also a co-funding implementing
agency (see Coordination). WFP's own program has activated twice on record: Cyclone Mocha
(May 2023, Teknaf/Cox's Bazar) and Cyclone Remal (May 2024, five south-western coastal
districts), both releasing cash to at-risk households about one day ahead of landfall.

## Trigger
Bangladesh Meteorological Department cyclone danger signals (e.g. Signal 10, out of a
maximum 11, for Remal) combined with WFP's own monitoring of forecast trajectory and
likely impact trigger activation. No formal public WFP trigger document or SOP with
specific thresholds or lead times was found for this program (see `extra.schema_strain`);
in practice, cash has moved to households about one day before landfall in both recorded
activations.

## Funding & scope
No single stated pre-arranged total or forward target-people figure was found for WFP's
own program (see `extra.schema_strain`). Funding has come per-activation from AA-earmarked
donor contributions: ECHO (2023 Mocha) and Germany via WFP's Anticipatory Action Trust
Fund (2024 Remal); WFP's broader Bangladesh AA programme (all hazards) also draws on
Canada, the EU, France, Korea, Norway, Sweden, UN CERF and private donors. Cash values:
BDT 4,100 (~US$38) per person in 2023; BDT 5,000 (~US$43) per household in 2024,
delivered via bKash mobile money.

## Coordination
WFP is separately a co-funding/implementing agency inside the OCHA/CERF collective
Bangladesh cyclone framework (`frameworks/bgd-cyclone`, 2025-04-25 version): USD 2.1M
CERF funding plus USD 1.6M co-funding (USD 3.7M of that framework's USD 9.95M total),
covering the same six coastal districts under a different, forecast-model-based trigger
(≥2-of-5-model 118 km/h wind-speed consensus). That collective framework has never been
triggered by a real cyclone as of its own last-checked date — including during Cyclone
Remal, where CERF instead repurposed existing AA project funds for post-disaster relief.
The activations listed below are WFP's independently-triggered, independently-funded cash
response, not activations of the CERF framework.

## Activations
- **26 May 2024 — Cyclone Remal**: BMD Signal 10. WFP's Anticipatory Action Trust Fund
  (Germany-funded) released cash to 30,000 households (~150,000 people) across 11
  upazilas in Khulna, Satkhira, Barguna, Patuakhali and Bhola — BDT 5,000 (US$43) each via
  bKash, about one day before landfall; over 3 million people were reached with
  early-warning messages.
- **13 May 2023 — Cyclone Mocha**: ECHO-funded WFP AA project activated in Teknaf, Cox's
  Bazar — ~60,000 households reached with early-warning messages (12 May) and ~28,000
  people with anticipatory cash, BDT 4,100 (US$38) each, one day ahead of the 14 May
  landfall near the Bangladesh-Myanmar border.
