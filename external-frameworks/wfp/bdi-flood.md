# WFP — Burundi flood

## Summary
WFP runs an EU/ECHO-funded forecast-based financing (FbF) programme for extreme-rainfall
flooding in Burundi, developed since 2020 in partnership with the Burundi Red Cross (BRC)
and the national meteorological agency IGEBU. It shares a single trigger design with BRC's
own (DREF-funded) flood EAP but disburses separately, through WFP's own ECHO allocation —
see `external-frameworks/ifrc/bdi-flood.md` for the parallel Red Cross instrument. The
current Anticipation Hub listing targets ~5,880 people. The programme's one confirmed
activation (Oct-Nov 2023) delivered cash and early-warning messaging to flood-prone
communes around Lake Tanganyika, firing through WFP rather than the (then still
unvalidated) BRC mechanism.

## Trigger
A single extreme-rainfall trigger, developed jointly by WFP, BRC and IGEBU, drives both
organisations' anticipatory action: an ICPAC (IGAD Climate Prediction and Applications
Centre) seasonal/short-range forecast of exceptional rainfall, confirmed by IGEBU, ahead of
Lake Tanganyika-basin flooding. In the 2023 activation, ICPAC forecast exceptional rain on
31 October and IGEBU confirmed it on 8 November, after which anticipatory cash and
early-warning actions were implemented 10-14 November — ahead of flooding that materialised
in Rumonge commune 26-29 November. No numeric rainfall threshold was found in the public
sources reviewed (contrast IFRC's Burundi flood page, which documents a Lake Tanganyika
water-level threshold of ~771.2m alongside the same IGEBU rainfall forecasting — the two
pages likely describe the same underlying trigger from different angles).

## Funding & scope
No confirmed pre-arranged funding envelope was found (see `extra.schema_strain` for the
unreconciled figures across Hub views). The current Anticipation Hub listing targets
~5,880 people. Actual disbursements found: an August 2021 pilot distribution of EUR 135,000
to ~13,000 people in flood-risk zones of southwestern Burundi (`extra.precursor`), and the
November 2023 activation, which paid 8,900 beneficiaries (1,780 households) a total of
US$144,996 (~US$82/household) plus early-warning messages to 10,000+ residents, funded
through WFP's ECHO-earmarked FbF project rather than CERF or DREF.

## Activations
- **October-November 2023 — Rumonge province (Muhuta and Rumonge communes):** the "first
  official anticipatory action activation in Burundi." ICPAC forecast of exceptional
  rainfall (31 Oct), IGEBU confirmation (8 Nov), cash and early-warning actions implemented
  10-14 Nov, ahead of flooding reported 26-29 Nov. 1,780 households (~8,900-10,000+ people)
  received ~US$82/household (US$144,996-145,960 total, reported slightly differently across
  sources) plus early-warning messaging. Funded through WFP's ECHO project; BRC's own
  DREF/EAP had not yet been validated at the time (see `extra.coordination`).
- An earlier (August 2021) EUR 135,000 distribution to ~13,000 people is a likely precursor
  but is not clearly documented as forecast-triggered — recorded in `extra.precursor`, not
  counted as an activation here.
