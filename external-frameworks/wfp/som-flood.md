# WFP — Somalia floods

## Summary
WFP Somalia and the Somali Disaster Management Agency (SoDMA) run an anticipatory action
plan for riverine flooding on the Juba and Shabelle rivers (Deyr and Gu seasons), within
WFP's integrated climate risk management programme. Developed September 2023 covering
flood-prone Hiraan, Middle Shabelle, Gedo and Lower Shabelle; activated twice in its
first year, with learning reports published January 2025.

## Trigger
Seasonal forecasts (e.g. El Niño-driven above-average Deyr rainfall) put the plan on
alert. Activation thresholds are river risk levels of 4.5–7.5 m from SWALIM's Flood Risk
and Response Information Management System (FRRIMS). On activation: early-warning
messaging (radio, loudspeaker, mass SMS), unconditional anticipatory cash delivered up to
~8 days before floodwaters arrive, and prepositioning (nutrition commodities, boats).

## Funding & scope
Multi-year anticipatory-action investment from the Government of Germany — notably **not**
CERF. Framework envelope ~US$7.99M targeting 455,608 people (Anticipation Hub listing).
Activation costs: US$4.19M (Oct 2023, incl. US$2.7M cash), US$1.06M cash (Apr 2024).

## Activations
- **Oct 2023 (Deyr)** — warnings to ~440,000 people; cash to 218,718 people; prepositioning
  across Jubaland, South West and Hirshabelle states.
- **Apr 2024 (Gu)** — Baardheere, Luuq, Jowhar, Beletweyne; warnings ~1.9M people; cash to
  79,512 people; 14 boats prepositioned with the Logistics Cluster.
