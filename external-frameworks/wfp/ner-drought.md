# WFP — Niger drought

## Summary
"Building National Capacities for Climate Forecast-based Financing in Niger" is a
WFP-led project (Sept 2019 – Dec 2022) that built local forecast-based-financing (FbF)
capacity in Dogo commune, Mirriah department, Zinder region, working with Croix-Rouge
Nigérienne, FAO, the national food-crisis body DNPGCA, the national meteorological
service (DMN) and the regional AGRHYMET centre. It is a capacity-building/pilot
project rather than a fully specified trigger-and-response framework — no numeric
indicator, threshold or lead time is published for it. Separately, WFP is a named
implementing partner in Niger's much larger, national-scale OCHA/CERF collective
drought AA framework (`frameworks/ner-drought/`); the Anticipation Hub's "WFP" listing
for Niger drought most plausibly reflects some mix of these two threads rather than
one independent WFP national framework (see Trigger and `extra.coordination`).

## Trigger
The source page describes forecast-based-financing capacity building — using seasonal
forecasts and early-warning information to inform local anticipatory decisions in Dogo
commune — but states no specific indicator, numeric threshold or lead time, and no
funding source or amount. It records that the project had "one activation" but leaves
the population reached as "not yet defined," with no date given.

For comparison, Niger's national OCHA/CERF collective drought framework (which WFP
helps implement) keys off an IRI seasonal-forecast probability of below-average JAS
rainfall combined with an ENACTS SPI observational check; see
[`frameworks/ner-drought/`](../../frameworks/ner-drought/) for that trigger's detail.

## Funding & scope
No funding amount or source is stated on the Dogo project page. The figures carried in
this page's frontmatter (US$500,000 pre-arranged; 400,000 target people) come from the
Anticipation Hub's global-map inventory record for WFP/drought/Niger, not from the
project document itself, and could not be independently verified or attributed to a
specific funding instrument in the sources checked — see `extra.schema_strain`.

By contrast, the separate OCHA/CERF collective framework is well documented: CERF
released US$9.5M in August 2022 (with Government of Norway co-funding) for a
WFP-implemented response planned to reach roughly 200,000 people across 29 vulnerable
communes.

## Activations
None with a confirmed date for the Dogo project. The source page mentions a single
past activation but does not give its date or the number of people reached.

The OCHA/CERF collective framework (not this page's subject) activated in August 2022
— see `frameworks/ner-drought/` for that record.
