# WFP — Bangladesh floods (Jamuna basin)

## Summary
WFP runs anticipatory cash transfers for monsoon flooding in the Jamuna (Brahmaputra)
basin, one of the longest-running AA programmes in its portfolio (lineage back to the
2019-2020 pilots with CERF). The July 2024 activation reached ~90,000 households before
the flood peak and was rigorously impact-evaluated alongside the Nepal programme.

## Trigger
Probabilistic flood forecasting for the Jamuna triggers pre-agreed anticipatory cash with
days of lead time. The exact current thresholds/design aren't stated in the public
outcome-focused sources reviewed — see `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
Not publicly stated for the current programme cycle; the 2024 activation delivered ~US$43
per household to ~90,000 households.

## Activations
- **Jul 2024** — Jamuna monsoon floods: anticipatory cash to ~90,000 households ahead of
  the peak; causal impacts published in WFP's 2025 impact evaluation.
- (Lineage: the Jul 2020 Jamuna activation was the OCHA/CERF pilot — tracked in the
  OCHA portfolio, not here.)
