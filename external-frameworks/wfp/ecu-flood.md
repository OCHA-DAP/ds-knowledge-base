# WFP — Ecuador floods

## Summary
WFP runs a forecast-based anticipatory cash-transfer mechanism for coastal flood risk in
Ecuador, piloted from 2023 with Ecuador's National Secretariat of Risk Management and
Emergency and INAMHI (the national meteorological institute), and targeting an estimated
7,300 people with a $362,521 pre-arranged envelope per the Anticipation Hub inventory. The
best-documented activation (Feb 2025) reached 208 households in Quevedo and Los Espinos
(Samborondón municipality, Guayas province) with US$185 cash transfers within 48 hours of
an INAMHI red alert. Ecuador's other, separate AA instrument — the Ecuadorian Red Cross's
IFRC-funded El Niño Early Action Protocol — is tracked on its own page (see
`extra.distinct_ecuadorian_red_cross_eap`).

## Trigger
Two complementary, forecast/monitoring layers documented in WFP's own accounts:
- **INAMHI meteorological alert:** a high-level/red alert combined with sustained
  rainfall of >50mm/day over 3-5 days. On 11 Feb 2025 this combination triggered
  distribution of cash assistance within 48 hours.
- **Community river-level monitoring (Quevedo):** volunteer "water sentinels" read a
  gauge stick daily and feed a household-based meteorological station linked to the
  national met system; when water level crosses a critical threshold (exact value not
  stated in sources reviewed), the same cash-transfer mechanism is triggered, historically
  within about 72 hours.
Thresholds were jointly designed by WFP, INAMHI, ESPOL and the Samborondón municipal
government using meteorological data and historical flood patterns.

## Funding & scope
Pre-arranged funding of $362,521 targeting ~7,300 people, per the Anticipation Hub's
Ecuador country page and global-map inventory (both agree on these figures). WFP
describes the programme as financed by Germany and Ireland, without a public per-donor
breakdown. The Feb 2025 activation delivered US$185 per household to 208 households
(~800-1,000 people) — a fraction of the framework's stated target, consistent with
Quevedo/Los Espinos being one activation site within a larger multi-site plan (see
`extra.schema_strain`).

## Activations
- **Feb 2025 — Quevedo and Los Espinos:** INAMHI red alert (11 Feb) for sustained heavy
  rainfall; WFP distributed US$185 cash transfers to 208 vulnerable households (127
  Quevedo, 81 Los Espinos) within 48 hours, plus SMS/WhatsApp early-warning messages,
  ahead of flooding reported to reach ~3m in height.
- A separate WFP account describes the Quevedo community river-gauge network reaching
  "731 community members" within 72 hours of a water-level trigger, but does not date
  this instance — not listed as a distinct activation (see `extra.schema_strain`).
- No activation of this WFP mechanism was found for the 2023-24 El Niño season; the
  Feb 2024 Ecuador flood EAP activation reported in that window belongs to the separate
  Ecuadorian Red Cross EAP, not WFP (see `extra.distinct_ecuadorian_red_cross_eap`).
