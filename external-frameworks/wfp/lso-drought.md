# WFP — Lesotho drought

## Summary
WFP's Anticipatory Action Plan (AAP) for drought in Lesotho, developed with the
Government of Lesotho's Disaster Management Authority across four pilot districts:
Mafeteng, Mohale's Hoek, Quthing, and Thaba-Tseka. It pre-arranges roughly US$8.2 million
in financing against defined moderate/severe drought thresholds, targeting up to 144,000
people. It was triggered for the first time in July 2023 ahead of the El Niño-driven
2023/24 drought.

## Trigger
The plan defines "moderate" and "severe" trigger thresholds tied to seasonal drought
forecasts, developed through district-level workshops between WFP and the Disaster
Management Authority. Full activation follows once the "severe" threshold is reached —
as happened in July 2023, when a forecast severe El Niño-induced drought for the 2023/24
rain season triggered the plan for the first time. Accessible public sources describe
the threshold qualitatively ("severe") rather than naming the specific drought index or
numeric value, and do not state a lead time in days; the underlying evaluation PDF could
not be text-extracted to check for further detail. See `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
The Anticipation Hub's live framework record lists ~US$8.2 million in prearranged
financing against a target of 144,000 people, across the four pilot districts of
Mafeteng, Mohale's Hoek, Quthing, and Thaba-Tseka. No Lesotho-specific donor breakdown is
published; WFP's global AA portfolio, which underwrites plans like this one, draws on
CERF, the Adaptation Fund, the Green Climate Fund, and bilateral donors — Germany, the EU
and Norway specifically co-funded the 2023/24 regional activation. The Adaptation Fund
separately finances the related IACoV climate-resilience programme (~US$10m Phase II,
2025-2029) in the same four districts, which includes scaling out anticipatory action
but is a distinct, broader project rather than this AA plan's own budget.

## Activations
One confirmed activation: triggered July 2023 ahead of the forecast El Niño-induced
2023/24 drought — the plan's first-ever activation. WFP delivered cash transfers,
drought-tolerant seeds and shade nets to 41,200 people, plus early-warning messages to
nearly 800,000, as part of a four-country (Lesotho, Madagascar, Mozambique, Zimbabwe)
regional activation that disbursed US$12.8-14m and reached 1.2m+ people with early
warnings — the largest AA activation in Southern Africa to date. The Anticipation Hub's
country page separately lists identical figures (931,101 reached, US$3,192,466
disbursed) under both 2023 and 2024; treated here as one double-listed event rather than
a confirmed second activation — see `extra.activation_figures_note`.
