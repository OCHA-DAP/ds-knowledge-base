# WFP — Haiti multiple (floods & cyclones)

## Summary
Since 2015, WFP has invested in anticipatory action (AA) for floods and cyclones in
Haiti, pivoting in 2022 from small-scale pilots to an integrated, nationwide programme
linked to the Government's shock-responsive social protection system. In 2023, WFP
Haiti developed its first Anticipatory Action Plan (AAP) for floods and cyclones,
activated for the first time in November 2023 — the country's first-ever AA
activation. The AAP is funded from WFP's own pre-arranged envelope (~USD 5 million for
2024-2025) and delivers cash through the ASPIRE shock-responsive social protection
programme's eWallet system. It is separate from, and predates, the OCHA/CERF collective
tropical-cyclone framework endorsed in August 2024 (see Coordination note below).

## Trigger
A two-stage process: a longer-range **readiness** trigger fires when pre-defined
rainfall-forecast thresholds are reached, prompting WFP and partners to prepare to
implement pre-agreed actions; a shorter-range **activation** trigger, verified by the
Government-chaired Adaptive/Shock-Responsive Social Protection Response Committee,
confirms full implementation on a no-regrets basis. In the November 2023 case, the
readiness stage opened 13 November (ahead of Tropical Depression 22's forecast heavy
rainfall), the committee confirmed activation 15 November, and early-warning messages
plus anticipatory cash reached recipients within two days (by 17 November). No specific
numeric threshold, lead time in hours, or forecast data product is stated in the public
case study — see `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
Up to **USD 5 million** pre-arranged for the 2024-2025 cycle (WFP's own AA financing
facility). Cash is disbursed as a top-up through the World Bank-funded ASPIRE
programme's existing eWallet distribution lists, targeting ASPIRE participants in
flood/cyclone-exposed departments (18,700 people reached in the Nov 2023 activation, in
four Grand'Anse districts). WFP's stated ambition for 2024 was countrywide coverage of
almost 600,000 people at risk of cyclone or flood (see `extra.schema_strain` for the
discrepancy with the Hub's recorded `target_people`). The broader programme (early
warning systems, government capacity building) has drawn on German Federal Foreign
Office humanitarian funding.

## Activations
- **November 2023 (floods)** — Haiti's first AA activation, triggered by forecast heavy
  rainfall from Tropical Depression 22. >500,000 people across five departments received
  early-warning SMS; 18,700 ASPIRE participants received anticipatory cash. 99% of
  recipients could withdraw the cash and 97% reported spending it in preparation for the
  rains (post-distribution monitoring).
- **August 2023 (Tropical Storm Franklin)** — a **test, not a full activation**: SMS
  early warnings sent to 84,000 numbers; the storm turned north and caused no impact in
  Haiti. See `extra.near_activation`.
- **Not included here:** Hurricane Melissa's October 2025 activation was delivered
  through the separate OCHA/CERF collective cyclone framework, not this WFP-only AAP —
  see `extra.coordination` and
  [frameworks/hti-hurricanes/2024-08-23.md](../../frameworks/hti-hurricanes/2024-08-23.md).
