# WFP — Mozambique tropical cyclone

## Summary
WFP's own anticipatory-action trust-fund commitment layered onto Mozambique's OCHA/CERF
collective cyclone AA framework (co-published by OCHA and WFP): a forecast-based
readiness/action/observational trigger on tropical-cyclone wind exposure and landfall
rainfall, releasing early warning, food assistance and support to temporary
accommodation centres. Not an independent WFP framework — see `extra.coordination`.

## Trigger
Three stages, keyed on forecast (Meteo-France La Reunion basin convention) wind exposure
and observed landfall rainfall. **Readiness** (72-120h lead): same conditions as Action.
**Action**: exposed area of a >=89 km/h severe tropical storm over Angoche, Maganja da
Costa, Machanga or Govuro districts, OR exposed area of a >=119 km/h cyclone over any
framework province. **Observational**: forecast landfall at >=119 km/h in a selected
province, OR 3-day around-landfall province rainfall >120mm.

## Funding & scope
WFP commits up to **USD 979,000** from its dedicated AA trust fund for the 2024/2025
season, on top of the collective framework's CERF pre-arrangement (USD 6M in the
2025-01-09 version this doc describes; USD 4.5M in the current 2026-01-09 revision, of
which WFP receives USD 1.35M as an implementing agency for Food Security + Logistics).
No WFP-specific target-people figure is confirmed in primary sources (Hub inventory
states 77,293; unverified).

## Activations
- **Dec 2024, Cyclone Chido** — plan initiated ~72h pre-landfall; government-confirmed
  activation in Angoche and Mogincual (Nampula); WFP supported food distribution in
  temporary accommodation centres. CERF itself booked Mozambique's Chido response as
  rapid response rather than AA.
- **Feb 2026, Cyclone Gezani** — collective CERF framework activation; WFP supported
  early-warning messaging in Sofala, Inhambane, Gaza, Maputo (same event as
  `frameworks/moz-cyclones`).
