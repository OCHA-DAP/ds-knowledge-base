# FAO — Bolivia flood

## Summary
FAO coordinates and implements an anticipatory action framework for flood in Bolivia,
active per the Anticipation Hub's 2024 global-map listing. It targets 7,000 people with
a USD 200,000 pre-arranged framework, and recorded one activation in 2024 (608 people
reached, USD 5,158 deployed). Flooding is a recurrent threat in Bolivia during the
November–March rainy season, with the lowland Amazon-basin departments of Beni and
Pando — and the Beni and Mamoré river basins in particular — most exposed.

## Trigger
No public document gives a numeric indicator, threshold or lead time for this specific
Hub-listed framework. FAO has a longer-running flood risk management footprint in the
Beni and Mamoré river basins, developed originally under DIPECHO/ECHO-funded resilience
projects with Bolivia's Ministry of Rural Development and Lands and the Vice Ministry of
Civil Defense: a "Protocol for the Territorialization of Flood-Threat Alerts" (staggered
monitoring of river levels) and communal risk management plans/agricultural risk
calendars that let communities activate contingency plans before floodwaters peak. This
is the most plausible basis for the AA framework's trigger logic, but no source
explicitly confirms the link, so it is reported here as background rather than as the
framework's confirmed mechanism (see `extra.schema_strain`).

## Funding & scope
USD 200,000 pre-arranged, targeting 7,000 people (Anticipation Hub global-map inventory,
framework record for 2024; funding source/mechanism not broken out publicly).
