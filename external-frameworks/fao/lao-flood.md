# FAO — Laos flood

## Summary
FAO runs a flood anticipatory action framework in Lao PDR built on an AI/machine-learning
trigger methodology developed jointly with the Department of Meteorology and Hydrology
(DMH) and the Ministry of Labour and Social Welfare (MoLSW), under the ECHO Pilot
Programmatic Partnership (funded by DG ECHO). It sits alongside FAO's companion drought
framework for Laos ([fao-lao-drought](lao-drought.md)) within the country's National
Anticipatory Action Technical Working Group structure.

## Trigger
DMH and FAO launched companion drought and flood trigger methodologies on 2 September
2024, using AI to monitor and anticipate disasters ahead of impact. For floods, DMH
monitors and tests the methodology in Savannakhet province and is to activate in case the
trigger is reached; a national Flood Anticipatory Action Protocol (AAP) has since been
implemented, with lessons learned shared at the AA Technical Working Group's January 2025
meeting. Public documents do not state the numeric threshold, the underlying
hydrological/forecast indicator, or the lead time in days.

## Funding & scope
Anticipation Hub lists a US$315,000 pre-arranged framework targeting 3,990 people; the
funding source breakdown is not public. A 2024 activation is recorded at US$100,000
reaching 4,000 people, again without a source breakdown or specific date.

## Activations
- **2024** — Hub-recorded flood activation, US$100,000 reaching 4,000 people. No
  month-level date, triggering indicator value, or dedicated news report found in public
  sources.
- No activations known before 2024 (the AI-based trigger methodology itself only
  launched September 2024; the 2024 figure may reflect a pre-methodology or
  transitional-period activation — unreconciled in public sources).
