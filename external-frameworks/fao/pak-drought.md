# FAO — Pakistan agricultural drought

## Summary
FAO's Anticipatory Action Protocol for agricultural drought in Pakistan, developed with
the Pakistan Meteorological Department (PMD), the Government of Sindh and District
Disaster Management Authorities, and funded through the FAO–DG ECHO Pilot Programmatic
Partnership (also covering Bangladesh, Lao PDR, the Philippines and Viet Nam). It targets
the most vulnerable farmers and livestock keepers in Pakistan's drought-prone arid
regions, where a shorter-than-usual wet season and below-average rainfall threaten
rural livelihoods. FAO is also an active member of Pakistan's national Technical
Working Group on Anticipatory Action and shock-responsive social protection.

## Trigger
A **Combined Drought Index (CDI)** blends six weighted indicators: soil moisture
observation (ICIMOD, 20%), Vegetation Health Index (FAO, 20%), SPI-1 rainfall
observation (PMD, 20%), SPI-3 rainfall forecast (PMD, 15%), SPEI forecast (PMD, 15%)
and an ENSO forecast (Australian Bureau of Meteorology/WMO/IRI-Columbia University,
10%). Three phases run roughly May–December:
- **Phase 1** (~May): CDI passes 70% in at least 2 districts → early warning messaging,
  drought-resistant seeds, training on water preservation and fodder management.
- **Phase 2** (~July): CDI passes 70% in at least 2 districts (confirmed by field
  assessment) → water storage, livestock feed/supplements/vaccinations, multi-purpose
  cash transfers.
- **Phase 3** (~September): CDI stays above 70% and/or a province declares drought →
  destocking of livestock, or additional livestock feed/water supplements.
Triggers are reviewed regularly and after each activation.

## Funding & scope
Estimated **USD 250,000** (Phase 1+2) plus a complementary **USD 145,000** for Phase 3 —
**USD 395,000** total — pre-arranged in-country under the FAO–DG ECHO Pilot Programmatic
Partnership, to safeguard the lives and livelihoods of **1,500 households** (per the
2023 protocol document). Funds can be topped up via FAO's SFERA Anticipatory Action
window. The Hub's global-map inventory separately lists 88,414 people targeted for this
framework record, and two other, larger FAO Pakistan drought entries (176,828 people/
$790,000; 284,147 people/$2,366,637) — the public protocol document does not reconcile
these different scales (see `extra.schema_strain`).

## Activations
None found in public sources. The Anticipation Hub's Pakistan activations listing
(checked 2026-07-21) shows no FAO drought entries — only Start Network and
Welthungerhilfe projects. The Hub lists this FAO drought framework as active in 2022,
2023 and 2024, but that reflects inventory presence, not a confirmed trigger event.
