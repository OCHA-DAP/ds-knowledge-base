# FAO — Philippines flood

## Summary
FAO, in partnership with the Philippine Department of Social Welfare and Development
(DSWD), runs a flood anticipatory-action pilot in Midsayap and Pigcawayan, North Cotabato
(SOCCSKSARGEN region, Mindanao), as one of three hazard pilots — alongside drought
(Isabela) and tropical cyclone (Surigao del Norte/Catanduanes) — under the joint
"B-SPARED" programme ("Building on Social Protection for Anticipatory Action in Response
to Emergencies and Disasters"), funded by the EU's Civil Protection and Humanitarian Aid
Operations (ECHO). It delivers multi-purpose cash and livelihood-protection support to
poor and vulnerable households (targeted via DSWD's Listahanan 3 system) ahead of forecast
flooding, aiming to protect roughly 84,000 people per the Anticipation Hub's inventory.
This is a distinct FAO-DSWD instrument from OCHA/CERF's Philippines framework, which
covers tropical cyclones only, not floods (see [`frameworks/phl-storms`](../../frameworks/phl-storms)).

## Trigger
Public sources do not disclose a specific hydro-meteorological trigger indicator or
threshold for the flood pilot (unlike, e.g., the parallel drought pilot's documented
rainfall/temperature/soil-moisture early-warning protocol). B-SPARED's general description
gives communities an approximately 3-day window ahead of a forecast flood to evacuate, harvest
or sell crops, and move livestock and productive assets before the flood's onset, at which
point a multi-purpose cash transfer and pre-positioned food packs are released. See
`extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
Anticipation Hub inventory: US$600,000 pre-arranged, ~84,000 people targeted (the Hub lists
matching $400K/84,000-person records for FAO's separate Philippines cyclone and drought
pilots — see `extra.program_name`). Donor: EU Civil Protection and Humanitarian Aid
Operations (ECHO/EU-CPHAO). A PHP 15 million (~US$270,000) figure is reported for the
initial 2023 pilot rollout but is not confirmed as flood-specific (`extra.funding_note`).
Geographic scope: flood-prone barangays of Midsayap (Lomopog, Macasandeg, Nes, Rangaban,
Palongoguen) and Pigcawayan, North Cotabato.

## Activations
None confirmed for the flood component. B-SPARED's one documented 2024 disbursement
(~21,250 households, ~US$205,300 total) was for the typhoon pilot ahead of Super Typhoon
Man-Yi in Cagayan/Catanduanes, not the Midsayap/Pigcawayan flood pilot. See
`extra.no_activation_found`.
