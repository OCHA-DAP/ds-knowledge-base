# FAO — Malawi drought

## Summary
FAO runs an active dry-spell anticipatory action programme in southern Malawi, currently
built around the SFERA/Germany-funded project OSRO/MLW/088/GER ("Anticipatory action
interventions to mitigate the negative effects of dry spells in the southern region of
Malawi during the planting season 2023/24"). It is distinct from — but reuses the trigger
concept of — an earlier OCHA/CERF collective dry-spell pilot (2021/22, `frameworks/mwi-drought`)
that never activated. The Anticipation Hub lists FAO's Malawi drought framework as active
with a US$1,000,000 budget and 41,450 people targeted, with recorded activations in 2023
and 2024.

## Trigger
The underlying "dry spell" concept — 14 consecutive days with 0–2mm cumulative rainfall —
was defined for the 2021/22 OCHA/CERF pilot in Malawi's southern region. FAO's current
programme is described in public sources only at the level of "dry spells predicted in the
seasonal weather forecast" triggering release of cash and agricultural inputs ahead of/
during the planting season; no source reviewed states a specific forecast product,
numeric threshold, or lead time for this FAO-only programme (as opposed to the shared
dry-spell definition). See `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
Per the Anticipation Hub's Malawi country page, FAO's active drought framework carries a
US$1,000,000 budget targeting 41,450 people. The confirmed, itemized tranche within this is
US$500,000 from the Government of Germany via FAO's Special Fund for Emergency and
Resilience Activities (SFERA), for OSRO/MLW/088/GER, implemented 1 October 2023 – 31 March
2024 in Balaka, Chikwawa and Neno districts (southern region). A further 2024 activation
(US$430,000, 17,144 people per the Hub) is recorded without an itemized funding source in
public documents reviewed.

## Activations
- **Oct 2023 – Mar 2024** — OSRO/MLW/088/GER: US$500,000 (SFERA/Germany) against the
  El Niño-linked 2023/24 dry spell; cash transfers (K180,000/farmer) and agricultural
  inputs delivered in Balaka, Chikwawa and Neno. Anticipation Hub: 14,356 people reached;
  a Nov 2024 press report cites a wider 30,280-farmer programme target that does not
  reconcile with the Hub figure (`extra.schema_strain`).
- **2024** — Further FAO drought activation per the Anticipation Hub: 17,144 people
  reached, US$430,000 disbursed. No dedicated public project document was found for this
  tranche.
- **2022** — No independent FAO activation confirmed; the Hub's 2022 caption most likely
  reflects FAO's role as one of five implementing agencies in the OCHA/CERF 2021/22
  dry-spell pilot, which never activated (see `extra.coordination`).
