# FAO — Madagascar drought (Grand Sud)

## Summary
FAO's longest-running drought anticipatory action programme: piloted in 2017/18 as Early
Warning Early Action in the Grand Sud, now FAO's drought AA framework within Madagascar's
national Anticipatory Action Plan, run with the Government (BNGRC, national met service),
the SISAV food-security information system and the national AA technical working group.
The 2017/18 pilot produced one of the sector's canonical ROI results (US$1 → US$2.5).

## Trigger
A monitoring–alert–action decision ladder: EWEA/SISAV drought and food-security
indicators, seasonal rainfall forecasts and IPC projections. 2017/18: the Sep 2017 EWEA
bulletin plus Oct 2017 IPC deterioration projection triggered release in Nov 2017. Current
system: a DGM/IRI/WFP forecast-trigger model — the July 2023 forecast of moderate Oct–Dec
drought activated the AAP in August 2023, ~3–5 months ahead of the lean season.

## Funding & scope
Current framework: US$2M pre-arranged targeting 243,000 people (Anticipation Hub). The
2017/18 pilot ran on US$400k from FAO's SFERA Early Action Window for 8,400 vulnerable
households; the joint 2023/24 activation disbursed US$4.26M reaching 151,000 people.

## Activations
- **Nov 2017 – May 2018** — EWEA early action (seeds, pumps, micro-irrigation, extension
  training); ROI US$1 → US$2.5, avoided losses ~US$78/household.
- **2022/23** — Grand Sud activation (thin public detail).
- **Aug 2023 – Jun 2024** — national AAP activation for Betioky/Betroka/Tsihombe; joint
  WFP–FAO–Government; US$4.26M, 151,000 people; after-action review July 2024.
