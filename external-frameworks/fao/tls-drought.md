# FAO — Timor-Leste drought

## Summary
FAO Timor-Leste operates an Anticipatory Action Protocol for Agricultural Drought,
co-developed with the government — the Civil Protection Authority (CPA), the National
Directorate for Meteorology and Geophysics (DNMG), and the Ministry of Agriculture,
Livestock, Fisheries and Forestry (MALFF) — and triggered by Timor-Leste's Combined
Drought Index. It was first activated in October 2023 during the El Niño-driven dry
season, targeting the five highest-risk municipalities. FAO co-chairs the national
Anticipatory Action Technical Working Group (AATWG) with CPA, within a broader Green
Climate Fund-financed programme (led by UNEP, with IFRC/the Climate Centre as technical
partners) to build Timor-Leste's early warning and anticipatory-action capacity. The
Anticipation Hub currently attributes ~USD 1.7 million of pre-arranged funding and
~106,000 targeted people to FAO's framework.

## Trigger
The protocol connects Timor-Leste's Combined Drought Index (CDI) — a joint FAO/government
product monitoring agricultural drought risk at municipality level — to a defined set of
anticipatory activities. In October 2023 the CDI showed drought alerts across 12 of the
country's 14 municipalities as the dry season intensified under El Niño conditions, with
anticipated peak impact in February-March 2024. FAO and CPA targeted the five
highest-risk municipalities (Baucau, Covalima, Liquica, Viqueque, and the Oecusse-Ambeno
Special Administrative Region) for early warning dissemination, farmer training and
community-specific anticipatory action plans. Public sources describe the CDI-to-action
linkage but do not state the specific numeric CDI threshold or lead time used to trigger
the protocol; the underlying protocol document could not be fetched in full text (HTTP
403, direct and via proxy) to verify these details directly.

## Funding & scope
- The Anticipation Hub attributes ~USD 1,700,001 in pre-arranged funding and ~106,000
  targeted people to FAO's Timor-Leste drought framework (country page, fetched
  2026-07-21) — up from the ~USD 1,000,001 / 53,000 people recorded in the Hub's earlier
  inventory snapshot; the Hub does not explain the change.
- The 2023 activation itself is separately recorded by the Hub as USD 271,666 reaching
  2,650 people directly; a different source (Climate Centre, reporting on the same
  activation) cites 612,000 "indirect beneficiaries" — the combined population of the
  five at-risk municipalities that received early-warning information, not direct
  recipients of assistance.
- Funding channel: FAO's Special Fund for Emergency and Rehabilitation Activities (SFERA);
  the 2023 allocation was itself funded by the German Federal Foreign Office.
- The protocol sits within a larger USD 21.7 million Green Climate Fund programme
  ("Enhancing Early Warning Systems to build greater resilience to hydro-meteorological
  hazards in Timor-Leste"), led by UNEP with FAO, IFRC and the Climate Centre as technical
  partners, covering multiple hazards (drought and flood) and sectors (agriculture,
  health, disaster management).

## Activations
- **October 2023** — FAO Timor-Leste activated its Anticipatory Action Protocol for
  Agricultural Drought as the Combined Drought Index signalled nationwide drought risk
  ahead of the El Niño dry season. Early warnings and training were disseminated to the
  five highest-risk municipalities. This is the only activation of the FAO drought
  protocol documented in the public sources reviewed — the Anticipation Hub lists FAO
  drought framework entries for 2022, 2023 and 2024, but only the 2023 activation is
  independently described in the sources found.
