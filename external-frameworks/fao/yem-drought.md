# FAO — Yemen drought

## Summary
FAO runs a drought anticipatory action programme in Yemen — self-funded, targeting 67,000
people with US$1,000,000 pre-arranged — that delivers cash/voucher assistance, livestock
water harvesting, and home-garden agricultural inputs. The programme draws its
early-warning evidence from the same Integrated Food Security Phase Classification (IPC)
and Joint Monitoring Report (JMR) system that FAO's "Risk Team" produces for the broader,
multi-agency **Yemen Preparedness Plan for Food and Nutrition Security Crises** — a
World Bank/UN-convened mechanism (the Yemen Partnership Group) that is not itself an
FAO- or OCHA/CERF-owned AA framework.

## Trigger
No public document sets out a standalone quantitative drought threshold specific to FAO's
own framework. Instead, FAO's drought programme sits within the wider Preparedness Plan's
triggering system, which FAO's Risk Team (with UNICEF, WFP, WHO, the World Bank and ACAPS)
operates: drought is one of four "triggerable events" defined for Yemen — alongside a major
economic/food-import/price shock, a major desert-locust outbreak, and a major disease
outbreak — with the 2008–10 and 2015–18 droughts as historical precedent, and the Plan
envisages that qualifying crises correspond to a **~1-in-3-year-or-rarer event**. The
bi-monthly JMR scores drought using a Standardised Precipitation Index (SPI): typical
level >-1.30, heightened 1.30–1.60, critical >1.60. This sits alongside the Plan's general
parameters — an acute shock has occurred, a severity threshold has been or is projected to
be breached (e.g. households moving from IPC Phase 3 to Phase 4/5), and conditions are at
risk of significantly worsening. A pre-assessment by the Risk Team, confirmation by the
Programming Team (Yemen Partners Technical Team, by 2/3 vote) and a final decision by the
Senior Team (Yemen Partnership Group) is designed to run within roughly one month of a new
IPC or JMR report. Ground meteorological data collection is constrained by insecurity —
Yemen shares no data with WMO's Global Basic Observing Network — so drought/rainfall
analysis leans heavily on satellite inputs. When conditions warrant scaling up, FAO's own
protocol provides cash and voucher assistance, water harvesting for livestock, and inputs
for home gardens.

## Funding & scope
US$1,000,000 pre-arranged, funded and implemented by FAO itself, targeting 67,000 people,
in a framework the Anticipation Hub and FAO both list as active with government
involvement, in a fragile/conflict-affected setting. (The specific FAO sub-fund, e.g.
SFERA, is not confirmed in public sources.)

## Activations
None confirmed. The Anticipation Hub inventory lists the framework as present in both 2023
and 2024, but FAO's 2025 regional mapping report — which does flag Iraq's drought framework
and the Sudan's multihazard framework as activated in 2024 — does not flag this Yemen
drought framework as activated that year.
