# FAO — Zimbabwe flood

## Summary
FAO runs a community-based anticipatory action programme for flooding in Zimbabwe,
alongside its drought AA work ([`fao/zwe-drought.md`](zwe-drought.md)), under a
multi-hazard project funded by the EU (ECHO) and the German Federal Foreign Office and
delivered with WFP, IFRC and the Government of Zimbabwe's Civil Protection Unit. It
operates in high-risk districts (Beitbridge, Matobo, Chipinge, with Gwanda also named in
flood-specific reporting), building community capacity for pre-emptive mitigation
(diversion channels, barricades, drainage) and delivering cash/input assistance ahead of
anticipated heavy rainfall. The programme is separate from the OCHA/CERF-coordinated
Zimbabwe El Niño (drought) Anticipatory Action Plan.

## Trigger
Not disclosed as a specific indicator/threshold/lead-time in any public document found.
FAO messaging describes a seasonal-outlook and short-range-forecast basis: advisories of
higher-than-average rainfall prompt community-level flood mitigation (small diversion
channels, barricades, improved field drainage) and, per the Anticipation Hub's activation
record, cash and other pre-arranged assistance. See `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
US$500,000 pre-arranged, targeting ~32,000 people per cycle (2023 and 2024 Anticipation
Hub records; the Hub's cumulative country-page total of 64,000/US$1.0M is these two
cycles summed, not a bigger single figure — see `extra.hub_cumulative`). Funded by the EU
(ECHO) and German Federal Foreign Office; delivered with WFP, IFRC and Zimbabwe's Civil
Protection Unit.

## Activations
- **2023** — flood activation reaching ~10,115 people with ~US$243,897 deployed
  (Anticipation Hub country dashboard). Exact trigger date/event not stated in public
  sources reviewed; 2023 included Cyclone Freddy's regional impacts and a heavy Southern
  Africa rains season.
- No other flood activations found in public sources for 2022 or 2024.
