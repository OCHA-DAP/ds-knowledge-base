# FAO — Vietnam tropical cyclone / typhoon

## Summary
FAO Viet Nam runs an anticipatory action protocol for typhoons, delivering unconditional
cash transfers, waterproof storage drums for food and agricultural assets, and
livestock-evacuation assistance to at-risk communities in central Viet Nam ahead of
forecast landfall. It sits under the EU-funded regional Pilot Programmatic Partnership
"Increasing capacities and scale for Anticipatory Action including through Social
Protection systems", implemented with the Viet Nam Disaster and Dyke Management
Authority (VNDMA) and provincial disaster-management committees. The Anticipation Hub
lists it as an active framework covering ~34,000 people with a US$430,000 budget.

## Trigger
ECMWF forecasts are monitored from as early as ~6 days before possible landfall; FAO's
anticipatory actions are triggered once forecast sustained wind speeds are expected to
pass **90 km/h** at landfall in the targeted central provinces. Actions are then rolled
out within 48 hours of the trigger being met, timed to complete in the roughly 3 days
before landfall (as demonstrated for Typhoon Noru in September 2022). No standalone
public SOP document with the full facet/threshold detail (as exists for the FAO
Philippines framework) was found — this is the level of detail the public record
supports.

## Funding & scope
Anticipation Hub listing: US$430,000 pre-arranged, targeting ~34,000 people. Funded
through the EU (DG ECHO) Pilot Programmatic Partnership "Increasing capacities and scale
for Anticipatory Action including through Social Protection systems"
(OSRO/GLO/113/EC, Aug 2021–Aug 2024, ~US$13.47M total across Bangladesh, Laos, Pakistan,
the Philippines and Viet Nam) — the Vietnam figure is this project's country allocation,
not a separately reported budget line. Delivery partners: VNDMA and provincial Committees
for Disaster Prevention and Control.

## Activations
- **Sep 2022** — Typhoon Noru: triggered ~3 days pre-landfall on the 90 km/h forecast
  wind threshold; cash and waterproof drums distributed and livestock relocated in the
  six communes closest to predicted landfall, in Thua Thien Hue and Quang Tri provinces.
- (Nov–Dec 2021: ~650 vulnerable households received unconditional cash via the national
  social-protection system — a pre-protocol shock-responsive social-protection pilot,
  not a typhoon-trigger activation.)
- No public record found of activation for later Viet Nam typhoons (e.g. Yagi, 2024)
  under this specific FAO protocol.
