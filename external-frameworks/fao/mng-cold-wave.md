# FAO — Mongolia cold wave (dzud)

## Summary
FAO's anticipatory action programme for dzud (the compound summer-drought/severe-winter
hazard that kills livestock and devastates herder livelihoods) in Mongolia, financed
through FAO's Early Action Fund and triggered off the national Dzud Risk Map. Piloted in
2017/18, it has since run in most high-risk winters (including jointly-timed action with
the Mongolian Red Cross/IFRC EAP in 2020, and a multi-agency response alongside government
in 2022/23), releasing cash and in-kind support to herder households in flagged aimags
before livestock losses peak.

## Trigger
The Dzud Risk Map — produced by Mongolia's national meteorological/environmental
monitoring agency (referred to as NAMEM in some FAO material, IRIHME in others) — combines
roughly 14 indicators: summer pasture and precipitation conditions, livestock density and
body condition, pasture carrying capacity/biomass, snow depth/density/cover days, and
winter temperature forecasts. It flags soums (districts) at medium or high dzud risk. FAO
sources describe two releases per season: an initial map around 20 November (earlier if
snow arrives early) and a refined one in December-January once snow data is solid,
together giving roughly 4-5 months of lead time before the typical April dzud peak. FAO
uses these warnings to select the aimags/soums and herder households for anticipatory
support (destocking-for-cash, feed/nutrient supplements, livestock-disease quarantine kits,
and — in 2022/23 — unconditional cash transfers).

## Funding & scope
The Anticipation Hub's current country listing puts the active FAO framework at US$2
million pre-arranged for 72,000 people; funding is drawn through FAO's Early Action
Fund/SFERA channel rather than a single named external donor (no public source-by-source
split found). Individual activations have run at smaller scale: the 2017/18 pilot released
US$290,000 for ~1,000 households (504 herder families directly, plus ~500 urban households
via meat distribution); Hub activation rows for 2023/2024 separately show US$477,649 for
10,290 people (see `extra.schema_strain` on why these aren't reconciled with the
framework-level total or entered as distinct activations). See `extra.coordination` on how
this FAO-specific piece relates to Mongolia's broader annual inter-agency Dzud Early Action
& Response Plan.

## Activations
- **December 2017 – February 2018** — first release on record, US$290,000 from FAO's Early
  Action Fund: destocking-for-cash (Dec 2017) then feed/nutrient supplements and
  Foot-and-Mouth-Disease quarantine kits (Jan-Feb 2018) for 504 herder households in
  Zavkhan, Arkhangai, Bulgan, Tuv and Uvurkhangai aimags, plus meat distribution to ~500
  vulnerable Ulaanbaatar households.
- **2020** — FAO acted on dzud risk-map warnings in parallel with the Mongolian Red Cross
  Society/IFRC Early Action Protocol (EAP2020MN02), with triggers and funding timing
  aligned between the two organisations to avoid duplicating support; FAO's own
  disbursement amount/reach for this year not found in public sources.
- **December 2022 – May 2023** — FAO, the Ministry of Food, Agriculture and Light Industry
  and the National Emergency Management Agency delivered anticipatory action across 15
  aimags, reaching 3,000+ herder households with unconditional cash transfers ahead of one
  of the more severe recent dzuds. FAO's after-action figures: avoided livestock losses
  equivalent to ~4 cattle/household, 6x the milk yield of non-beneficiary households, and
  (per FAO's sector-wide 2023 ROI reporting) over US$7 in avoided losses/added benefit per
  US$1 spent.
