# FAO — Colombia drought (El Niño anticipatory action)

## Summary
FAO Colombia runs an El Niño/ENSO-triggered drought anticipatory action programme with the
Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development (MADR), protecting the agricultural
livelihoods of Indigenous and Afro-descendant communities — chiefly the Wayúu in La
Guajira and neighbouring Cesar. It began as a 2018 La Guajira pilot and was formalized in
2023 as a national agricultural-sector El Niño plan financed by Germany through FAO's
SFERA emergency fund, with a 2024-2027 roadmap to expand and institutionalize the approach.

## Trigger
Forecast-based rather than a single published numeric threshold: MADR and FAO act on
seasonal-forecast consensus from IDEAM (national met/hydrological service), DIMAR and
UNGRD — typically framed as ENSO-neutral-to-El-Niño transition signals from the countries'
agroclimate technical roundtables. In the 2018 pilot, the trigger was the observed and
projected Venezuelan-migration influx into La Guajira combined with a forecast of
below-average Sep–Oct 2018 rainfall threatening the planting season. The 2023/24 national
plan followed the WMO's July 2023 El Niño declaration, officially recognized in Colombia in
November 2023. No public source gives a quantitative cutoff (e.g. an ONI value) or a fixed
lead-time window; see `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
US$1,000,000 pre-arranged, financed by the Government of Germany through FAO's SFERA
(Special Fund for Emergency and Rehabilitation Activities) Anticipatory Action Window,
targeting 61,339 people (23,633 women) — reported on the ground as 12,202 ethnic and
Afro-descendant families in La Guajira and Cesar. Interventions: agricultural input
distribution (840 tonnes), animal-health campaigns/feed, and rehabilitation of water
systems (16 m³/day supply to 35 communities directly, 109 indirectly).

## Activations
- **Sep 2018 (La Guajira pilot)** — triggered on migration influx + forecast drought;
  community production centres, drought-tolerant seed/tool distribution, animal-health
  campaigns, water-point rehabilitation. Reported ROI US$1 → US$2.6.
- **Nov 2023 – 2024 (national El Niño plan)** — MADR+FAO Agricultural Sector Plan for
  Preparation, Anticipatory Action and Response to El Niño 2023-2024; US$1M (Germany/SFERA)
  reached 61,339 people/12,202 families in La Guajira and Cesar with agricultural inputs and
  water-system rehabilitation.

No FAO-attributed activation was found for the government's subsequent May 2026 El Niño
contingency planning cycle (see `extra.evolution`).
