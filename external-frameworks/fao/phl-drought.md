# FAO — Philippines drought

## Summary
FAO's Early Warning Early Action (EWEA) system for agricultural drought in the
Philippines, built from 2018 in Mindanao (Cotabato and Maguindanao provinces) and later
extended to Isabela Province (Luzon) via the DSWD-partnered B-SPARED social-protection
pilot. It triggers on a composite forecast/observation drought index and releases cash
and farming-input support to smallholder rice and corn farmers ahead of El Niño-driven
dry spells, working through national government counterparts (DSWD, Department of
Agriculture, PAGASA, PhilSA) rather than as a standalone humanitarian appeal.

## Trigger
A weighted composite index combines 10 forecast-based indicators (seasonal
rainfall/temperature outlooks, ENSO and IOD indices) with 4 observation-based indicators
(rainfall, soil moisture, vegetation condition), each scored 0-3 for deviation from
normal. The combined score is expressed as a percentage: 0-32% keeps the situation under
monitoring, 33-50% triggers a field assessment, and above 50% triggers action. The index
is assessed three times per year, aligned to the rice-growing season (April/May,
July/August, October/November). Exact per-region numeric thresholds and lead times in
days are not stated in the public sources found; the description above is as reported in
an academic review of FAO's operational protocol, not a primary FAO technical document.

## Funding & scope
US$400,000 pre-arranged (Anticipation Hub), targeting 84,000 people. Funding sources are
mixed and not fully documented in one place: the original 2018 Mindanao activation drew
on FAO's own Anticipatory Action Fund; the 2023-24 Isabela (B-SPARED) component was
EU-supported and delivered with DSWD; the original Mindanao EWEA system build was
supported by the Government of Belgium. See `extra.funding_note`.

## Activations
- **November 2018 — Mindanao.** The rainfall-forecast index reached the action
  threshold in Cotabato and Maguindanao provinces about 4.5 months earlier than a
  typical reactive response; FAO released US$400,000 from its Anticipatory Action Fund
  for rice-farming communities.
- **2024 — Isabela (Echague) and reported more broadly.** Hub-recorded activation:
  16,203 people reached, US$350,000 deployed. The best-documented component is the
  B-SPARED pilot in Echague municipality, Isabela Province, with DSWD: forecasts from
  late 2023 of El Niño-induced agricultural drought triggered one-time multipurpose cash
  (~3,450 PHP per household, about 30% of the Minimum Expenditure Basket) plus farm tools
  and PPE for roughly 954-964 rain-fed rice and corn households across 25 villages,
  identified through the Listahanan 3 poverty registry.
