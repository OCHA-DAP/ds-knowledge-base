# FAO — Laos drought

## Summary
FAO runs an anticipatory action protocol for agricultural drought in Lao PDR, built on
a Combined Drought Index (CDI) monitored jointly with the government's Department of
Meteorology and Hydrology (DMH). The protocol was developed from 2021 under a DG
ECHO-funded regional pilot partnership and is delivered with the Ministry of Labour and
Social Welfare, releasing pre-arranged agricultural and cash support to farming
households when the CDI signals elevated drought risk.

## Trigger
A monitoring-alert-action approach: the CDI combines rainfall/soil-moisture observation
with forecast data as an objective drought signal; crossing an agreed threshold triggers
release of pre-arranged support. In March 2024 the CDI met or surpassed a 60% threshold
in Oudomxay, Phongsaly, Sayabouly and Vientiane provinces — simultaneous with CDI
threshold breaches in Cambodia and Viet Nam during the 2023/24 El Niño drought. DMH's
AI/ML-based drought trigger methodology has been tested and deployed in Luang Prabang,
Savannakhet and Oudomxay provinces. Public sources do not give a numeric lead time.

## Funding & scope
The Anticipation Hub records a US$1,000,000 pre-arranged framework targeting 20,000
people; the specific funding source for that pool is not stated publicly. The protocol
itself was developed under a three-year DG ECHO Pilot Programmatic Partnership spanning
five countries (Bangladesh, Lao PDR, Pakistan, Philippines, Viet Nam), targeting Luang
Prabang and Savannakhet provinces in Laos initially. A National Anticipatory Action
Technical Working Group, established in 2023, coordinates scale-up.

Separately, since 28 May 2026, FAO and SEADRIF have piloted a CDI-triggered parametric
drought insurance product giving the Ministry of Finance direct payout access — a newer,
sovereign-level instrument distinct from this protocol's farmer-level delivery (see
`extra.related_development`).

## Activations
- **March 2024** — CDI reached the activation threshold in Lao PDR (alongside Cambodia
  and Viet Nam). US$345,326 released, reaching 26,074 people with drought-resistant
  seeds, climate-smart-agriculture training, early-warning messages and unconditional
  cash grants.
- No activations known before 2024.
