# FAO — Uganda drought

## Summary
FAO runs an active drought anticipatory action framework in Uganda, centred on the
Karamoja sub-region — nine districts (Abim, Amudat, Kaabong, Karenga, Kotido, Moroto,
Nabilatuk, Nakapiripirit, Napak) where, per the Anticipation Hub, "45 per cent of the
population is facing high levels of food insecurity." It operates through a government-
owned, multi-hazard early-warning system built jointly with WFP and Uganda's Office of the
Prime Minister under the EU (Pro-ACT)- and Danida-funded "Strengthening Shock-Responsive
Systems in Karamoja" project, feeding a Drought Anticipatory Action Plan (AAP) that has been
tested through stakeholder simulation exercises. The Anticipation Hub lists it as active
across 2023-2024 with a ~US$10.9M envelope and 1.5M people targeted; no dated activation of
this specific AAP was found in public sources.

## Trigger
Public sources describe the Drought AAP only at the process level: early-warning
information is disseminated through the Karamoja early-warning system (developed with
Uganda's National Meteorological Authority), and a stakeholder simulation exercise
"apprised different stakeholders of what the dissemination of early warning information
entails, message-flow processes, coordination measures and the requirements for activating
the Drought AAP." No public document reviewed states the specific indicator (e.g. an SPI or
forecast-probability threshold), its numeric threshold, or the lead time before a season at
which the AAP would fire — see `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
The Anticipation Hub's Uganda country page lists FAO's drought framework as active with a
budget of US$10,943,078 and 1,522,405 people targeted. The underlying delivery vehicle named
in sources is the EU (Pro-Resilience Action, Pro-ACT) and Danida-funded "Strengthening
Shock-Responsive Systems in Karamoja" project, run jointly by FAO, WFP and Uganda's Office
of the Prime Minister (Department of Relief, Disaster Preparedness and Management); no
source itemizes how the Hub's US$10.9M figure splits across these funders (see
`extra.schema_strain`).

## Activations
None known. The framework has been listed "active" on the Anticipation Hub since at least
2023, and the Drought AAP has been stakeholder-tested via simulation exercise, but no public
source reviewed reports a season in which it actually triggered (funds released, specific
actions delivered against a met threshold).
