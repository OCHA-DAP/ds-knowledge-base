# FAO — Bolivia drought

## Summary
FAO runs an anticipatory action framework for agricultural drought in the Bolivian
Altiplano, one of the country's most drought-exposed and poorest regions, where
subsistence farming (potato, quinoa, cañahua, barley, oats, alfalfa) is the main
livelihood. The framework works with national counterparts (SENAMHI, the Ministry of
Environment and Water, Civil Defence) to release pre-arranged support — water
harvesting/storage systems, animal feed and health support, and drought-tolerant
seeds/agricultural inputs — ahead of peak drought impact.

## Trigger
Public sources describe a monitoring-and-early-warning approach built on seasonal/El
Niño-linked drought forecasts, but do not spell out FAO's own numeric activation
threshold. Bolivia's national drought monitor — developed with SENAMHI, the Ministry of
Environment and Water (MMAyA) and Civil Defence (VIDECI) under a CIF-financed Pilot
Program for Climate Resilience, operational since July 2020 — tracks a combined index (3-
month SPEI, 2-month SWDI, 1-month VHI and 1-month FAPAR) calibrated against recorded
socio-economic drought impacts. Whether FAO's AA protocol activates directly off this
combined index, or off a separate ENSO-forecast trigger, is not stated in any single
public document found.

## Funding & scope
Anticipation Hub lists a US$350,000 pre-arranged framework targeting 18,500 people. The
2023 activation was funded by a US$344,412 contribution from the Government of Belgium
through FAO's SFERA Anticipatory Action window — close enough to the full framework
amount that this contribution appears to be what enabled the framework at the time.
Coverage spans the Altiplano's six most drought-affected departments (over 2,800
communities, ~486,000 families exposed, per FAO reporting), with direct support to a
subset of vulnerable family-farming households.

## Activations
- **2023** — Belgium's US$344,412 SFERA contribution funded drought anticipatory action
  during Bolivia's driest period on record (2023 El Niño): water harvesting/storage
  systems, animal feed/health support, and drought-tolerant seeds/agricultural inputs.
  Reach is reported as 7,573 people (Anticipation Hub) or 6,500 households (FAO/ReliefWeb)
  — the two figures aren't reconciled in public sources.
- The Anticipation Hub also lists a 2024 drought record for FAO Bolivia, but no
  activation-level detail (date, funding, reach) was found in public sources beyond that
  bare listing.
