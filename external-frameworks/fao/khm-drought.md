# FAO — Cambodia drought

## Summary
FAO runs a drought anticipatory action framework in Cambodia built on a Combined
Drought Index (CDI) monitored with the Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries
(MAFF) and the Ministry of Water Resources and Meteorology (MOWRAM). It is one of FAO's
Southeast Asia drought AA systems (alongside Viet Nam and Lao PDR), and part of FAO's
wider El Niño 2023/24 anticipatory action programme covering 34 countries.

## Trigger
A monitoring–alert–action approach: the CDI signals the risk of dry conditions harming
agricultural production; when it reaches the agreed activation threshold, field
verification confirms the trigger before FAO's Anticipatory Action Protocol for
Agricultural Drought releases pre-arranged funds. In March 2024 the CDI reached the
activation threshold in Cambodia, Viet Nam and Lao PDR simultaneously, and the drought
AA was confirmed after field verification. Public sources do not state the CDI's
numeric threshold or the lead time in days/weeks.

## Funding & scope
Anticipation Hub lists a US$2,000,000 pre-arranged framework targeting 92,000 people.
The March 2024 activation was funded with US$500,000 from the Government of Germany
through FAO's SFERA Anticipatory Action window (project OSRO/CMB/056/GER), reaching
3,429 farming, livestock-keeping and fishing households.

## Activations
- **March 2024** — CDI-triggered drought activation (with Viet Nam and Lao PDR),
  confirmed after field verification. Germany's US$500,000 SFERA contribution funded
  cash transfers, drought-tolerant seeds and animal health support for 3,429 households
  (OSRO/CMB/056/GER).
- No activations known before 2024.
