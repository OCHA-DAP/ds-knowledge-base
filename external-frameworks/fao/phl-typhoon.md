# FAO — Philippines typhoons (AA-MPC component)

## Summary
FAO Philippines runs the anticipatory multi-purpose cash (AA-MPC) component of the
collective OCHA/CERF anticipatory action framework for tropical cyclones (Regions 5
Bicol, 8 Eastern Visayas, 13 Caraga), with the Department of Agriculture, BFAR,
provincial governments and PAGASA. The **collective framework is an OCHA portfolio page**
([frameworks/phl-storms/](../../frameworks/phl-storms/)); this page tracks FAO's piece —
cash to agri-dependent families, agro-advisories, and securing boats/tools/crops before
landfall.

## Trigger
Follows the collective framework: readiness 3–7 days pre-landfall; activation at/before
**72 hours before forecast landfall** when the ECMWF-driven IFRC/510 impact model
predicts ≥50% probability of ≥50,000 houses totally damaged, or ≥85% of ≥8,000, in the
pilot regions. The Oct 2025 revision frames eligibility as TCs with >177 km/h 10-min
sustained winds impacting Regions 5/8/13 (~1-in-3.4-year return period).

## Funding & scope
FAO framework listing: US$400k pre-arranged, 84,000 people (Anticipation Hub); SFERA
(ECHO, Germany) plus CERF through the collective envelope. FAO targets in the collective
doc: MPC to 15,150 agri-dependent families (15,500 in the 2025 revision), agro-advisories
to 17,000 more households, safe storage for 600 fishing households.

## Activations
- **Nov 2024** — Super Typhoon Man-yi (Pepito): FAO protocols triggered (US$150k, 6,400
  people per the Hub).
- **Nov 2025** — Super Typhoon Fung-wong (Uwan): activated ~3 days out; cash and
  boat/equipment/crop-securing delivered up to 24h pre-landfall.
- (Dec 2021: full AA-MPC simulation in Catanduanes — a test, not an activation.)
