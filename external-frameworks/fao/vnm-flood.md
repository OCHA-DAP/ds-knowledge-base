# FAO — Vietnam flood

## Summary
FAO's Anticipatory Action Protocol for Typhoons and Tropical Cyclone-induced Flooding,
developed with Viet Nam's Disaster and Dyke Management Authority (VDDMA) and NGO
partners (Vietnam Red Cross, CARE, World Vision, Plan International) for six
central-coast provinces: Phu Yen, Quang Binh, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, Quang Tri and
Thua Thien-Hue. Part of a three-year (2021-2024) project on "increasing capacities and
scale for anticipatory action including through social protection systems," funded by
the EU's Directorate-General for European Civil Protection and Humanitarian Aid
Operations (DG ECHO). One activation is documented: Typhoon Noru, September 2022.

## Trigger
Forecast wind speed passing roughly 90 km/h (typhoon-strength) triggers release of
pre-arranged assistance within 48 hours, about three days ahead of landfall, in the
communes closest to predicted landfall. No public document gives a distinct numeric
rainfall/flood-level threshold — the protocol triggers on storm intensity, with
typhoon-induced flooding as the impact channel it protects against.

## Funding & scope
Anticipation Hub inventory: US$430,000 pre-arranged, 34,000 people targeted — figures
identical to the Hub's separate "cyclone" listing for Vietnam (see `schema_strain`
above), suggesting one underlying framework tracked under two hazard tags. The wider
project (readiness, systems-building and response) has a total budget of roughly
US$1.7 million funded by DG ECHO. Six central-coast provinces are covered under the
protocol; the 2022 activation itself reached six communes in Thua Thien Hue and
Quang Tri.

## Activations
- **September 2022, Typhoon Noru** — the only documented activation. Wind forecasts
  crossed the ~90 km/h threshold around 24 September; within 48 hours FAO distributed
  sealed waterproof containers (300 each in Thua Thien Hue and Quang Tri provinces) and
  cash assistance (300 packages in Quang Tri) to poor/near-poor households ahead of the
  28 September landfall. A mixed-methods impact assessment found improved food security
  and reduced negative coping mechanisms; every US$1 invested in waterproof drums
  avoided an estimated US$0.46 in losses.
- No activation is confirmed in public sources for Typhoon Yagi (September 2024),
  despite the Hub inventory listing 2024 as a year for this framework.
