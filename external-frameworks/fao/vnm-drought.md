# FAO — Vietnam drought

## Summary
FAO runs a drought anticipatory action system in Viet Nam built on a Combined Drought
Index (CDI), developed alongside twin systems in Cambodia and Lao PDR as part of FAO's
Southeast Asia anticipatory action programme. A related but distinct DG ECHO-funded
project — "Increasing Capacities and Scale for Anticipatory Action including through
Social Protection Systems" — built forecast-based financing/early-warning-early-action
mechanisms specifically for Gia Lai (Central Highlands) and Ca Mau (Mekong Delta)
provinces, implemented with Viet Nam's Department of Dyke Management and Natural
Disaster Prevention and Control.

## Trigger
A monitoring–alert–action approach: the CDI signals the risk of dry conditions harming
agricultural production; when it reaches the agreed activation threshold, field
verification confirms the trigger before FAO's Anticipatory Action Protocol for
Agricultural Drought releases pre-arranged funds. In March 2024 the CDI reached the
activation threshold in Viet Nam, Cambodia and Lao PDR simultaneously, confirmed after
field verification. Public sources do not state the CDI's numeric threshold or the lead
time in days/weeks.

## Funding & scope
The Anticipation Hub's global-map inventory lists a US$1,000,000 pre-arranged framework
targeting 34,000 people, but no public document breaks this figure down by year or
corroborates it independently (see `extra.schema_strain`). Separately, the DG ECHO-funded
regional social-protection-systems project has an estimated Viet Nam component budget of
US$1.7 million, covering (among other hazards) the Gia Lai/Ca Mau drought FbF mechanism.

## Activations
- **March 2024** — CDI-triggered drought activation (with Cambodia and Lao PDR),
  confirmed after field verification; described by FAO's Viet Nam Representative as the
  country's first early-action response to drought. Implemented as cash support to
  families affected by the drought and saltwater-intrusion crisis in the Mekong Delta
  (April 2024). Viet-Nam-specific funding amount and households-reached figures were not
  found in public sources.
- No activations known before 2024.
