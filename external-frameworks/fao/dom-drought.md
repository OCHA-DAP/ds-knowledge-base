# FAO — Dominican Republic drought

## Summary
FAO lists an active drought anticipatory action framework for the Dominican Republic on the
Anticipation Hub's global map (first appearing in the 2024 inventory), with US$600,000
pre-arranged funding targeting 13,500 people. Public sources do not surface a standalone,
DOM-specific trigger protocol; FAO's drought work in-country instead sits inside a longer
institutional build-out — desertification/drought policy support since 2018, an
ECHO-funded resilience-project consortium, a Feb 2025 inter-institutional drought technical
table, and a Jul 2026 national dialogue on the forecast 2026/27 El Niño — run jointly with
the Dominican government (Ministry of Agriculture, Ministry of the Presidency, INDOMET,
INDRHI) and partner UN agencies (WFP).

## Trigger
No numeric threshold or fixed lead time is published for a Dominican Republic-specific FAO
drought protocol. The country's drought risk is ENSO-driven — El Niño episodes bring
warmer- and drier-than-average conditions, chiefly June-August — and current planning
(the Jul 2026 government-FAO dialogue) is keyed to international model forecasts of El Niño
development in the second half of 2026, addressing planting planning, agricultural
credit/insurance, fire early-warning and anticipatory-action strengthening ahead of that
window. The Hub's linked framework document is FAO's global, 41-country La Niña
Anticipatory Action and Response Plan (Jan 2025 update) rather than a DOM-specific protocol
— see `extra.schema_strain`.

## Funding & scope
US$600,000 pre-arranged funding targeting 13,500 people, per the Anticipation Hub global map
and country page. No source breakdown (e.g. SFERA vs. bilateral) was found in public
sources. IFAD's IFAD13 programme is separately reported (Feb 2025) as resourcing related
irrigation/drought-management/digital-agriculture work in the same institutional push, but
its relationship to this specific $600,000 figure is unconfirmed.

## Activations
None known — the Anticipation Hub records zero activations for this framework, and no news
coverage of an activation was found.
