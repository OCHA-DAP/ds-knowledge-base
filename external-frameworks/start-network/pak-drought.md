# START — Pakistan drought

## Summary
Start Network runs a scientific-trigger drought anticipatory-action programme in
Pakistan — its first national pilot of what began as the "Drought Financing Facility"
and is now folded into Start Ready, the network's pooled disaster-risk-financing system.
Since March 2020 it has worked with the University of Reading's TAMSAT-ALERT team to
build a province-specific drought model covering Punjab, Sindh and (from 2021-2022)
Balochistan, releasing pre-positioned funds through local and international NGO members
ahead of a forecast poor winter (rabi) growing season. The Anticipation Hub currently
lists the framework as active, with a USD 2,366,637 pre-arranged envelope against
284,147 target people.

## Trigger
The drought model uses a different indicator per province, reflecting each province's
distinct hydrometeorology: modelled winter-wheat yield (derived from NDVI) in Punjab;
Vegetation Health Index (VHI) in Sindh, where irrigation dependence means "precipitation
cannot, on its own, be used to monitor agricultural drought"; and precipitation directly
in Balochistan. A two-level threshold then applies: a district crosses when its
indicator falls to the 20th percentile or below, and the province-wide trigger fires when
more than 25% of the province's population lives in such districts. The model produces
skilful forecasts up to roughly two months ahead of the season's end (30 April), reaching
robust accuracy for province-scale release decisions by February — enabling anticipatory
fund release before impacts peak.

## Funding & scope
The Anticipation Hub's current Pakistan listing gives a pre-arranged envelope of USD
2,366,637 against 284,147 target people, funded through Start Ready — Start Network's
pooled disaster-risk-financing mechanism (launched late 2021, first risk pool live 2022),
under which donors can earmark funds to the Pakistan drought plan specifically or
contribute unearmarked funding allocated across country plans by the Start Ready
Governance Committee. The programme covers Punjab and Sindh (from 2020-2021) and
Balochistan (added 2021-2022). Implementing partners named in the Hub inventory are
Concern Worldwide (2022) and, from 2023, acted (ACTED), Doaba Foundation, Help
Foundation, Laar Humanitarian and Development Programme, Muslim Hands, Rural Empowerment
and Institutional Development (REPID), and Sangtani Women Rural Development
Organization — see `extra.schema_strain` on a reconciliation gap between this figure and
the one this page's stub was originally generated from.

## Activations
- **2024** — the Anticipation Hub's Pakistan activation record shows a drought
  activation disbursing USD 879,221 to reach 92,291 people; the precise date within 2024
  and the full district list weren't available in accessible sources. A local
  implementing partner, HOPE, reported drought early-action work (livestock support and
  vaccination, drinking-water system rehabilitation, community engagement) in Washuk
  district, Balochistan, in February-March 2024, "supported by Start Network, READY
  Pakistan" — consistent with this activation.
- No confirmed activation record was found for 2022 or 2023. An October 2023 "Drought
  Anticipatory Contingency Planning Workshop" (hosted by Concern Worldwide and
  Welthungerhilfe) is preparatory contingency planning, not itself an activation — see
  `extra.near_miss`.
