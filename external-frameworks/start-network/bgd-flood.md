# START — Bangladesh flood

## Summary
Branded by the Anticipation Hub as a "Start Network" flood framework, this page in practice
covers **SUFAL** (Supporting/Scaling-up Flood Forecast-based Action and Learning in
Bangladesh), a CARE Bangladesh-led, ECHO-funded consortium (Phase I: Aug 2019-2021; Phase II:
Jul 2021-Jun 2025, partners Concern Worldwide, RIMES, Oxford Policy Management, and local
NGOs including Islamic Relief, SKS Foundation and ESDO) building institutional and community
capacity for forecast-based action against Brahmaputra-Jamuna monsoon flooding. No source
found ties Start Network itself to funding or formally coordinating the project — see
`extra.org_attribution`. SUFAL-II piloted the Government of Bangladesh's National Early
Action Protocol (NEAP) for monsoon riverine flood, approved June 2024.

## Trigger
The NEAP trigger matrix runs five scenarios from readiness to action, keyed to the Flood
Forecasting and Warning Centre (FFWC) and RIMES's probabilistic streamflow outlooks for the
Brahmaputra-Jamuna. **Scenario 2 (readiness):** FFWC forecasts water level within 1m of the
government-defined Danger Level, with RIMES's 1-month outlook showing >50% probability and
15-day outlook showing 50-75% probability of Danger Level exceedance. **Scenario 5
(action):** confirmed flood conditions release evacuation assistance and cash grants in the
working char areas. Public sources describe the two ends of the matrix in detail (activated
1 and 4 July 2024 respectively) but not scenarios 1, 3 and 4.

## Funding & scope
ECHO-funded throughout (Phase I also co-funded by Aktion Deutschland Hilft); Phase II's
budget is EUR 2.2 million for 1 Jul 2021-31 Dec 2023 per CARE's project factsheet, though
other Hub sources describe Phase II extending to June 2025. Reach has been reported
inconsistently across sources and Hub views (~100,000 in Phase I; ~40,000 at a Phase II
milestone point; Hub API totals ranging from ~28,000 to ~453,500 depending on aggregation) —
see `extra.funding_detail` and `extra.schema_strain`. Working areas: Ulipur and Chilmari
(Kurigram), Fulchari and Saghata (Gaibandha), Madarganj and Sarisabari (Jamalpur), extending
to Bogura by 2024.

## Activations
- **2020 monsoon** (Phase I) — FFWC-based warnings via voice message, religious-centre
  broadcast and boat/bicycle loudspeaker reached ~100,000 people (~97% of target) in
  Kurigram, Gaibandha and Jamalpur; 93% of recipients took preventive action.
- **2022** — seasonal voice-message round, 10,012 households enrolled.
- **2023** — seasonal early-warning dissemination, 14,126 people reached.
- **1 & 4 July 2024** — NEAP Scenario 2 (readiness) then Scenario 5 (action) triggered on
  FFWC/RIMES forecasts; cash grants and evacuation support released in char areas of
  Kurigram, Gaibandha, Jamalpur and Bogura. Concurrent with the OCHA/CERF collective
  framework's own 4 Jul 2024 trigger and BDRCS's 9 Jul 2024 EAP activation (see
  `extra.coordination`).
