# Ethiopia Drought — 2020-12-07

> The canonical trigger for the endorsed framework is the 2020 AA Plan PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo (`code_ref`) implements a redesigned analysis that is NOT yet endorsed — it differs substantially from the PDF trigger.

## Summary

Ethiopia's anticipatory action framework for drought was established in December 2020 as a pilot under the OCHA/CERF AA programme. The framework activates when both a food insecurity criterion (IPC projections) AND a drought criterion (seasonal rainfall forecasts or FEWSNET driver) are simultaneously met in at least one ADMIN1 region. It was first triggered in December 2020 (Phase 1) and March 2021 (Phase 2), releasing USD 20 million from CERF for multi-sector early actions targeting 900,000+ people. The repo (ds-aa-eth-drought) now contains a substantially redesigned zone-based SEAS5 trigger analysis covering three seasons (MAM, JJAS, OND) that has not yet been endorsed in a published document.

## Method

The endorsed 2020 framework uses a two-component AND logic: (1) food insecurity measured via IPC 3-month and 6-month projections at ADMIN1 level, and (2) drought conditions indicated by either two seasonal rainfall forecast models showing ≥50% probability of below-average rainfall, or drought named as a food insecurity driver in Global-IPC or FEWSNET publications. Activation requires both components to be met simultaneously in at least one ADMIN1 region.

The in-development repo trigger (not yet endorsed) uses a different approach: for each of three Ethiopian rainfall seasons (MAM, JJAS, OND), SEAS5 seasonal forecasts are ranked historically to compute zone-level return periods. The number of admin-2 zones exceeding a 5-year return period is counted; if this count exceeds a season-specific threshold, the trigger fires. ERA5 observational data provides a parallel monitoring layer (not a trigger component). LEAP WRSI and ASI vegetation indices are also tracked as supporting indicators.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** (endorsed) IPC food insecurity projections + ICPAC/NMA/NMME/IRI seasonal rainfall forecasts OR FEWSNET drought driver designation; (in-development repo) SEAS5 zone count at 5-year return period per season
- **Decision rule (plain language):** The endorsed trigger fires if at least one ADMIN1 region has ≥20% of its population projected at IPC 4+ (or ≥30% at IPC 3+ with a 5pp increase), AND at least two seasonal forecasts show ≥50% probability of below-average rainfall OR drought is named as a driver by FEWSNET/Global-IPC. Both conditions must be met simultaneously.
- **Activation structure:** Two-step (food insecurity AND drought); single-country, ADMIN1-level resolution; not more than once per year even if threshold crossed multiple times. The first activation was split into two phases (December 2020 and March 2021) as an ad hoc operational decision — the phased approach was not pre-designed.
- **Calibration:** The 2020 thresholds (20%/30% IPC population, 50% rainfall probability) were set through stakeholder consensus. The evaluation report (April 2021) noted the 50% probability threshold was considered too high — it was not met during the first activation, which triggered via the FEWSNET driver condition only. The in-development repo analysis calibrates zone-count thresholds against a 5-year return period (historical SEAS5 ranking, 1997–2025 reference period).
- **Authoritative source:** The 2020 AA Plan PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo implements an in-development redesign.
- **Operated by:** OCHA/CHD monitors the endorsed trigger; the WFP/IRI-EMI Maproom system operates a parallel woreda-level trigger for Somali, Oromia, and South Ethiopia regions independently of this framework.

## Trigger windows

**Counting rule:** The endorsed 2020 framework has a **single** activation window — a two-component AND mechanism (food insecurity AND drought) evaluated at ADMIN1 level, fired at most once per year regardless of how many seasons/regions cross the threshold. It does not segment the trigger by season; the five named rainy seasons (Kiremt, Belg, Gu, Karan, Deyr across four geographic areas) drive the *seasonal action calendars* (Annex II), not separate trigger windows. `n_windows = 1`.

| window | basis | indicator | threshold | lead time | return period | releases |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Drought-related food insecurity (single, AND-logic) | mixed | (food insecurity) IPC 3-month/6-month projection; AND (drought) ≥2 seasonal rainfall forecasts (ICPAC/NMA/NMME/IRI) OR FEWSNET/Global-IPC drought-driver designation | food insecurity: ≥20% of one ADMIN1 at IPC4+ OR ≥30% at IPC3+ with +5pp vs current; drought: ≥50% probability of below-average rainfall from ≥2 forecasts OR drought named as a driver | up to ~6 months (long-term IPC projection + seasonal forecast horizon) | n/a — consensus/bespoke thresholds, not return-period calibrated | CERF (Rapid Response Window) / Ethiopia Humanitarian Fund |

*The forecast leg (seasonal rainfall probability) makes the drought criterion partly forecast-based; the FEWSNET/Global-IPC driver leg and the IPC projections are published-assessment/observational, so the window basis is **mixed**. The MAM/JJAS/OND zone-count thresholds (SEAS5 ≥15/≥35/≥14 zones at ≥5yr RP; HIGHLIGHT_RULES in the repo notebooks) are the **in-development repo redesign**, NOT part of this endorsed window — see `extra.repo_trigger_thresholds_in_development` and `discrepancies`.*

## Monitoring

The 2020 framework specifies monitoring via IPC publication cycle (3-month and 6-month projections, published ~twice yearly) cross-referenced with seasonal rainfall forecasts from ICPAC, NMA, NMME, and IRI at forecast publication intervals. FEWSNET publications are checked for drought driver designations. No automated monitoring pipeline exists in the repo. OCHA's CHD team monitored the trigger manually with an experimental dashboard during the 2020-2021 activation. The repo's notebooks (05_4 through 05_6) provide season-level summaries run on an as-needed basis against SEAS5 and ERA5 data in the project database.

- **December 2020 (first activation):** Framework triggered — **confirmed** in the April 2021 evaluation report. The drought condition was met via the FEWSNET/Global-IPC drought-driver designation; the 50% rainfall-probability threshold was NOT met (the evaluation flags this as a design limitation). The food-insecurity (IPC) condition was met. *Unverified in the provided sources:* the CERF USD 13.2M amount, the ~13M IPC3+ figure, and the exact date — these require an external CERF-allocation record.
- **March 2021 (reported Phase 2):** A reported re-confirmation/second disbursement; the phased approach was ad hoc, not pre-designed. *Unverified in the provided sources:* the USD 6.8M amount, the USD 20M total, and the 900,000 target are not stated in the framework PDF or the evaluation report.
- **September 2024 — NOT this framework (parallel WFP/EMI system):** WFP triggered AA via the IRI/EMI Maproom for OND 2024 La Niña drought (15 woredas in Somali region; 67,753 people received multipurpose cash; USD 6.6M from Germany and Denmark — see `eth-drought-2024-activation.txt`). This ran on the WFP/EMI system **independently of** the OCHA/CERF framework documented here, so it is recorded in `discrepancies`/`extra`, not as an activation of this trigger.

## Key decisions & rationale

The 2020 trigger was designed as a two-component AND mechanism to reduce false positives: food insecurity projections alone could reflect non-drought causes (conflict, displacement), while rainfall forecasts alone have skill limitations. The combination ensures drought-related food insecurity specifically. The IPC thresholds (20%/30%) were consensus-based and reflected appetite for risk appropriate for CERF-scale interventions rather than sub-national targeting.

The 50% probability threshold for rainfall was explicitly flagged as too high in the April 2021 evaluation — the first activation used the FEWSNET driver fallback. The evaluation recommended moving toward more quantitative, data-driven thresholds, which the repo's zone-count approach attempts to address.

The repo redesign (zone-count of SEAS5 at 5-year return period) reflects recommendations from the evaluation to: (a) increase objectivity via quantitative thresholds; (b) align with seasonal calendar specifics (three distinct seasons for Ethiopia); (c) enable sub-national targeting via zone-level resolution. The WFP/IRI Maproom system uses a similar zone/woreda count approach but is calibrated separately for Somali, Oromia, and South Ethiopia regions with moderate/severe severity tiers.

## Open questions / known issues

- No published endorsed version exists for the zone-count trigger design in the repo. When will this be formally endorsed and published?
- The two parallel systems (OCHA/CHD + WFP/IRI-Maproom) target overlapping geographies with different trigger designs. Coordination and alignment between these two systems is not documented in the repo.
- The 50% probability threshold for seasonal forecasts was acknowledged as too high in 2021; the repo's redesigned approach uses return periods instead, but the endorsed threshold in the PDF remains uncorrected.
- Funding for future activations: the 2020 CERF allocation was one-time. What is the pre-committed funding envelope for a future OCHA/CHD-triggered activation?
- Target people: the 900,000 figure is from the 2020-2021 activation. The in-development framework may target different populations given zone-level calibration.
