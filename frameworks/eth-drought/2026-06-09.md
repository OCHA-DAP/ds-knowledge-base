# Ethiopia Drought — 2026-06-09 (in development)

> **In-development successor to the 2020-12-07 endorsed version. The trigger definitions are now owner-endorsed (issue #341), but there is no published/CERF-endorsed framework doc. The SEAS5 analysis (MAM & JJAS) lives in the repo at `code_ref` (SHA 6c9e731, `main` branch) and is subject to change; the OND window is delegated to the external WFP EMI/IRI Maproom.**
> The endorsed 2020 trigger (single AND-logic IPC+rainfall window) is documented on the separate [2020-12-07 page](./2020-12-07.md). This page captures the redesign (SEAS5 zone-count for MAM/JJAS + WFP Maproom for OND).

# --- section ---

## Summary

Ethiopia's in-development drought anticipatory action redesign replaces the single AND-logic IPC+rainfall trigger (endorsed December 2020) with a quantitative, season-specific zone-count approach. For its two forecast windows the design uses SEAS5 seasonal forecasts ranked historically by admin-2 zone to identify years with unusually dry forecasts; when the number of zones reaching a 5-year return period meets a season-calibrated threshold, the trigger fires. Three distinct seasonal windows cover Ethiopia's main rainfall regimes: **MAM** (Belg, Feb-issued SEAS5, ≥15 of 62 zones), **JJAS** (Kiremt, Apr OR May-issued SEAS5, ≥35 of 83 zones), and **OND** (Deyr/short rains) — which is **not** SEAS5-based but is delegated to the **WFP EMI/IRI Maproom NextGen** trigger over 19 zones. ERA5 observations, LEAP WRSI, ASI, and VHI are tracked as parallel validation layers. A verified 1997–2025 backtest puts the framework-level return period at **~1-in-3.0 yr for MAM** (10 activations / 29 yrs) and **~1-in-4.3 yr for JJAS** (7 / 29 yrs). The framework owner has endorsed a definition table (thresholds, zones monitored, ~1mo/1–2mo lead times, and a planned $5M/$2M/$3M MAM/JJAS/OND envelope split; issue #341), but the design is still exploratory-notebook only (no production pipeline) and has no published/CERF-endorsed framework document.

# --- section ---

## Method

The two SEAS5 forecast windows (**MAM** and **JJAS**) share the following method; the **OND** window is handled by the external WFP EMI/IRI Maproom NextGen trigger (19 zones) and is not built by this analysis.

1. **Zone selection:** A season-specific set of admin-2 zones (pcodes) is loaded from a blob CSV. Zone lists were compiled based on FEWSNET livelihood-zone seasonality data. Owner-confirmed counts of zones monitored: **MAM 62, JJAS 83, OND 19** (issue #341).

2. **SEAS5 ranking:** Monthly SEAS5 ensemble-mean precipitation forecasts (issued in the months preceding each season) are fetched from the production DB (`public.seas5`). Monthly values are scaled to total mm using days-in-month, summed over the season months per zone, and ranked historically (1997–2025, ~29 years). Return periods are computed as `(n_years + 1) / rank`.

3. **Zone count:** Zones with `return_period >= 5` (i.e., ranking in the driest 20% historically) are counted per forecast issue-month and year.

4. **Threshold comparison:** The season-level zone count is compared against a headline threshold (**MAM ≥15, JJAS ≥35**). Meeting the threshold constitutes a trigger event.

5. **Validation:** The SEAS5 predictive column is displayed alongside ERA5 observed zone counts, LEAP Rangeland WRSI (poor+very poor zones), Cropland WRSI (final, poor+complete failure), ASI weighted average, and humanitarian validation data (CERF allocations, people affected) — all in a styled summary table used for manual review.

**Current-year vs historical ranking (important subtlety):** historical years are ranked against the historical-only pool (n=29); the incoming forecast year is ranked against history+itself, and only *its own* zone-count is read off — the history is never re-ranked when a new forecast year is added. This is why the exported activation list is stable year-to-year (issue #341).

# --- section ---

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** for MAM & JJAS, the SEAS5 seasonal forecast — a zone-count of admin-2 pcodes reaching a 5-year return period for cumulative seasonal rainfall; for OND, the WFP EMI/IRI Maproom NextGen trigger (19 zones), which is a distinct data source and not part of the SEAS5 redesign.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** For MAM and JJAS, take the SEAS5 forecast issued in the monitoring window (Feb for MAM; **Apr OR May** for JJAS — Mar is monitoring context only). For each in-scope admin-2 zone, compute total forecast seasonal rainfall and rank it against the historical distribution (1997–2025). If the ranked position corresponds to a return period of 5 years or worse (driest ~20% of years historically), mark the zone "at risk." Count such zones; if the count meets or exceeds the season threshold (**MAM 15, JJAS 35**), the trigger fires. OND is decided off the WFP Maproom system, not this rule.
- **Activation structure:** Three windows (MAM, JJAS, OND), each over a distinct zone set. **JJAS: the operative trigger is the Apr OR May issued forecast** (issue #341 — either month reaching ≥35 zones); Mar appears in the notebooks as monitoring context only, not an operative decision month. **OND** is the WFP Maproom trigger over 19 zones (not a SEAS5 zone-count; the earlier notebook "≥14 SEAS5 zones for OND" was exploratory, not endorsed).
- **Backtested framework-level return period (verified, 1997–2025):** MAM **~1-in-3.0 yr** (10 activation-years / 29); JJAS **~1-in-4.3 yr** (7 / 29). These are the values the owner's endorsed backtest reports; the export in `exploration/export_trigger_data.py` reproduces the endorsed 1997–2026 activation list exactly.
- **Calibration:** Thresholds (MAM 15 / JJAS 35) were set by examining historical frequency relative to years with confirmed drought impacts (CERF allocations, people affected). The 5-year per-zone return period targets ~20% annual exceedance probability per zone; the aggregate zone-count threshold yields the framework-level return periods above. The thresholds are now owner-endorsed (issue #341) though still with no published framework PDF.
- **Authoritative source:** `trigger_source: repo` — this page derives from the in-development analysis at `code_ref`. There is no published framework PDF for this trigger design.
- **Operated by:** OCHA-CHD for the in-house SEAS5 analysis (MAM & JJAS). The OND window is delegated to the **WFP-operated** EMI/IRI Maproom NextGen system; OCHA-CHD does not operate it.

| MAM (Belg, Mar–May) | forecast | SEAS5 Feb-issued zone count at >=5yr RP | >=15 zones (of 62 monitored) | ~1 month (Feb forecast for Mar–May season) | ~1-in-3.0yr framework-level (backtest: 10 activation-years / 29, 1997–2025) | $5M (owner-provided planned envelope; not a confirmed CERF pre-arrangement) |

## Key decisions & rationale

**Why zone-count instead of AND-logic IPC+rainfall?** The April 2021 evaluation of the 2020 activation found that the 50% probability rainfall threshold was too high (it was never met; the first activation used the FEWSNET driver fallback). The IPC projection component also introduced subjectivity. The redesign moves to a purely quantitative approach: objective SEAS5 ranking against a historical reference, with threshold calibrated by examining overlap with years of confirmed humanitarian impact.

**Why per-zone return periods instead of regional averages?** Zone-level analysis enables sub-national targeting — a framework-level aggregate count identifies how many zones are simultaneously at risk, rather than whether a regional average is anomalous. This is consistent with the WFP/IRI-Maproom design philosophy (woreda-level thresholds) that ran in parallel with the OCHA framework.

**Why three seasons?** Ethiopia has multiple distinct rainfall regimes (Belg in the east-central, Kiremt in the highlands/west, Deyr/OND in the south and east). A single annual trigger does not capture the seasonal specificity needed to pre-position for each livelihood zone's critical rainfall period.

**Why 5-year return period per zone?** The 1-in-5-year threshold (~20% exceedance probability per zone) was chosen as the per-zone building block. The aggregate count threshold is then calibrated to give a framework-level return period consistent with CERF AA norms — the verified 1997–2025 backtest confirms **~1-in-3.0 yr for MAM** and **~1-in-4.3 yr for JJAS** (issue #341), i.e. within the intended 3–5 year band.

# --- section ---

## Changes from previous version

The superseded endorsed version (2020-12-07) used a single AND-logic window: (a) IPC food insecurity projections meeting a population threshold in at least one ADMIN1, AND (b) seasonal rainfall forecasts from two or more models showing >=50% probability of below-average rainfall, OR drought named as a driver by FEWSNET/Global-IPC.

This in-development redesign differs fundamentally in:
- **Indicator:** Drops IPC projections and FEWSNET driver designation; uses SEAS5 zone-count quantitative trigger only.
- **Administrative resolution:** Moves from ADMIN1 (region) to ADMIN2 (zone) — much finer targeting.
- **Season specificity:** Three independent seasonal windows (MAM/JJAS/OND) vs one annual combined trigger.
- **Calibration:** Return-period-based (historical SEAS5 ranking) vs consensus-based probability thresholds.
- **Basis:** Purely forecast-based (SEAS5) vs mixed (forecast + IPC published assessments).
- **Objective:** Increased objectivity, reduced dependence on IPC publication cycle, alignment with WFP/IRI Maproom design philosophy.

These changes were motivated by the April 2021 evaluation's finding that the rainfall leg of the 2020 trigger did not bind and the IPC leg could not be objectively thresholded.
