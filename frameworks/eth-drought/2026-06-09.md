# Ethiopia Drought — 2026-06-09 (in development)

> **In-development successor to the 2020-12-07 endorsed version; not yet endorsed, no published framework doc. The canonical trigger design lives in the repo at `code_ref` (SHA 6c9e731, `main` branch) and is subject to change.**
> The endorsed 2020 trigger (single AND-logic IPC+rainfall window) is documented on the separate [2020-12-07 page](./2020-12-07.md). This page captures the repo's redesigned zone-count trigger only.

# --- section ---

## Summary

Ethiopia's in-development drought anticipatory action redesign replaces the single AND-logic IPC+rainfall trigger (endorsed December 2020) with a quantitative, season-specific zone-count approach. The design uses SEAS5 seasonal forecasts ranked historically by admin-2 zone to identify years with unusually dry forecasts; when the number of zones reaching a 5-year return period exceeds a season-calibrated threshold, the trigger fires. Three distinct seasonal windows cover Ethiopia's main rainfall regimes: MAM (Belg, Feb-issued SEAS5), JJAS (Kiremt, Apr/May-issued SEAS5), and OND (Deyr/short rains, Jun–Sep-issued SEAS5). ERA5 observations, LEAP WRSI, ASI, and VHI are tracked as parallel validation layers. The redesign is exploratory-notebook only (no production pipeline), not endorsed, and has no pre-committed funding envelope.

# --- section ---

## Method

For each of the three Ethiopian rainy seasons the analysis:

1. **Zone selection:** A season-specific set of admin-2 zones (pcodes) is loaded from a blob CSV. Zone lists for MAM, JJAS, and OND were compiled based on FEWSNET livelihood-zone seasonality data.

2. **SEAS5 ranking:** Monthly SEAS5 ensemble-mean precipitation forecasts (issued in the months preceding each season) are fetched from the production DB (`public.seas5`). Monthly values are scaled to total mm using days-in-month, summed over the season months per zone, and ranked historically (1997–2025, ~29 years). Return periods are computed as `(n_years + 1) / rank`.

3. **Zone count:** Zones with `return_period >= 5` (i.e., ranking in the driest 20% historically) are counted per forecast issue-month and year.

4. **Threshold comparison:** The season-level zone count is compared against a headline threshold (MAM: 15, JJAS: 35, OND: 14). Exceeding the threshold would constitute a trigger event in the proposed design.

5. **Validation:** The SEAS5 predictive column is displayed alongside ERA5 observed zone counts, LEAP Rangeland WRSI (poor+very poor zones), Cropland WRSI (final, poor+complete failure), ASI weighted average, and humanitarian validation data (CERF allocations, people affected) — all in a styled summary table used for manual review.

# --- section ---

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** SEAS5 seasonal forecast, zone-count of admin-2 pcodes reaching a 5-year return period for cumulative seasonal rainfall, evaluated separately for MAM/JJAS/OND
- **Decision rule (plain language):** For each season, take the latest available SEAS5 forecast issued in the monitoring window (Feb for MAM; Apr or May for JJAS; Jun–Sep for OND). For each in-scope admin-2 zone, compute total forecast seasonal rainfall. Rank it against the historical distribution (1997–2025). If the ranked position corresponds to a return period of 5 years or worse (i.e., among the driest 20% of years historically), mark the zone as "at risk." Count all such zones. If the count meets or exceeds the season-specific threshold (MAM: 15, JJAS: 35, OND: 14), the trigger fires.
- **Activation structure:** Three independent windows (MAM, JJAS, OND). Each window evaluates a distinct set of zones. JJAS evaluates multiple issue-months (Mar/Apr/May in the notebooks) — it is unclear whether the Apr/May threshold of 35 applies to any single issue-month independently or requires multiple months to agree (the trigger_mask in the notebook activates on any SEAS5 column meeting the threshold). OND similarly evaluates Jun/Jul/Aug/Sep issued forecasts with a threshold of 14 for all.
- **Calibration:** Thresholds (15/35/14) were set by examining historical frequency relative to years with confirmed drought impacts (CERF allocations, people affected). The 5-year per-zone return period was chosen to target ~20% annual exceedance probability per zone, with the aggregate threshold intended to achieve a framework-level return period of approximately 5 years. Formal calibration documentation does not exist; thresholds are embedded in the notebook HIGHLIGHT_RULES.
- **Authoritative source:** `trigger_source: repo` — this page derives from the in-development analysis at `code_ref`. There is no published framework PDF for this trigger design.
- **Operated by:** OCHA/CHD (the analysis is in-house; no external system operates this trigger).

## Trigger windows

**Counting rule:** Three rows — one per seasonal rainfall window (MAM, JJAS, OND). Each has a distinct zone set, SEAS5 issue-month window, and threshold. The trigger_mask logic is applied independently per season. `n_windows = 3`.

| window | basis | indicator | threshold | lead time | return period | releases |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MAM (Belg, Mar–May) | forecast | SEAS5 Feb-issued zone count at >=5yr RP | >=15 zones out of ~50+ MAM zones | ~1–3 months (Feb forecast for Mar–May season) | ~5yr framework-level (calibrated against validation data) | null (development-stage, no pre-committed funding) |
| JJAS (Kiremt, Jun–Sep) | forecast | SEAS5 Apr/May-issued zone count at >=5yr RP | >=35 zones (applied to Apr and/or May issue-months) | ~1–3 months (Apr/May forecast for Jun–Sep season) | ~5yr framework-level | null (development-stage) |
| OND (Deyr, Oct–Dec) | forecast | SEAS5 Jun/Jul/Aug/Sep-issued zone count at >=5yr RP | >=14 zones (applied independently per issue-month) | ~1–4 months (Jun–Sep forecast for Oct–Dec season) | ~5yr framework-level | null (development-stage) |

*Supporting columns tracked alongside but NOT part of the trigger: ERA5 observed zone counts (at >=5yr RP); LEAP Rangeland WRSI (poor+very poor zones); LEAP Cropland basket final WRSI (poor+complete failure zones); ASI weighted average; VHI; humanitarian validation data (CERF, people affected). The MAM/JJAS notebooks define the trigger_mask using SEAS5 columns only; the OND notebook applies trigger_mask more broadly — see Open questions.*

## Key decisions & rationale

**Why zone-count instead of AND-logic IPC+rainfall?** The April 2021 evaluation of the 2020 activation found that the 50% probability rainfall threshold was too high (it was never met; the first activation used the FEWSNET driver fallback). The IPC projection component also introduced subjectivity. The redesign moves to a purely quantitative approach: objective SEAS5 ranking against a historical reference, with threshold calibrated by examining overlap with years of confirmed humanitarian impact.

**Why per-zone return periods instead of regional averages?** Zone-level analysis enables sub-national targeting — a framework-level aggregate count identifies how many zones are simultaneously at risk, rather than whether a regional average is anomalous. This is consistent with the WFP/IRI-Maproom design philosophy (woreda-level thresholds) that ran in parallel with the OCHA framework.

**Why three seasons?** Ethiopia has multiple distinct rainfall regimes (Belg in the east-central, Kiremt in the highlands/west, Deyr/OND in the south and east). A single annual trigger does not capture the seasonal specificity needed to pre-position for each livelihood zone's critical rainfall period.

**Why 5-year return period per zone?** The 1-in-5-year threshold (~20% exceedance probability per zone) was chosen as the per-zone building block. The aggregate count threshold is then calibrated to give a framework-level return period in the range of 3–5 years, consistent with CERF AA norms.

# --- section ---

## Changes from previous version

The superseded endorsed version (2020-12-07) used a single AND-logic window: (a) IPC food insecurity projections meeting a population threshold in at least one ADMIN1, AND (b) seasonal rainfall forecasts from two or more models showing >=50% probability of below-average rainfall, OR drought named as a driver by FEWSNET/Global-IPC.

This in-development redesign differs fundamentally in:
- **Indicator:** Drops IPC projections and FEWSNET driver designation; uses SEAS5 zone-count quantitative trigger only.
- **Administrative resolution:** Moves from ADMIN1 (region) to ADMIN2 (zone) — much finer targeting.
- **Season specificity:** Three independent seasonal windows (MAM/JJAS/OND) vs one annual combined trigger.
- **Calibration:** Return-period-based (historical SEAS5 ranking) vs consensus-based probability thresholds.
- **Basis:** Purely forecast-based (SEAS5) vs mixed (forecast + IPC published assessments).
- **Objective:** Increased objectivity, reduced dependence on IPC publication cycle, alignment with WFP/IRI Maproom design philosophy.

These changes were motivated by the April 2021 evaluation's finding that the rainfall leg of the 2020 trigger did not bind and the IPC leg could not be objectively thresholded.

## Open questions / known issues

- **Endorsement timeline:** No published framework doc exists for this zone-count design. When will it be formally endorsed? The September 2024 OCHA/CERF/EHF activation (OND 2024, ~USD 17–18M) appears to have used an updated framework — but it is not clear whether that activation used this repo's trigger design or a separate updated endorsed PDF. If a new endorsed PDF exists for the 2024 activation, this page should be updated or replaced.
- **2024 OND activation — framework identity:** Public sources (CERF/OCHA/ReliefWeb) confirm a large OCHA/EHF/CERF AA activation for OND 2024 drought in Somali, Oromia, and South Ethiopia (~USD 17–18M; 1.9M people; Sep–Oct 2024 trigger). It is not confirmed whether this fired on (a) this repo's zone-count trigger, (b) the 2020 PDF trigger, or (c) a separate updated endorsed framework PDF not yet captured in this KB. The 2024 activation may belong on the 2020-12-07 endorsed page (if fired under that framework) or may require a new endorsed-version page (if a new PDF was published). This needs resolution before either page's `activations` list is updated.
- **trigger_mask asymmetry:** The MAM and JJAS notebooks exclude ERA5/ASI/VHI columns from the trigger_mask (the logical OR for bolding the table); the OND notebook applies trigger_mask to all HIGHLIGHT_RULES columns including ERA5. It is unclear whether the OND design intends ERA5 as a trigger component or only a monitoring layer.
- **JJAS multi-month logic:** The JJAS threshold (35) appears to apply per issue-month (Mar, Apr, May separately); the notebook plots all three sub-panels with the threshold line only on Apr and May. Whether any single issue-month suffices for activation, or whether multiple months must agree, is not stated.
- **Zone list provenance:** Zone definition CSVs for each season are stored on the blob but not in the repo. The criteria for zone inclusion/exclusion (FEWSNET livelihood zones, rainfall seasonality maps, historical data coverage) are not documented.
- **Funding envelope:** No pre-committed funding exists for this development-stage trigger. Any future activation would need a new CERF/EHF pre-arrangement. The 2020 CERF allocation ($20M) was a one-time disbursement.
- **Relationship with WFP/IRI-Maproom system:** The WFP-operated EMI/IRI Maproom runs a parallel woreda-level AA trigger for Somali, Oromia, and South Ethiopia. The OND zone set in this repo overlaps substantially with that system's geography. How these two systems would be coordinated in a future joint activation is not addressed in the repo.
