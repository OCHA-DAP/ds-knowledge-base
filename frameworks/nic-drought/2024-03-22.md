## Summary

Nicaragua was the fourth country in OCHA's original Central America Dry Corridor drought Anticipatory Action framework, alongside El Salvador, Guatemala and Honduras. A Nicaragua-specific framework document — "Anticipatory Action Framework Dry Corridor Nicaragua" — was published on ReliefWeb on 22 March 2024, describing the drought situation in Nicaragua, the shared forecasting trigger, coordination arrangements, priority sectoral interventions, and targeting criteria for the most vulnerable people in five (unnamed in available sources) municipalities of Nicaragua's Dry Corridor. The overall 4-country umbrella framework was endorsed in February 2024 by the Resident Coordinators of Honduras, El Salvador and Guatemala, the UNDCO Regional Director standing in for Nicaragua, and the Emergency Relief Coordinator, with a stated validity of two years from March 2024 (through ~March 2026). No Nicaragua-specific pre-arranged CERF envelope was confirmed in public sources, and no activation involving Nicaragua was ever recorded. The 2025 renewal of the Dry Corridor framework dropped Nicaragua entirely — its title changed from covering "El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras and Nicaragua" (2024) to "El Salvador, Guatemala and Honduras" (2025) — and Nicaragua has been absent from every subsequent activation (2025, 2026).

## Method

The Nicaragua component explicitly "builds on the overall AA Framework for the Dry Corridor" and shares that framework's forecasting trigger ("the model"), pre-agreed activation protocol and action plans ("the delivery"), and pre-arranged financing ("the money") with El Salvador, Guatemala and Honduras. The sibling GTM/HND/SLV framework (see [lac-dry-corridor](../lac-dry-corridor/2025-02.md)) computes seasonal rainfall from ECMWF SEAS5 forecasts against empirical return-period thresholds for two cropping-season windows. The Nicaragua document itself describes the local rainy season as running mid-May through mid-October, with a month-long dry-spell interruption in July–August (the "canícula") that affects maize planting and growing — consistent with a Primera/Postrera two-window structure, though the Nicaragua-specific threshold values and exact monitoring months were not confirmed in the sources available this session (see Open questions).

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** ECMWF SEAS5 seasonal precipitation forecast (inferred by analogy to the shared Dry Corridor trigger; not independently confirmed for Nicaragua).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** As described for the shared framework: when seasonal rainfall forecasts for Nicaragua's Dry Corridor municipalities fall below the pre-agreed threshold ahead of the Primera or Postrera cropping season, the trigger is met and pre-arranged funds would be released for the pre-agreed anticipatory actions. The Nicaragua-specific threshold value, exact lead time and return period were not found in the sources available this session.
- **Activation structure:** Believed to mirror the sibling GTM/HND/SLV two-window (Primera/Postrera) structure; not confirmed independently for Nicaragua.
- **Calibration:** Not confirmed for Nicaragua specifically; the sibling framework uses an empirical RP-4 (25th percentile) threshold from 1981–2022 SEAS5 hindcast data.
- **Authoritative source:** The Nicaragua-specific ReliefWeb document (`framework_doc`). No PDF full text or annex was obtained this session — only the ReliefWeb listing summary and secondary web-search snippets.
- **Operated by:** Not confirmed. No dedicated OCHA CHD monitoring pipeline for Nicaragua was found; an informal, unofficial Nicaragua monitoring script exists in `ocha-dap/ds-aa-lac-dry-corridor` (`update_nica_monitoring.R`, per that repo's discrepancies) but is not established as this framework's operational trigger.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Nicaragua endorsed via UNDCO, not an RC.** Unlike Honduras, El Salvador and Guatemala — each endorsed by their national Resident Coordinator — Nicaragua's endorsement came from a "UNDCO Regional Director" standing in for the country. This is consistent with Nicaragua having expelled OCHA's country presence in 2022; no source directly states this as the cause of Nicaragua's later exclusion, but the differing endorsement/operating modality is a plausible structural factor.

**No confirmed dedicated funding for Nicaragua.** The overall Dry Corridor framework's pre-arranged CERF envelope is confirmed as $10.5M split cleanly across three countries in the 2025 renewal (GTM $4M, HND $4M, SLV $2.5M — summing exactly to the total, with no residual left for a fourth country). This suggests Nicaragua's 2024 inclusion may have been design/geographic (shared trigger, shared document family) rather than accompanied by its own earmarked pre-arranged finance — but this is inferred, not confirmed from a source stating it directly.

**Dropped in the 2025 renewal.** The 2025 update explicitly retitled the umbrella framework from covering four countries to three, removing Nicaragua from both the document title and (per the clean 3-way funding split) the funded scope.
