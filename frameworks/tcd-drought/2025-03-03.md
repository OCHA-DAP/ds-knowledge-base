# Chad Drought — 2025-03-03

## Summary

The Chad anticipatory action drought framework triggers CERF funding (up to $8M USD) for drought preparedness across seven Sahelian provinces when either SEAS5 seasonal precipitation forecasts signal a dry season ahead (Windows 1 and 2) or end-of-season biomass measurements confirm drought onset (Window 3). The framework has three independent trigger windows, each linked to a distinct intervention package (livelihood protection for main harvest, livelihood protection for market gardening and livestock, and early humanitarian impact mitigation). All three windows can be activated independently; if all fire, the maximum $8M is released. The OCHA Centre for Humanitarian Data (CHD) monitors all triggers and notifies the Humanitarian Coordinator upon threshold exceedance, triggering CERF disbursement.

## Method

**Forecast trigger (Windows 1 and 2):** ECMWF SEAS5 ensemble mean precipitation forecasts are downloaded monthly. For each pixel in the AOI, the current forecast is ranked against the historical distribution (1981–2024) to produce a pixel-wise historical percentile rank. The spatial 20th percentile (i.e., the value exceeded by 80% of AOI pixels) is computed. If this spatial 20th percentile falls below the 15th historical percentile threshold, the trigger fires. Equivalently: at least 20% of the AOI has forecast JAS rainfall in the lowest 15% of historical seasons.

**Observational trigger (Window 3):** Cumulative biomass at the third dekad of August (dekad 24) is computed from ACF/GeoSahel DMP data aggregated over the expanded 7-province AOI. A linear trend over 1999–2024 is fitted to the absolute biomass time series. The anomaly is computed as `actual / linear_fit_prediction * 100`. If this trend-adjusted anomaly is below 84.5%, the trigger fires (a ~5-year return period).

**Combined return period:** The CERF-accepted design target is a 4.5-year overall return period (at least one window fires). Individual Windows 1 and 2 each have ~13-year return periods; Window 3 has ~5 years.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:**
  - Windows 1 & 2: SEAS5 ECMWF ensemble mean seasonal forecast for JAS precipitation
  - Window 3: ACF/GeoSahel cumulative biomass (ADM1-aggregated, third dekad August), trend-adjusted anomaly

- **Decision rule (plain language):**
  - Window 1 fires if SEAS5 issued in March OR April shows at least 20% of the AOI having forecast JAS rainfall in the lowest 15% of historical years.
  - Window 2 fires if SEAS5 issued in May OR June shows the same condition.
  - Window 3 fires if cumulative biomass by end of August is below 84.5% of what the long-term linear trend predicts for that year (i.e., a drought year relative to trend).

- **Activation structure:** Three independent OR-linked windows. Each window activates individually; a single fired window releases its allocated funds. No AND condition between windows. Within each forecast window, the March or April (Window 1) / May or June (Window 2) issue month fires the window — the most favourable single month suffices.

- **Calibration:** Threshold selected from 18 candidate combinations (all achieving the CERF-required 4.5-year overall RP), choosing the option with the lowest and most balanced individual-window RPs (Option 1 in combined_rp_2025). The SEAS5 threshold of 15th percentile was chosen for approximate comparability to the superseded IRI ≥42.5% tercile-probability threshold (both proxy the same "lowest tercile" concept). The biomasse 84.5% threshold corrects for an observed positive trend in vegetation productivity; without trend correction the threshold would fail to detect 2017 (a known drought year) while falsely catching 2002.

- **Authoritative source:** 2025-03-03 framework PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo (`code_ref`) implements and derives it.

- **Operated by:** OCHA CHD (null — internal). CHD monitors, verifies with national meteorological service (DREM), and certifies trigger to HC and CERF.

## Monitoring

OCHA CHD monitors SEAS5 (monthly, March–June) and ACF biomass (once, early September). Each monitoring cycle, CHD convenes the extended ICC (including Chad national meteorological service DREM) to verify data. If threshold met, CHD alerts HC, who notifies HCT and CERF secretariat for fund disbursement. Operational monitoring notebooks: `analysis/monitoring_2025_seas5.md` (SEAS5) and `analysis/monitoring_2025_biomasse.md` (biomass). The `2025-monitoring` branch is the current operational branch; last commit 2025-05-05 (monitoring run for May 2026 issued month, year=2026 in notebook).

## Key decisions & rationale

**Switch from IRI to SEAS5:** The 2022 pilot used IRI seasonal forecasts (Columbia University) at ≥42.5% probability of below-normal JAS precipitation (tercile). The 2025 revision switched to ECMWF SEAS5 due to "operational uncertainties" in IRI production. To approximate the IRI tercile-probability concept with a continuous data source, SEAS5 ensemble means were converted to pixel-wise historical percentile ranks; the spatial 20th-percentile of this rank field approximates the fraction of the area with forecast in the lowest tercile. The 15th percentile threshold was chosen to match the IRI's implicit severity level.

**Trend correction for biomass:** A statistically significant positive trend was found in AOI aggregate biomass (1999–2024). Without trend correction, the 5-year RP threshold would have triggered on 2002 (not a reported drought year) but missed 2017 (CERF rapid response issued). Trend-adjusting corrects this: the 2025 framework uses `actual / linear_fit * 100` with a threshold of 84.5%, replacing the 2022 `≤ 80% of climatological mean` rule.

**AOI expansion:** The geographic scope grew from five provinces (Lac, Kanem, Bahr-El-Gazal, Batha, Wadi Fira) to seven (adding Ouaddaï and Sila) to improve alignment with food insecurity data and the ICC vulnerability analysis (November 2024).

**Biomasse over CHIRPS/WRSI:** Biomass was evaluated against other drought indicators (ERA5, CHIRPS, WRSI) and selected because it showed the earliest and best signal for drought severity by early September, enabling action before the full humanitarian impact manifests.

**Target return period:** The CERF committed to a 4.5-year overall return period, meaning all $8M would be spent on average once every 4.5 years (i.e., the "effective" RP, which equals the overall RP when at most one trigger fires per year). The design explores 18 combinations meeting this target and selects the one with the most balanced individual window RPs and the highest expected accuracy of the observational trigger.

## Changes from previous version

Compared to 2022-10-24 (pilot):

1. **Forecast data source:** IRI tercile probability → SEAS5 ensemble mean percentile rank (operational stability and data access).
2. **Forecast threshold:** ≥42.5% IRI probability → 20% of AOI area at/below 15th historical SEAS5 percentile.
3. **Biomass threshold:** ≤80% raw anomaly vs climatological mean → <84.5% trend-adjusted anomaly (to correct upward vegetation trend).
4. **AOI:** 5 provinces → 7 provinces (added Ouaddaï and Sila).
5. **Total budget:** $10M (2022) → $8M (2025).
6. **Agency mix:** UNHCR removed; WHO (OMS) added (health activities in Window 3).

8. **Monitoring dates:** Mid-month (17th) IRI publication → 5th of month SEAS5 publication.
9. **Framework scope:** Described as pilot in 2022; described as endorsed collective AA framework in 2025.

## Open questions / known issues

- The `ADM1_AOI_PCODES` (5-province) and `NEW_ADM1_AOI_PCODES` (7-province) constants coexist in `src/constants.py`. Scripts not yet updated to use the new constant will silently process the wrong AOI. Should be resolved by removing or renaming the old constant.
- The `blob_utils.py` module uses direct Azure SDK calls (raw SAS token) rather than `ocha-stratus`, contrary to team conventions. The `origin/ocha-stratus-migrate` branch (2025-03-12) already performs this migration but is unmerged — it should be reviewed and merged into the operational branch.
- **Dev-slot deployment:** `codab.py` reads/writes the AOI admin boundaries from the `dev` blob slot (`get_container_client` defaults to `stage='dev'`), while `seas5.py` reads forecast rasters from `prod`. Operational monitoring therefore runs against a mixed prod/dev storage configuration; the AOI geometry should be promoted to prod (or all reads pinned to one slot).
- **Companion-repo (BFA) drift:** `src/datasources/bfa_codab.py`, `exploration/bfa-jul-2024.png`, and the "match the BFA plot" comment in `monitoring_2025_seas5.md` are Burkina Faso leftovers from the repo this was forked from. Dead and country-mismatched; safe to remove.
- Dead IRI code (`exploration/iri.md`, `exploration/iri_historical.md`, `src/datasources/iri.py`) is retained from the 2022 design. Safe to delete but not yet cleaned up.
- The seas5-zscore branch (March 2025) explored a z-score variant of the SEAS5 trigger that was ultimately not adopted. It is unmerged and can be closed/archived.
- Accuracy assessment in PDF Annex 4 uses 2006–2024 window and CERF/EM-DAT as proxy "target years" (2021, 2017, 2016, 2011, 2009). This is an approximation since drought years were not formally agreed by the working group. Any future revision should formalise the target year list.
- Framework covers 2025 season (and potentially 2026). If not triggered in 2025, partners committed to further improvements before 2026. No formal 2026 revision process documented yet.
