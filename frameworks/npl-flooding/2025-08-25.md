# Nepal Flood — 2025-08-25

## Summary

The Nepal Anticipatory Action Framework for Monsoon Floods (2025 revision) triggers coordinated humanitarian pre-action for communities at risk of severe flooding in the **Koshi River Basin**, eastern Nepal. It uses a two-step trigger: a GloFAS-based readiness trigger (7-day lead time) followed by a DHM bulletin-based action trigger (up to 3-day lead). When both triggers fire — or the action trigger fires directly — CERF releases up to $2.7 million to FAO, UNFPA, UNICEF, and WFP to deliver multi-purpose cash, in-kind WASH and agricultural supplies, and protection services to up to 50,000 people across 9 municipalities in Sunsari (Koshi Province) and Saptari (Madhesh Province) districts. The 2025 version is a major revision of the 2024 framework: it narrows scope to eastern Nepal only, raises the readiness threshold from RP2 at 70% probability to RP5 at 50% probability, and replaces the compound GloFAS+DHM action trigger with a purely DHM bulletin-based action trigger.

## Method

Data flows from two sources into the two-stage trigger:

1. **GloFAS (readiness):** The ECMWF/Copernicus Global Flood Awareness System provides a 7-day ensemble forecast of river discharge at Chatara station on the Koshi river. CHD and RCO monitor this daily during the monsoon season (June 1 through end of season). The readiness trigger fires when the GloFAS 7-day forecast predicts ≥ 50% probability of reaching the 1-in-5-year return period (10,300 m³/s at Chatara).

2. **DHM bulletin (action):** The Department of Hydrology and Meteorology publishes 3-day flood-*forecast* bulletins for Chatara station. The action trigger is purely qualitative and forecast-based: all three of the following conditions must simultaneously appear in the bulletin — (a) Chatara Station is marked red (forecasted to exceed danger level of 7.0 m), (b) water level trend is forecasted to "significantly increase", and (c) the advisory mentions "extreme flooding" expected in Sunsari and Saptari districts. (The 2024 design's real-time observational gauge component was removed in 2025, so the action trigger is entirely forecast-driven.)

CERF funds disburse on readiness trigger, with the readiness portion available immediately and the action portion held until the action trigger fires. If the action trigger fires without a prior readiness trigger, CERF releases full funds on RC confirmation of activation.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** GloFAS 7-day ensemble forecast (readiness); DHM 3-day flood bulletin (action)
- **Decision rule (plain language):** When GloFAS shows ≥ 50% of ensemble members crossing the 1-in-5-year discharge at Chatara within 7 days, the readiness trigger fires and agencies begin preparatory activities. If the subsequent DHM bulletin marks Chatara red AND projects a significant water-level increase AND mentions extreme flooding in Sunsari and Saptari, the action trigger fires and agencies begin delivering assistance. Either trigger can disburse CERF funds (action trigger requires RC confirmation if no prior readiness trigger).
- **Activation structure:** Two-stage (readiness → action), with a "skip to action" pathway if the readiness trigger is missed. RC can opt out of activation within 12 hours of the action trigger if the activation group majority recommends it (activation is the default).
- **Calibration:** GloFAS readiness threshold: RP5 at Chatara = 10,300 m³/s (from Copernicus dashboard, Gumbel fit to 1979–present reanalysis annual maxima). Return period of the 50%-at-RP5 readiness trigger is not explicitly stated in the 2025 PDF. Action threshold: qualitative DHM bulletin descriptors with no explicit hydrological return-period equivalence; the PDF explicitly flags this as a limitation.
- **Authoritative source:** 2025 framework PDF (endorsed by RC 25 August 2025, by ERC 25 August 2025). See `framework_doc`.
- **Operated by:** CHD and RCO Nepal (jointly). No automated monitoring system; manual daily monitoring by staff.

## Trigger windows

**Counting rule:** Two distinct activation windows (readiness and action), each with different data sources, lead times, and release mechanisms. They target the same geographic area and population, so this is a time-differentiated two-window design.

| window | basis | indicator | threshold | lead time | return period | releases |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| readiness | forecast | GloFAS ensemble discharge at Chatara | ≥ 50% probability of exceeding 10,300 m³/s within 7 days | up to 7 days | RP5 (1-in-5 year) | CERF readiness funds; agencies begin preparatory activities |
| action | forecast (bulletin) | DHM 3-day flood-forecast bulletin: Chatara forecast red + forecast "significant increase" + advisory "extreme flooding" in Sunsari & Saptari | All 3 qualitative (forecast) conditions met simultaneously | up to 3 days | not quantified (qualitative descriptors) | CERF action funds; agencies deliver cash, in-kind, services |

## Monitoring

Monitoring is done manually by CHD and RCO daily during the monsoon season (June 1 through end of the 2025 season). The activation protocol is:

1. CHD/RCO populate a log sheet daily for GloFAS readiness trigger.
2. DHM publishes bulletins daily on the DHM website; RCO, CHD, and NDRRMA monitor these for action trigger conditions.
3. On readiness trigger: CHD emails RC, RCO, CERF, UN agencies, NDRRMA, and DHM. CERF sends approval letters. Agencies begin readiness activities.
4. On action trigger: CHD emails notification. RCO convenes activation group (RC, WFP, UNICEF, FAO, UNFPA, DHM, NDRRMA, CHD, ROAP) within 12 hours. Default is activation unless majority recommends opt-out. NDRRMA convenes executive committee to confirm.

There is no deployed app or automated monitoring pipeline in this repo. Cross-reference `infrastructure/deployments.md` — no Azure app entry for this framework.

## Historical activations

**Two real activations to date:**

1. **October 2024 — Koshi Basin (action trigger):** Flooding along the Koshi river basin triggered the action trigger. CERF released $3.4 million reportedly within 6 minutes. WFP, UNICEF, FAO, and UNFPA provided digital cash assistance, WASH supplies, food, and gender-based violence/SRH services to communities in Sunsari and Saptari districts. 249 people killed, 178 injured, 18 missing in the 27–28 September rains (per press release dated 17 October 2024). This was the first activation for the eastern (Koshi) basin.

2. **October 2022 — Karnali Basin (action trigger):** Predicted severe flooding in western Nepal triggered the framework for the Karnali (Chisapani) basin. $3.2 million released from CERF. UNFPA, UN Women, WFP and partners distributed cash, relief packages, hygiene/dignity/health kits, and psycho-social counseling. This was the first-ever activation of the Nepal AA framework.

The 2025 PDF notes that neither of these activations would have met the current 2025 action trigger conditions (the DHM bulletin-based qualitative criteria), which is acknowledged by the RCO as a known limitation accepted in exchange for better government alignment.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Why RP5 at 50% instead of RP2 at 70% (the 2024 design)?** The 2025 revision raised the readiness bar substantially. The 2024 framework's RP2 at 70% probability had calibrated effective return periods of ~1-in-2.5 years at Chatara due to GloFAS underestimation bias. The 2025 revision moves to RP5 explicitly to reduce false alarms and better reflect severe events, accepting that the framework will fire less frequently.

**Why qualitative DHM bulletins for the action trigger instead of quantified thresholds?** DHM requested this design to ensure government ownership and alignment with the national warning system. The framework explicitly acknowledges the trade-off: the qualitative descriptors have no known hydrological return-period equivalence, and neither the 2022 nor 2024 activations would have triggered under this mechanism. A future improvement listed is to clarify with DHM whether "extreme flooding" corresponds to specific hydrological thresholds.

**Why narrow to Koshi only for 2025?** The 2024 framework covered both Koshi and Karnali basins ($6.5M total). The 2025 revision focuses resources on the east where the 2024 activation generated lessons, reducing the CERF allocation to $2.7M and target population to 50,000 (from 238,831). WFP and NRCS are noted as running independent AA initiatives in western Nepal.

**Why remove GloFAS from the action trigger?** DHM advised against GloFAS for the action trigger on the grounds that while it captures flood timing adequately, it does not reliably reflect severity. This is consistent with the Quarto book finding that GloFAS was only "good to moderate" for Chatara extreme values (10–35% negative bias at all lead times for extremes).

**Alignment with SRSP:** The 2025 framework explicitly aligns cash transfer values and beneficiary targeting with Nepal's newly adopted Shock-Responsive Social Protection and Relief Distribution Standard (November 2024). The 2024 framework used a flat 15,000 NPR per household; the 2025 version uses 5,000 NPR or 8,000 NPR depending on household size.

## Open questions / known issues

- The return-period equivalence of the action trigger's qualitative descriptors ("significantly increase", "extreme flooding") remains undefined. DHM has been asked to clarify whether these correspond to specific hydrological thresholds (listed as a future improvement in the 2025 PDF).
- Neither the 2022 nor 2024 activations would have met the current bulletin-based action trigger conditions. This means the empirical return period of the 2025 action trigger cannot yet be estimated from historical activations.
- The repo (`ds-aa-npl-flooding`) does not implement a monitoring pipeline. When a pipeline is built, it should encode the 2025 trigger (50% at RP5 for readiness; 3-condition DHM bulletin for action) and should NOT use the RP2/70% parameters still present in the analysis notebooks.
- GEOGloWS evaluation (Quarto book) recommends against adopting GEOGloWS as a trigger source. The GRRR tangential finding (6/7 DHM event matches at Chisapani) is worth a dedicated evaluation if the Karnali basin is reintroduced.
- The Chisapani station (Karnali) in constants.py is no longer in scope for the 2025 framework. If retained for future analysis, it should be flagged as out-of-scope for the current endorsed framework. The notebooks' Raigoan constants (`raigoan_rp_2`/`raigoan_rp_5`) are orphaned — not in constants.py STATIONS, out of all framework scope, and their only use is commented out; they should be removed or migrated to a documented exploratory config.
- The repo persists its cached GEOGloWS data to the DEV blob stage (`BLOB_STAGE="dev"`). If any of this is ever needed operationally, the storage stage and path convention should be reviewed against the `{PROJECT_PREFIX}/{raw|processed}/{datasource}/` standard and promoted out of the dev slot.
