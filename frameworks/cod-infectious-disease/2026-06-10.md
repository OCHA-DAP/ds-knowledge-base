# DRC Cholera — 2026-06-10

## Summary

Revised version of the DRC cholera anticipatory action framework (the `cod-infectious-disease` framework, superseding version 2025-03-11). It is an observational trigger on PNECHOL-MD epidemiological surveillance — now keyed on **suspected cholera cases only** (deaths and case-incidence were dropped as risk indicators in this revision) — at health-zone level, with two independent geography-based scenarios: **Scenario 1** covers the five endemic provinces (Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu, Tanganyika, Haut-Lomami, Haut-Katanga) and **Scenario 2** covers all non-endemic provinces. Each scenario can trigger independently, releasing CERF funds to UNICEF and WHO for WASH and health rapid-response activities. The headline change versus 2025 is **funding**: the pre-arranged CERF envelope rose from $3M to **$4M over two years**, and the per-activation cap from $750,000 to **$1,000,000** (still a maximum of four activations), plus **$480,000 of partner cofinancing**. The framework was first endorsed in December 2022, has activated six times (twice in 2023, three times in 2025, once in 2026), and was re-endorsed on this revision (HCT 11 June, RC/HC 12 June, ERC 24 June 2026).

## Method

Weekly epidemiological data are sourced from **PNECHOL-MD** (Programme National pour l'Élimination du Choléra et le Contrôle des Autres Maladies Diarrhéiques), the national cholera programme — a national aggregate database by health zone and epidemiological week, updated weekly with a ~2-week validation lag. This revision adds **SIMR/IDSR** (Integrated Disease Surveillance and Response) data, shared by WHO, as a complementary source. Only aggregated (health-zone × week) data are used; no personal or sensitive data.

The trigger uses a single indicator — **suspected cholera cases** — evaluated against a per-scenario alert threshold that combines an absolute weekly case count with a week-over-week rate of increase. For 2026 the alert is defined as: **174 suspected cases/week** in a single health zone in the endemic provinces (**63** in non-endemic provinces), **OR** a **4× increase** in suspected cases over the previous week, **OR** a combination of the two. The threshold is recomputed **annually each January** from the last five years of data to maintain the intended return period; OCHA CHD calculates and circulates the new values, and the framework document is updated accordingly (no additional approval needed).

The framework is reached when a health zone stays at alert level for **three consecutive weeks** AND at least one suspected case in that health zone was **laboratory-confirmed in the preceding 30 days**. OCHA's Centre for Humanitarian Data runs the monitoring and alerts partners; WHO and UNICEF have 48 hours to collectively waive an activation (leadership resolves any disagreement within a further 24 hours), otherwise CERF disburses.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** Weekly PNECHOL-MD (and complementary IDSR/SIMR) **suspected cholera cases** at health-zone level. Deaths and case-incidence, used in 2025, are **no longer trigger indicators**.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** A health zone reaches alert level — ≥174 suspected cases/week (endemic) or ≥63 (non-endemic), or a ≥4× week-over-week jump, or a combination — for **three consecutive weeks**, with at least one lab-confirmed suspected case in the prior 30 days. A 6-month spatiotemporal cooldown blocks re-activation in the same or an adjacent province.
- **Activation structure:** Two independent scenarios by geography (Scenario 1 endemic provinces / Scenario 2 non-endemic provinces). Each can trigger separately and release up to $1M.
- **Calibration:** Thresholds set from the last five years of historical data and recomputed **annually in January** (changed from quarterly in 2025). The trigger is calibrated so that the full $4M envelope disburses on average once every two years (≈$2M/year average), with an estimated activation probability of about **once every six months per scenario**.
- **Authoritative source:** This version's framework PDF (`framework_doc`, 10 June 2026), read in full for this page. The repo (`code_ref`) implements/derives the design but was not re-checked this session (private repo).
- **Operated by:** OCHA Centre for Humanitarian Data (CHD) runs the monitoring and trigger alert; no external party operates the live trigger.

## Monitoring

OCHA CHD monitors weekly against PNECHOL-MD data (via the DSE / Disease Surveillance E-platform database), now complemented by WHO-shared IDSR/SIMR data. On trigger, CHD notifies WHO, UNICEF, OCHA DRC, OCHA HQ (CERF, CIS/HFSA, CRD/OAD), OCHA regional office (ROWCA), PNECHOL-MD and the RC/HC, and states which health zone. WHO and UNICEF have **48 hours** to collectively object; on disagreement, agency/PNECHOL/OCHA leadership decides within a further 24 hours; otherwise CERF disburses. The threshold values are recomputed and circulated each January. See `frameworks/cod-infectious-disease/2025-03-11.md` §Monitoring for the prior version.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Why more money:** The revision raises the per-activation cap from $750,000 to $1,000,000 and the total envelope from $3M to $4M (still max four activations over two years). The doc frames this as aligning the envelope with the modified (higher) trigger threshold and maximising impact, following six activations' worth of lessons and DRC's worst cholera outbreak in 25 years.
- **Why drop deaths and incidence:** The revision simplifies the trigger to suspected cases only, removing deaths and case-incidence as risk indicators "to account for the dynamic nature of cholera" — concentrating on the single most timely, least-lagged signal.
- **Why raise the endemic threshold (99→174 cases) and lower the non-endemic one (65→63):** Recalibrated on five years of data to keep the intended return period as case dynamics shifted; the threshold is now re-fit annually (January) rather than quarterly.
- **Why include IDSR/SIMR:** Adds WHO's longer, publicly accessible surveillance series to complement the (shorter, confidential) PNECHOL-MD record — the data-source migration the prior version's repo branch had been evaluating.
- **Why 48h waiver (was 24h) and 4-month implementation window (was 3):** Operational lessons from prior activations — more decision time for agencies and a longer delivery runway.

## Changes from previous version

Versus 2025-03-11 (per §1 Résumé and §5 Apprentissage of the 2026 doc):

1. **Funding increased:** per-activation cap $750,000 → **$1,000,000**; total pre-arranged CERF envelope $3M → **$4M** (still max four activations over two years). New: **$480,000 partner cofinancing** (UNICEF $200k prep; WHO $160k prep + $120k implementation).
2. **Trigger indicator narrowed:** deaths and case-incidence removed; **suspected cases only**.
3. **Thresholds recalibrated:** endemic 99 → **174 cases/week**; non-endemic 65 → **63 cases/week** (4× week-over-week increase retained).
4. **Baseline update cadence:** quarterly → **annual (January)**, on the last five years of data.
5. **IDSR/SIMR data formally included** as a WHO-shared complementary source (previously an unendorsed exploration).
6. **Improved activation protocol:** waiver window 24h → **48h**; implementation timeline **3 → 4 months**; greater geographic flexibility and stronger local coordination.
7. **Two-scenario endemic/non-endemic structure retained** (unchanged from 2025).
