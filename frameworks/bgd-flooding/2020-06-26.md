# Bangladesh Flood — 2020-06-26

> No DS code repo covers this pilot version; the trigger is reconstructed from public OCHA/CERF documents (`framework_doc` + the CERF allocation report + corroborating secondary sources). This page is a sourced institutional summary, not a derivation from code.

## Summary

Bangladesh's monsoon flood anticipatory action (AA) framework began as an OCHA/CERF-facilitated pilot covering the Jamuna river basin, pre-endorsed by the Resident Coordinator (25 June 2020) and the Emergency Relief Coordinator (26 June 2020). It used a two-step GloFAS/FFWC trigger to release pre-arranged CERF funding (up to USD 7.5M pre-arranged for one severe flooding event over a two-year pilot) to FAO, WFP and UNFPA, working with the Bangladesh Red Crescent Society (BDRCS), ahead of severe flooding in five vulnerable districts. It activated once, in July 2020: CERF released USD 5.2-5.3M within four hours of the trigger being reached -- reported as CERF's fastest-ever allocation -- reaching 321,715 people with cash, livestock protection, and sexual/reproductive-health and GBV services. This pilot version was later superseded (Padma basin added in 2023, further revised in 2025 -- see `frameworks/bgd-flooding/2025-04-25.md`).

## Method

The pilot combined two forecast systems monitored jointly by the UN Resident Coordinator's Office and partner agencies:
- **GloFAS** (Global Flood Awareness System): a probabilistic ensemble model giving a 15-day discharge forecast at the Bahadurabad gauging station on the Jamuna.
- **FFWC** (Bangladesh Flood Forecasting and Warning Centre): a deterministic government forecast system giving a 5-day water-level forecast at Bahadurabad, referenced against the government-defined "Danger Level".

When the pre-activation (readiness) condition was met, CERF released funds to FAO, WFP and UNFPA (WFP sub-granting to BDRCS) to begin pre-agreed anticipatory activities; a second, tighter-lead-time activation condition confirmed imminence. There is no evidence of an automated monitoring pipeline for this pilot version -- monitoring was carried out by the OCHA-led team tracking GloFAS/FFWC outputs directly.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** GloFAS ensemble discharge probability at Bahadurabad (pre-activation); FFWC deterministic water-level forecast at Bahadurabad (activation).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** The pre-activation trigger fires when GloFAS and/or the FFWC 15-day forecast model shows, at 10-day lead time, a more than 50% chance that discharge at Bahadurabad will cross 100,000 m3/s (a 1-in-5-year return-period flow) sustained over 3 days. The activation trigger fires when the FFWC 5-day deterministic forecast shows the water level at Bahadurabad crossing the government "Danger Level" + 0.85 m (reported as roughly a 1-in-10-year event).
- **Activation structure:** Single-basin (Jamuna only), two-step: pre-activation then activation. CERF releases funds on the pre-activation trigger; unused readiness funds are understood to be reimbursed if the activation trigger is not subsequently reached (consistent with later versions of this framework).
- **Calibration:** Pre-activation set at the 1-in-5-year GloFAS return-period flow (100,000 m3/s at Bahadurabad); activation set higher, at roughly a 1-in-10-year severity via the FFWC Danger Level + 0.85 m offset.
- **Authoritative source:** the pilot framework document, pre-endorsed 25-26 June 2020 (`framework_doc`). No code repo implements or derives this version.
- **Operated by:** OCHA-led monitoring team; no automated pipeline identified for this version.

## Historical activations

This pilot version activated **once**:

- **4 July 2020:** Triggered roughly two weeks ahead of the expected mid-to-late-July 2020 Jamuna flood peak. CERF released funding and gave agencies spending authority within four hours of the trigger being reached -- reported as CERF's fastest-ever allocation. Total CERF allocation: USD 5.2M (headline figure; itemized allocation-report total USD 5,339,084 -- FAO USD 500,000; WFP USD 4,250,000, including a subgrant to BDRCS; UNFPA USD 589,084), against a pre-arranged ceiling of USD 7.5M. Reached 321,715 people (110,316 women; 99,188 men; 39,210 girls; 38,772 boys) via: unconditional cash transfers (WFP/BDRCS, ~USD 53 per household to 61,500 households: 55,500 WFP, 6,000 BDRCS); livestock protection and flood-proof storage of productive assets (FAO, 14,000 households); and sexual and reproductive health, GBV and psychosocial support services (UNFPA, ~15,000 women and girls). Original plan targeted 390,000 people.

No further activations occurred under this pilot version; the framework was subsequently revised (Padma basin added in 2023) before its next confirmed activation in July 2024 under the 2025-04-25 version (see `frameworks/bgd-flooding/2025-04-25.md`).

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Two-step pre-activation/activation design:** Mirrors the structure retained in later versions -- a longer-lead probabilistic GloFAS trigger to begin pre-positioning, followed by a shorter-lead deterministic FFWC trigger to confirm imminence, balancing early action against false-alarm risk.
- **Jamuna-only, 5-district scope:** The pilot deliberately started narrow (Bogura, Gaibandha, Kurigram, Jamalpur, Sirajganj) to prove the model before expansion; the Padma basin (3 further districts) was added only in the 2023 revision.
- **CERF "no-regrets" pre-arranged financing:** A standing CERF ceiling (USD 7.5M) was pre-arranged for a single severe-flooding event over a two-year pilot window, enabling near-instant disbursement (within four hours of the trigger) rather than a post-event appeal.
- **Multi-sectoral agency split:** FAO (livelihoods/livestock), WFP+BDRCS (cash), and UNFPA (SRHR/GBV/PSS) were selected to cover complementary sectoral needs rather than a single response modality.
