# Bangladesh Flood — 2025-04-25

> The canonical trigger is described in the 2025 framework PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo (`code_ref`) contains the underlying analysis (GloFAS return-period calibration, FFWC station analysis) that derived the trigger but does not implement a live monitoring pipeline.

## Summary

Bangladesh's monsoon flood anticipatory action framework uses a two-step trigger system to release pre-arranged CERF funding ahead of severe flooding in the Jamuna and Padma river basins. A probabilistic GloFAS-based readiness trigger fires at 12–15 day lead time; a deterministic FFWC-based action trigger fires at 3–5 day lead time. The framework covers 8 districts and 38 upazilas, and was first activated in 2020 and most recently in July 2024, disbursing $6.2M from CERF and $2.3M from partners. The 2025 version is pre-approved for up to $6M from CERF covering the 2025 and 2026 monsoon seasons.

## Method

The framework combines two forecast systems:
- **GloFAS** (Global Flood Awareness System, v3.1 used for calibration; operational system runs on v4): a probabilistic ensemble model providing 15-day river discharge forecasts. Calibrated using GloFAS reanalysis to derive 1-in-5-year return period thresholds at specific gauging stations.
- **FFWC** (Bangladesh Flood Forecasting & Warning Centre): a deterministic operational system providing 5-day water level forecasts at key gauging stations, referenced against government-defined "Danger Levels."
- **RIMES** (Regional Integrated Multi-Hazard Early Warning System): also used alongside GloFAS to confirm sustained/increasing trend for the action trigger.

Monitoring is done manually by an OCHA-led team (led by H.M. Shahid Hassan) who populate two Google Sheets log files (one per basin) twice daily from June onward. When a trigger condition is met, the monitoring team emails the Resident Coordinator and agencies. There is no automated pipeline in the repo.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** GloFAS ensemble discharge probabilities (readiness); FFWC deterministic water level forecasts + GloFAS/RIMES trend confirmation (action).
- **Decision rule:** There are four trigger conditions — two basins (Jamuna, Padma), each with a readiness and an action window:
  - *Readiness (Jamuna):* GloFAS ensemble ≥50% probability of discharge at Bahadurabad exceeding the 1-in-5-year RP (100,000 m³/s) for 3 consecutive days, at maximum 15-day lead time.
  - *Readiness (Padma):* GloFAS ensemble ≥50% probability of discharge at Baruria transit near Goalando exceeding the 1-in-5-year RP for 3 consecutive days, at 12–15-day lead time.
  - *Action (Jamuna):* FFWC 5-day deterministic forecast crosses government Danger Level + 0.85 m at Bahadurabad, AND GloFAS/RIMES shows sustained or increasing discharge trend at Bahadurabad for at least 3 consecutive days from the day the danger level is forecast to be crossed.
  - *Action (Padma):* FFWC 5-day deterministic forecast (at 3–5 day lead time) crosses 1-in-5-year RP ("Danger Level" + 0.5 m) at Mawa station, AND GloFAS/RIMES shows sustained or increasing discharge trend at Baruria transit for at least 3 consecutive days.
- **Activation structure:** Two independent basin triggers (Jamuna and Padma). Either basin can fire independently. Within each basin, a readiness trigger precedes an action trigger, but an action-without-readiness is also valid (CERF disburses 100% immediately). CERF releases funds upon readiness trigger; agencies may begin readiness activities between readiness and action; unused funds from readiness-only seasons are reimbursed.
- **Calibration:** 1-in-5-year return period from GloFAS reanalysis (v3.1 used for calibration). The Jamuna 1-in-5-year RP threshold is explicitly stated in the draft trigger message as 100,000 m³/s. Threshold for Padma at Goalando/Baruria was derived similarly (see analysis/06–08). FFWC danger levels are government-defined; the framework adds fixed offsets (+0.85 m Jamuna, +0.5 m Padma) to set a higher-than-DL action threshold corresponding approximately to the 1-in-5-year return period.
- **Authoritative source:** 2025 framework PDF (April 2025, endorsed 25 April 2025). The repo contains the calibration analysis, not a live trigger implementation.
- **Operated by:** OCHA-led monitoring team (manual, Google Sheets-based).

## Monitoring

Monitoring is conducted manually by the OCHA-led forecast monitoring team (H.M. Shahid Hassan) starting in June each monsoon season. GloFAS and FFWC data are checked twice daily and recorded in Google Sheets:
- Jamuna: [Google Sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/13ZZT16susCEjsCLHIDOjtYA-A0_r2bnD/edit#gid=705641025)
- Padma: [Google Sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1r3CzPFA1yRK1x3bN90UfWEsAKZ2FmkgH/edit#gid=1017991757)

The OCHA-led team communicates trigger status to the RC/RCO and concerned agencies via email and WhatsApp when readiness or action thresholds are approached or reached. A companion monitoring app exists for Bangladesh cyclones (`ds-aa-bgd-cyclone-monitoring`) but no equivalent automated app exists for flooding.

## Historical activations

This framework has been activated twice:
1. **2020 (Jamuna):** First OCHA-coordinated activation of the flood AA framework. Exact trigger date not specified in the 2025 PDF.
2. **July 2024 (Jamuna):** Most recent activation per the 2025 PDF. Disbursed $6.2 million from CERF and $2.3 million from partners to provide lifesaving assistance ahead of peak flooding.

The 2024 activation informed the 2025 framework revision, including lessons on joint early warning, common beneficiary database utilization, and coordination with government.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Two-step trigger:** The readiness/action two-step design gives agencies a window to pre-position supplies and mobilize staff before the action trigger fires. Readiness at 12–15 days provides operational lead time; action at 3–5 days confirms imminence.
- **1-in-5-year threshold:** Chosen as the severity level where floods "surpass community coping ability" — defined as affecting >40% of a population and/or damaging >20% of household assets. This calibration was conducted against GloFAS reanalysis.
- **GloFAS v3.1 update:** The upgrade from v2.2 doubled the number of successful pre-activations historically, reduced false positives, and enabled extension of readiness trigger from 10-day to 15-day lead time. The 2025 framework operates on v4 but the repo calibration analysis used v3.1.
- **Padma basin expansion:** Added in the 2023 edition (expanded from Jamuna-only) based on a combination of forecast skill, flood risk, INFORM vulnerability, and implementation capacity. The Padma readiness trigger specifies a 12–15 day range (rather than the Jamuna "maximum 15 days") reflecting skill analysis specific to the Baruria/Goalando station.
- **FFWC "Danger Level" offset:** Using DL+0.85 m (Jamuna) and DL+0.5 m (Padma, where "DL+0.5 = 1-in-5-year RP") rather than the raw danger level ensures the trigger fires only for severe events. The PDF notes FFWC has a negative bias (underprediction) at longer lead times, which may cause missed action triggers.
- **CERF no-regrets disbursement:** CERF releases funds on a no-regrets basis immediately upon readiness trigger, with readiness-tranche funds refundable if no action trigger follows. This design balances speed of disbursement with fiduciary accountability.

## Changes from previous version

- **Supersedes:** The 2023 edition (approximate date unknown; Padma basin was first added in 2023).
- **GloFAS upgrade:** From v2.2 to v3.1 (and now operational on v4), enabling 15-day instead of 10-day readiness trigger.
- **Maintained Padma coverage:** Geographic expansion first introduced in 2023 is maintained in 2025 (no further expansion or contraction).
- **Co-financing target:** $3,993,055 secured from WFP, BDRCS, BRAC, Concern Worldwide, Start Network, and Save the Children — a formal co-financing mobilization mechanism formalized in this version.
- **Joint early warning:** 2024 activation experience led to more explicit provisions for joint early warning messages and common beneficiary database utilization.
- **2024 activation lessons:** Updated action plan reflects learnings from the July 2024 activation.
