# Bangladesh Flood — 2025-04-25

> The canonical trigger is the framework PDF at `framework_doc`; this page explains it and reconciles it with the repo.

## Summary

Bangladesh's OCHA-coordinated anticipatory action framework for monsoon river floods covers two river basins: the Jamuna (Brahmaputra) and the Padma. It uses a two-step trigger system — a 15-day readiness trigger based on GloFAS probabilistic discharge forecasts, and a 5-day action trigger based on the Bangladesh Flood Forecasting and Warning Centre (FFWC) deterministic water-level forecast, confirmed by GloFAS/RIMES trend signals. When either trigger fires, up to $6M from CERF is automatically released to FAO, UNICEF, UNFPA, and WFP to deliver unconditional cash, WASH supplies, animal feed, and GBV/SRH support to roughly 150,000 pre-identified vulnerable households across 38 upazilas. The framework has been activated twice — in 2020 and July 2024.

## Method

Data flows from two forecast sources:

1. **GloFAS (v3.1)** — global probabilistic river discharge forecast. At each issue time the ensemble probability of exceeding the 1-in-5-year return period discharge is computed at the Bahadurabad gauge (Jamuna) and the Baruria transit/Goalando gauge (Padma). If the probability exceeds 50% for three consecutive days within the forecast window, the readiness trigger fires.

2. **FFWC (Bangladesh Flood Forecasting and Warning Centre)** — deterministic 5-day water-level forecast. Thresholds are defined relative to the government's official "Danger Level" at each gauge. If the FFWC forecast crosses the threshold and GloFAS/RIMES shows a sustained or increasing discharge trend for 3+ consecutive days, the action trigger fires.

An OCHA-led forecast monitoring team populates a Google Sheets log twice daily from June onward. When a trigger condition is met, the team emails the RC/RCO and all partner agencies with an intervention map identifying affected unions. CERF approval letters are sent immediately on readiness trigger; no further decision meeting is required to activate.

Intervention targeting uses a common pre-verified beneficiary database of poor/vulnerable households in the 38 priority upazilas (Adm3-level pcodes listed in the PDF annex).

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** GloFAS v3.1 ensemble discharge probability (readiness); FFWC 5-day deterministic water-level forecast + GloFAS/RIMES probabilistic trend confirmation (action).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** Readiness fires when GloFAS gives more-than-50% chance that discharge will exceed the 1-in-5-year return period for 3 consecutive days within a 15-day window (Jamuna) or 12–15-day window (Padma). Action fires when FFWC forecasts water level crossing a defined threshold for 3 consecutive days within 5 days, AND a longer-range model (GloFAS or RIMES) confirms the discharge is sustained or rising for those same 3 days.
- **Activation structure:** Two rivers × two stages = 4 independent trigger components. Any one of the four can fire independently. CERF is released on the first readiness trigger of the monsoon season; action trigger funds complete the disbursement for final-mile delivery.
- **Calibration:** Return-period thresholds calibrated against GloFAS reanalysis and FFWC historical water-level records. The 1-in-5-year RP was chosen as the minimum threshold for a "severe shock" estimated to affect 40%+ of a population or damage 20%+ of household assets. Calibration analysis is in the repo (`analysis/` notebooks) but was finalized externally (Google Slides linked in PDF footnotes 7–8). GloFAS was updated from v2.2 to v3.1 between the 2020 and 2023 frameworks, which doubled successful pre-activations and reduced false alarms for the Jamuna readiness trigger.
- **Authoritative source:** 2025 framework PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo implements supporting analysis but does not contain the operational monitoring pipeline.
- **Operated by:** OCHA RCO Bangladesh (OCHA-led forecast monitoring team since 2025; was BDRCS-led in 2023).

## Historical activations

Two real activations have occurred:

1. **2020** — Jamuna basin. First OCHA-coordinated AA activation in Bangladesh. Yielded extensive independent evaluations and learning. The BDRCS had been doing anticipatory action since 2015 under its own EAP.

2. **July 2024** — Jamuna basin. $6.2M released from CERF and $2.3M from partners. Provided lifesaving assistance (cash, WASH, animal feed, GBV support) ahead of peak flooding. Shaheda, a cash recipient, is quoted in the 2025 PDF. This activation directly informed the 2025 framework revision.

The framework has not been activated for the Padma basin (added in 2023).

## Key decisions & rationale

- **1-in-5-year RP threshold:** Chosen as the minimum severity at which a flood affects 40%+ of a population or damages 20%+ of household assets — the agreed-upon humanitarian impact threshold.
- **Two-step trigger design:** The 15-day readiness trigger (GloFAS probabilistic) provides early warning for pre-positioning and readiness activities; the 5-day action trigger (FFWC deterministic) confirms that action should start. CERF releases on readiness to begin activities; action trigger confirms full deployment.
- **GloFAS v3.1 upgrade:** The move from v2.2 to v3.1 (between 2020 and 2023 versions) was critical — it doubled successful pre-activations for Jamuna while reducing false alarms, and allowed extending the readiness lead time from 10 to 15 days.
- **Padma basin expansion (2023):** Added Mawa (FFWC action gauge) and Baruria/Goalando (GloFAS readiness gauge) based on analysis showing sufficient forecast skill. The Padma readiness has a 12–15 day window (not max 15 days like Jamuna) reflecting the shorter usable forecast horizon for this basin.
- **FFWC underprediction bias:** A known limitation acknowledged in both the 2023 and 2025 PDFs. FFWC systematically underpredicts at longer lead times and high water levels, creating risk of missed action triggers. Collaboration with FFWC on improving this is ongoing.
- **CERF trigger mechanics:** CERF releases at the first readiness trigger of a season. If no action trigger follows, unused readiness funds are reimbursed. If only an action trigger fires (no prior readiness), CERF releases 100% of funds but the narrower lead time limits what can be achieved.
- **Start Network separate trigger:** Start Bangladesh uses a different threshold (10-day forecast, >70% probability at district level) with $750K pre-arranged. It operates in parallel, not as part of the CERF framework.

## Changes from previous version

Changes from the 2023 version (endorsed 14 August 2023):

1. **Monitoring team:** Changed from "BDRCS-led" to "OCHA-led" forecast monitoring team — reflecting a shift in operational ownership.
2. **CERF envelope reduced:** 2023 allocated up to $7.5M CERF across Jamuna, Padma, and a cyclone reserve bucket; 2025 reduced to $6M dedicated to floods only. The cyclone AA is now a separate framework. (The 2024 activation used $6.2M CERF.)
3. **Cash amount increased:** 2023 stipulated 4,500 Taka per household; 2025 increased to 5,000 Taka per household.
4. **Co-financing increased:** 2025 reports $3,993,055 in co-financing (WFP, BDRCS, BRAC, Concern Worldwide, Start Network, Save the Children); the 2023 version reported agency co-financing of ~$3.7M (WFP, FAO, UNFPA, BDRCS, Save the Children) alongside CERF.
5. **Trigger text unchanged:** The trigger thresholds and indicator logic are identical between 2023 and 2025 versions. No threshold recalibration occurred.
6. **Learning integration:** The 2025 version explicitly incorporates lessons from the 2024 activation (quotes recipient; references impact evaluations).
7. **WFP orientation of DMCs:** 2025 adds WFP organizing orientation of local Disaster Management Committees (DMCs) on AA intervention.

## Open questions / known issues

- The Mawa absolute danger level is not stated in the PDF — only "Danger Level + 0.5m". The repo analysis (notebook 07) used 6.11m as the Mawa baseline danger level, but this is exploratory and not confirmed as the official FFWC danger level for that station.
- No trigger-monitoring code exists in the repo; live monitoring relies on manually-updated Google Sheets. This is an operational fragility.
- The repo analysis covers only through 2022; the 2024 activation occurred outside the analyzed period and no post-hoc analysis of that activation appears in the repo.
- FFWC underprediction bias (acknowledged in both 2023 and 2025 PDFs) has not been resolved; collaboration is "ongoing."
- Geographic coverage could expand to additional river basins, but no timeline is given.
- A 2026 companion cyclone framework is implied ("expanding to other hazards") but is separate from this flood framework.
