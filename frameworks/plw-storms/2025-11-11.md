# Palau Tropical Cyclone — 2025-11-11 (development)

> The canonical trigger calibration is the analysis at `code_ref`; this page explains it and does not redefine it. The framework has not yet been formally endorsed.

## Summary

Palau is developing a national Anticipatory Action Framework for tropical cyclones, led by the National Emergency Management Office (NEMO) and Palau Weather Service (PWS) with support from OCHA, UNDRR, and the Pacific Humanitarian Team AA Technical Working Group. The framework applies a two-tier trigger combining wind speed and 2-day rainfall thresholds, calibrated against historical IBTrACS/IMERG observations but monitored operationally on forecasts (a mixed basis). When a storm meets either tier, pre-agreed activities (shelter, health/protection, agriculture) are activated using pre-arranged financing. As of the November 2025 workshop, the trigger thresholds had been agreed in principle but not yet finalized or submitted for NEC/Cabinet endorsement.

## Method

1. **Historical calibration (repo):** IBTrACS storm tracks are filtered to those passing within 250 km of Palau. IMERG daily precipitation is clipped to Palau's ADM0 boundary and a 2-day rolling mean is computed for each storm's passage window. Each storm is assigned a wind-speed category (JMA × 1.12 = USA 1-min equivalent) and a maximum 2-day rainfall value. The optimization notebook sweeps candidate wind-category and rainfall thresholds to find combinations that trigger at a target return period (~3 years using 25 seasons, 2000–2024). EM-DAT impact data (total affected) is overlaid as bubble size for visual validation.

2. **Trigger design (workshop/PDF):** A two-tier structure was agreed. Tier 1 activates readiness/early actions; Tier 2 activates full response. Both tiers use wind speed AND rainfall as joint conditions. Thresholds are to be validated against the three major historical events (Haiyan 2013, Surigae 2021, Rai 2021) and finalized by PWS and NEMO with CHD technical support.

3. **Operational monitoring:** PWS (Palau National Weather Service Office) is the primary authority. Forecast data comes from JTWC, Guam NWS, and Japan Meteorological Agency. The live trigger fires when PWS-monitored conditions meet the agreed thresholds; no automated pipeline exists yet.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** IBTrACS-derived wind speed (knots) within 250 km of Palau + IMERG 2-day accumulated rainfall averaged over the whole country (mm / inches)
- **Decision rule (plain language):** When a tropical system comes within range of Palau AND both wind speed and 2-day rainfall cross the tier threshold simultaneously, the corresponding activation level fires. Tier 1 activates readiness and preparedness actions 5 days out; Tier 2 triggers full pre-positioned response at 48 hours.
- **Activation structure:** Two tiers (severity). Tier 1 = readiness (lower thresholds); Tier 2 = full activation (higher thresholds). Both use an AND condition (wind AND rainfall must both exceed the threshold).
- **Calibration:** Return-period approach. Target ~3-year RP using 25 years of IBTrACS/IMERG data (2000–2024). Scope analysis cites existing benchmarks: Category 1+ (>34 kn) within 250 km has a 1.8-year RP; Category 3+ (>64 kn) has a 4.8-year RP.
- **Authoritative source:** Workshop Final Report (2025-11-11, `framework_doc`). The scope analysis (2025-06-11) is the precursor document; both are in `framework_doc_annexes`.
- **Operated by:** NEMO/PWS for the live decision. OCHA Centre for Humanitarian Data provides technical support for trigger calibration. No third-party system (IRI Maproom, PRISM, etc.).

## Trigger windows

**Counting rule:** Two distinct tiers with different severity thresholds constitute two windows. Both apply country-wide and differ only on the threshold level and expected response depth.

| window | basis | indicator | threshold | lead time | return period | releases |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — readiness | mixed (forecast-monitored, obs-calibrated) | wind-speed AND rainfall-2day | "34 mph winds + 3–4 in (76–102 mm) 2-day rainfall" | ~120h (T–5 days) | ~1.8 years (Cat 1+) | RDAT activation, stock checks, pre-positioning, early warning messages |
| Tier 2 — full activation | mixed (forecast-monitored, obs-calibrated) | wind-speed AND rainfall-2day | "≥39 mph + >4 in (>102 mm) 2-day rainfall" | ~48h (T–2 days) | ~3 years (target) | Evacuation centre opening, NFI distribution, medical deployment, GBV services, agriculture inputs |

> Note: thresholds in the workshop report mix knots and mph. Tier 1 is stated as "~30 knots (34 mph)" — these are not equivalent (~30 kn = ~34.5 mph); the parenthetical mph value appears to be the operative threshold. Tier 2 uses "≥39 mph" (~34 kn), which is nearly the same wind speed as Tier 1's "34 mph." This internal inconsistency is likely a draft/reporting artefact; the two thresholds were intended to be distinct. PWS/CHD must resolve before finalization.

## Monitoring

No operational monitoring pipeline is deployed as of the November 2025 workshop. The roadmap calls for finalizing trigger analysis and developing an Operational Plan and SOPs for trigger notification and activation. PWS will lead monitoring using JTWC, Guam NWS, and JMA forecast products. OCHA CHD will support the technical design of the trigger mechanism.

## Historical activations

This framework has **never been activated** — it is still in development. No pre-arranged financing has been released under this framework.

Past significant cyclone events in Palau (documented in the scope analysis, used for calibration):
- Bopha (December 2012): 100 kn wind at Palau, 92 homes destroyed, $10.1 M damage
- Haiyan (November 2013): 115 kn wind at Palau, 433 homes damaged/destroyed, $5.9 M damage
- Surigae (April 2021): 85 kn wind at Palau, 7,288 people affected, 1,500 homes damaged, $4.8 M damage
- Rai (December 2021): 70 kn wind at close range, 240 homes damaged, $1.5 M damage

These are past disaster responses (not AA activations), included as the historical basis for trigger calibration.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Wind + rainfall joint trigger:** NWSO confirmed wind speed and rainfall are the most operationally meaningful indicators for Palau's context, where low-category storms paired with heavy rainfall still cause significant damage (as seen with Rai 2021 and Tropical Storm Banyan 2022). A single wind threshold alone would miss high-impact rainfall events.
- **Two-tier structure:** Tier 1 enables early readiness actions with longer lead time (~5 days), when forecast certainty is lower but preparation is valuable. Tier 2 triggers the full response at ~48h when the forecast is more reliable. This balances false-alarm cost against readiness benefit.
- **250 km proximity buffer:** The scope analysis uses 250 km as the canonical distance for return-period calculations (cited as the basis for the 1.8- and 4.8-year RPs). The ibtracs.md exploration notebook tested 400 km as an alternative but the optimization analysis uses 250 km.
- **IBTrACS JMA source with USA wind conversion:** The repo uses JMA wind speeds × 1.12 to approximate USA 1-minute sustained winds. This conversion is standard (IBTrACS documentation) but introduces a ~12% upward shift. The workshop report does not state which wind standard the final thresholds reference.
- **National ownership:** The framework is deliberately nationally led, with NEMO and PWS chairing the AA TWG and controlling trigger decisions. OCHA provides technical support but is not the operating authority.

## Changes from previous version

This is the first version of the Palau AA framework. The scope analysis (June 2025) was a precursor scoping document, not a framework version, so `supersedes` is null. Key evolution between the scope analysis and workshop report:
- Scope analysis (June 2025): indicative thresholds of 25 knots + 2 inches; single-tier design implied.
- Workshop report (November 2025): two-tier design agreed; thresholds shifted to ~30 kn + 3–4 in (Tier 1) and ≥39 mph + >4 in (Tier 2).
- Workshop confirmed AA will be annexed to the revised national DRM Framework (2016).

## Open questions / known issues

1. **Tier 1 vs Tier 2 wind threshold inconsistency:** The workshop report states Tier 1 as "~30 knots (34 mph)" and Tier 2 as "≥39 mph." At 30 knots ≈ 34.5 mph, these are essentially the same wind speed. The two thresholds must be distinct for the two-tier system to function — this appears to be a drafting error (possibly Tier 1 intended as ≤34 mph and Tier 2 ≥39 mph, or the 34 mph value was meant as a different unit).
2. **Return period alignment:** The optimization notebook targets a 3-year RP for Tier 2. The scope analysis independently cites 1.8-year RP for Cat 1+ within 250 km. These imply different trigger frequencies; the mapping between IBTrACS categories and the workshop thresholds needs to be made explicit.
3. **Financing uncommitted:** No fuel funding has been pre-arranged. The ADB USD 20 million contingent credit, IFRC DREF, and government emergency budget are candidates but none confirmed.
4. **Formal endorsement pending:** The framework is not yet endorsed. The roadmap called for NEC review in October 2025 and Cabinet endorsement in November 2025, but the workshop (11–12 November 2025) was still in the design phase.
5. **400 km vs 250 km buffer:** The ibtracs.md notebook saves outputs for both 250 km (default) and 400 km buffers but the obsv_optimization.md notebook does not clearly specify which it uses (loads `ibtracs_imerg_stats.parquet` which corresponds to the 250 km default, but also loads `ibtracs_imerg_d250_stats.parquet` as a commented-out variant). The canonical buffer for the live trigger needs to be locked.
