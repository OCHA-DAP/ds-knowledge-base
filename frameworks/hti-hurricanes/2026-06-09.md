# Haiti hurricanes — 2026-06-09 (dev, melissa-exposure branch)

> In-development successor to the 2024-08-23 endorsed version; not yet endorsed, no published framework doc; trigger from the `melissa-exposure` branch and subject to change.
> The canonical trigger is the code at `code_ref`; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

This development version redesigns the Haiti tropical-cyclone AA trigger around **population exposure to sustained winds** rather than distance-gated wind+rain thresholds. Initiated after Hurricane Melissa (October 2025) — the first real activation under the endorsed v1 framework — the branch explores a simplified **OR trigger** combining: (1) NHC forecast wind-buffer population exposure, (2) NHC forecast 2-day rainfall, (3) observed 2-day rainfall, and (4) an NHC Hurricane Warning for Haiti. The selected design option ("Hurricane Warning OR, 64 kt") targets approximately 12 triggering storms per 24 seasons (RP ~2.1 yr). No new framework PDF exists; analysis lives in `exploration/wsp_trigger.py` on the `melissa-exposure` branch.

## Method

Data flows from two sources. **Wind exposure** is computed from NHC wind-swath geometries (forecast-only future cone + cumulative observed swath) intersected with GHSL 2025 population rasters at ADM0, stored in DB tables `storms.nhc_tracks_fcastonly_exposure` and `storms.nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure` (via the `ds-storms-pipeline`). **Rainfall** continues to use CHIRPS-GEFS/IMERG 2-day rolling national-mean totals from the existing `nhc.load_hist_fcast_monitors` archive. **Hurricane Warning** is a boolean flag from the NHC advisory text, hardcoded in the marimo app for historical storms.

The indicator data is joined per storm at ADM0 level. A 2D optimization sweep over exposure threshold × forecast-rain threshold determines thresholds that exactly activate `n = 12` storms over the 2002–2025 record, with the observed-rain threshold set deterministically from the residual. The best option is chosen by maximising CERF-storm count, then total affected population, then minimising the exposure threshold.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** max(fcastonly exposure + cumulative observed exposure) at ≥64 kt OR forecast 2-day rolling rainfall ≥ threshold OR observed 2-day rolling rainfall ≥ threshold OR NHC Hurricane Warning for Haiti. Pre-cutoff issuances only (>48 h before closest approach), consistent with the v1 action-window cutoff.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** a storm activates if, at any point before the 48-hour lead-time cutoff, the total population exposed to forecast sustained winds ≥64 kt crosses the calibrated threshold — OR the NHC issues a Hurricane Warning for Haiti — OR the 2-day national-mean forecast rainfall exceeds the calibrated threshold. After the cutoff, the observed-only ≥64 kt wind exposure OR the observed 2-day rainfall can still activate the framework.
- **Activation structure:** single stage (OR logic across four conditions); no explicit readiness/action/obsv split in the new design. The marimo app presents one combined trigger per storm.
- **Calibration:** return-period against 2002–2025 IBTrACS/NHC/IMERG record. Target n=12 storms (RP ~2.1 yr over 24 seasons). Optimization maximises CERF-storm coverage before coverage of high-impact storms. Wind level is 64 kt (the "Hurricane Warning OR, 64 kt ★" selected option per the activation-summary doc cell).
- **Authoritative source:** `exploration/wsp_trigger.py` on the `melissa-exposure` branch (no framework PDF).
- **Operated by:** OCHA/CHD (same as v1). The deployed monitoring app at `https://chd-ds-aa-hti-hurricanes-app.azurewebsites.net` still runs the v1 trigger; the new design is not yet deployed.

## Trigger windows

**Counting rule:** the new design collapses to one conceptual activation gate with OR conditions across forecast and observed time windows.

| window | basis | indicator | threshold | lead time | return period | releases |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| forecast | forecast | wind exposure (≥64 kt pop) OR forecast 2-day rain OR NHC Hurricane Warning for Haiti | optimised by 2D sweep (exp threshold computed dynamically; ~2.1 yr RP over all conditions) | >48 h before closest approach | ~2.1 yr (per season, combined) | not yet defined |
| observed | observational | observed-only ≥64 kt wind exposure OR observed 2-day rolling rainfall ≥ threshold | same exposure threshold as forecast window; observed-rain threshold optimised as residual (deterministic from remaining slots after forecast conditions are counted) | 0–48 h / post-landfall | included in ~2.1 yr combined | not yet defined |

Note: exact numeric thresholds are not hardcoded in any constants file; they are computed dynamically in the `rain_trigger_opt_hur` cell of `wsp_trigger.py` and vary with the historical record used.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Why exposure instead of distance?** The v1 trigger used a 230 km proximity gate as a proxy for hazard. The melissa-exposure branch replaces this with direct population-exposure estimates from NHC wind-swath buffers intersected with GHSL 2025 population, which is more physically meaningful and aligns with the `ds-storms-pipeline` exposure infrastructure built since v1.

**Why OR logic (not AND)?** The v1 AND logic (wind AND rain) is retained for the v1 pipeline but the new design relaxes this to OR: a storm with extreme wind exposure but modest rainfall (e.g., fast-moving category 5) or extreme rainfall but modest tracked exposure should still activate. The Hurricane Warning condition is an operational anchor — it captures storms with an official government-level warning regardless of the modelled exposure thresholds.

**Fcastonly + cumulative observed (non-overlapping sum):** The exposure indicator is the sum of the future forecast cone exposure and the cumulative observed swath up to each issuance, using `nhc_tracks_fcastonly_exposure` and `nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure` joined via `pd.merge_asof`. This avoids double-counting the already-swept area.

**Selected option: "Hurricane Warning OR, 64 kt ★":** The activation-summary cell in `wsp_trigger.py` documents the selected option as the Hurricane-Warning OR variant at 64 kt. ~5 historical storms carry an NHC Hurricane Warning for Haiti; the optimization fills the remaining ~7 slots. The 64 kt wind level most closely aligns with the v1 trigger (also 64 kt for the forecast stages) and with the NHC Hurricane Warning threshold.

**Post-Melissa analysis motivation:** The `exp_calc.md` and `exp_dgpc.md` notebooks compute ADM2-level wind and rainfall exposure for Melissa specifically and validate against DGPC impact data, supporting the calibration rationale.

## Changes from previous version

The endorsed 2024-08-23 version uses a **distance-gated wind+rain AND trigger** (≤230 km proximity, wind ≥64/50 kt AND 2-day rain ≥42/70 mm) across three time stages. This development version:

1. **Drops the distance gate** in favour of direct population exposure from wind-swath buffers (fcastonly + cumulative observed, non-overlapping).
2. **Drops the AND logic** — OR logic across exposure / rainfall / Hurricane Warning conditions.
3. **Collapses three stages to two windows** (forecast and observed) in the conceptual model.
4. **Adds NHC Hurricane Warning as a mandatory activation condition** — any storm with a Hurricane Warning for Haiti automatically activates regardless of exposure or rainfall values.
5. **Changes calibration target** from RP ~3–5 yr (per stage) to ~2.1 yr (combined, n=12 over 24 seasons).
6. **Uses GHSL 2025 population** as the exposure denominator.
7. **Relies on dev-DB tables** (`storms.nhc_tracks_fcastonly_exposure`, `storms.nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure`) populated by the `ds-storms-pipeline`, which did not exist at v1 design time.

The v1 trigger thresholds and pipeline code remain in `src/constants.py` and `pipelines/` unchanged as of the branch tip (SHA 731776c).

## Open questions / known issues

- **No canonical threshold file:** the optimised thresholds are computed at app runtime; they need to be committed to `src/constants.py` or a config before any pipeline update.
- **Wind level choice:** three options (34 / 50 / 64 kt) are explored; 64 kt is documented as selected in the activation-summary cell but this is not a formal decision.
- **Single-stage vs multi-stage:** the new design has one activation gate; CERF typically requires a readiness + action structure. Whether to re-introduce a readiness stage (e.g., at 120 h) is unresolved.
- **Observed-rainfall threshold:** computed deterministically as the residual after the forecast + Hurricane Warning conditions are counted; no independent calibration.
- **Historical data coverage:** Melissa 2025 is imputed in the historical record (not yet in EM-DAT; observed rain = 80 mm hardcoded from IMERG, CERF amount = $4M imputed). The optimization is sensitive to Melissa's inclusion.
- **Pipeline update not started:** `pipelines/check_fcast_trigger.py` and `check_obsv_trigger.py` still run v1 logic; updating them to the new exposure-based trigger requires DB access to the `nhc_tracks_fcastonly_exposure` table and new monitoring record schema.
- **Obsv pipeline TODO** (carried from v1): the observational pipeline is still triggered by the old IMERG pipeline (TODO: switch to `ds-raster-stats` trigger).
