# Cuba Tropical Cyclone — 2026-06-17 (development)

> **In-development successor to the 2025-08-26 endorsed version; not yet endorsed, no published framework doc; trigger design is from the `2026-trigger` branch (SHA e4f5971) and subject to change.**
>
> The canonical trigger is the code at `code_ref`; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

# --- section: summary ---

## Summary

This development page captures the in-progress 2026 trigger redesign for Cuba hurricanes anticipatory action, as it exists on the `2026-trigger` branch of `ocha-dap/ds-aa-cub-hurricanes`. The endorsed 2025-08-26 version uses NHC wind speed (knots) within a 230 km ZMA buffer as the primary forecast indicator, combined with IMERG rainfall for the observational window. The 2026 redesign proposes replacing the wind-speed-in-ZMA indicator with **population exposed to hurricane-force winds** (at 34/50/64 kt thresholds), using NHC wind-buffer polygons intersected with GHSL population rasters, and combining this with observed IMERG rainfall in an OR trigger structure. The redesign is in active exploration via a marimo interactive app (`exploration/wsp_trigger.py`); no option has been selected and no threshold values have been committed to the monitoring pipeline. No framework doc exists; the endorsed version remains authoritative until this design is approved.

# --- section: method ---

## Method

The new design builds on a wind-exposure database computed by the `ds-storms-pipeline` (tables `storms.nhc_tracks_fcast_exposure`, `storms.nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure`, `storms.ibtracs_wind_exposure` in the dev DB, all filtered to `iso3 = 'CUB'`, `admin_level = 0`).

1. **Exposure metric construction**: For each NHC forecast issuance, the forecast population exposure (people within the X-kt wind-buffer polygon) is read from `nhc_tracks_fcast_exposure`. Observed exposure accumulates over the storm period from `nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure`. The "total exposure" at any forecast issuance = forecast exposure + cumulative maximum observed exposure up to that point (`pd.merge_asof` join). For historical storms (pre-NHC DB), `ibtracs_wind_exposure` provides observed exposure only (no forecast series).

2. **Indicator set**: Three wind levels are explored (34/50/64 kt), producing six exposure columns per storm (final observed, max total fcast). Additionally, the observed IMERG q80 2-day rainfall over Cuba (from `fcast_obsv_combined_stats.parquet`, column `q80_obsv`) is retained as a secondary indicator.

3. **Trigger structure**: An OR trigger — storm triggers if (total exposure >= exposure threshold) OR (IMERG q80 >= rainfall threshold). The exposure threshold is set as the n-th largest total exposure across the 2002-2025 storm set for a target of n=10 triggering storms (RP ~2.6 yrs). The rainfall threshold is set deterministically as the (n - n_exp)-th largest q80 value among storms not already triggered by exposure, guaranteeing exactly n=10 total.

4. **Comparison**: The marimo app (`wsp_trigger.py`) also shows the "old option 1b" trigger (the pre-endorsed candidate from `threestage_trig_plot.md` using ZMA wind >= 120/105 kt AND CHIRPS-GEFS/IMERG q80) for comparison alongside the new exposure-based options. This is distinct from the final endorsed trigger.

5. **Lead-time analysis**: For the 64-kt exposure-only option, the app computes lead time as: earliest forecast issuance meeting the threshold minus the time of the largest jump in observed 64-kt exposure (proxy for storm arrival). This helps evaluate whether the new trigger provides adequate warning lead time.

# --- section: trigger-logic ---

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** NHC wind-buffer polygons (forecast and observed) intersected with GHSL 2025 population (3-arc-second) → population exposed at 34/50/64 kt; and NASA IMERG v7b 2-day q80 over Cuba (observational only).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** A trigger fires if, at any NHC forecast issuance during the storm's approach, the total population exposed to hurricane-force winds (forecast + cumulative observed) meets or exceeds an optimized threshold. As a backstop, a trigger also fires if the observed IMERG 2-day q80 rainfall reaches a separately calibrated threshold. Either condition alone is sufficient (OR logic). The exact wind-speed level (34/50/64 kt) and the thresholds are not yet determined.
- **Activation structure:** Two-window structure (forecast-weighted and observational fallback), analogous to the endorsed version but collapsing readiness and action into a single exposure-based forecast window. No three-stage structure is carried forward in the current design.
- **Calibration:** n=10 triggering storms over 2002-2025 (24 seasons), return period ~2.6 years. Maximizes CERF storm count, then Total Affected, then minimizes exposure threshold. This is a lower RP than the endorsed design (3.7 yrs). The choice of 2.6 yrs vs 3.7 yrs has NOT been finalized; the RP target itself remains open.
- **Authoritative source:** None — no published framework doc. The `code_ref` points to the exploration notebooks on the `2026-trigger` branch. The endorsed PDF for version 2025-08-26 remains authoritative for the live trigger.
- **Operated by:** OCHA CHD. The exploration app is deployed to `chd-ds-cub-trigger.azurewebsites.net` as an internal analysis tool.

# --- section: key-decisions ---

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Why shift from ZMA-wind to population exposure:** The endorsed trigger uses a binary ZMA-entry + wind-speed threshold, which does not distinguish a storm that barely clips the ZMA from one that makes a direct hit over a densely populated area. The exposure metric captures severity and spatial distribution in a single number. Hurricane Melissa (Oct 2025, the first real activation) demonstrated that a wind-only ZMA trigger fired correctly but did not naturally communicate population impact scale to decision-makers.
- **Why 64 kt as the primary candidate:** 64 kt (hurricane force) captures the most damaging wind levels and has better discrimination across the historical record than 34 kt or 50 kt. A 64-kt exposure-only option (no rainfall component) is also being explored as a simpler, more communicable trigger.
- **Why a lower RP target (2.6 yrs vs 3.7 yrs):** The 2002-2025 analysis window and addition of Melissa shift the calibration slightly. Whether to target the same RP as the endorsed version or accept a more frequent trigger is an open question for the design team and CERF.
- **Why OR logic (not AND):** The endorsed observational trigger requires wind AND rainfall simultaneously. The new design separates them: either exposure OR rainfall is sufficient. This makes the trigger more legible and ensures high-impact wind events trigger even if rainfall is modest.
- **Melissa impact data:** Melissa's EM-DAT entry is not yet finalized; the marimo app hardcodes 2.2M affected and USD 7.5M CERF+other as placeholders (source unclear from notebook).

# --- section: changes ---

## Changes from previous version

Relative to the endorsed 2025-08-26 version:

1. **Indicator shift — ZMA wind speed → population wind exposure.** The new design replaces the binary "storm enters ZMA at X kt" test with a continuous population-exposure metric. This requires new DB tables from `ds-storms-pipeline` (not present in the endorsed setup).
2. **Trigger structure — three-stage → two-window OR.** The readiness / anticipatory action / early response sequence is being simplified to a forecast-exposure trigger OR observed-rainfall fallback, without an explicit readiness stage.
3. **Return period — 3.7 yr → ~2.6 yr (candidate, not confirmed).** The new calibration window (2002-2025, n=10 storms) yields a lower RP. This is not yet agreed.
4. **Rainfall role — AND condition in observational → OR fallback.** Under the endorsed design, rainfall is required alongside wind for the observational trigger. In the new design, rainfall is a standalone fallback, not a joint condition.
5. **Melissa 2025 included in calibration.** The first real activation is included in the optimization dataset, which increases the number of calibration storms and slightly shifts threshold estimates.
6. **App deployment moved to standalone Azure app.** The monitoring visualization previously used the shared `chd-ds-seas5-viz` slot (`cub-app`); the new exploration app is deployed to `chd-ds-cub-trigger.azurewebsites.net`.

# --- section: open-questions ---

## Open questions / known issues

- **Which wind level?** The optimization explores 34/50/64 kt. No wind level has been selected. 64 kt is the leading candidate (hurricane force, simplest narrative), but 50 kt may cover more impactful storms.
- **Should rainfall be retained?** The 64-kt exposure-only option (no rainfall at all) is being evaluated. If retained, should rainfall be an OR fallback or a joint AND condition?
- **RP target: 2.6 or 3.7 years?** The new calibration yields ~2.6 yrs with n=10 storms; the endorsed design targeted 3.7 yrs. The CERF guidance may require alignment with the existing envelope.
- **Readiness stage?** The three-stage design (readiness / action / observational) provided early signaling. The new two-window design drops readiness; whether to add it back has not been decided.
- **Stale THRESHS constant:** `src/constants.py THRESHS['obsv']['p'] = 96.2` (should be 98.2 per endorsed PDF) is unresolved on this branch. Must be corrected before any monitoring pipeline update.
- **Melissa EM-DAT data:** The `Total Affected = 2,200,017` and `Amount in US$ = 7,500,016` for Melissa (hardcoded in wsp_trigger.py) should be validated against final EM-DAT and CERF records once available.
- **DB dependency for the new trigger:** The new design depends on `storms.nhc_tracks_fcast_exposure`, `storms.nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure`, and `storms.ibtracs_wind_exposure` in the dev DB. These are produced by `ds-storms-pipeline` (NHC WSP exposure tables). The KB page for the endorsed version flags a `<!-- TODO -->` on documenting this dependency; the new design makes it a hard requirement.
- **No endorsement pathway documented:** The branch does not contain a draft framework document or endorsement timeline. The design is in active exploration as of June 2026.
