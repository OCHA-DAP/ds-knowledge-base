# Cuba Tropical Cyclone — 2026-05-27 (development)

> **Status: In-development successor to the 2025-08-26 endorsed version; not yet endorsed, no published framework doc; trigger design from the 2026-trigger branch and subject to change.**
>
> The canonical trigger is the code at `code_ref`; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

An in-development redesign of Cuba's anticipatory action trigger for the 2026 hurricane season, explored on the `2026-trigger` branch following the experience of Hurricane Melissa (October 2025). The core proposal replaces the endorsed trigger's ZMA-wind-speed indicator with a **population wind-exposure** indicator (people inside NHC wind-buffer polygons at 34/50/64 kt thresholds, derived from GHSL population data), combined with an OR-logic against observed IMERG rainfall. Multiple wind levels and a rainfall-free 64-kt-only option are being evaluated. No thresholds have been finalised, no framework PDF has been published, and the operational monitoring code has not been updated. This page captures the design exploration as of branch commit `a5a80c0` (2026-05-27).

## Method

1. **Wind exposure data:** NHC wind-buffer polygons (34/50/64 kt radii) are intersected with the GHSL 2025 population raster for Cuba (admin_level=0). Two metrics are computed per storm and wind level:
   - **Final observed exposure** (`nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure`): population inside the observed wind swath at the last `valid_time`.
   - **Fcastonly exposure** (`nhc_tracks_fcastonly_exposure`): population in the *future-only* forecast cone at each NHC advisory issuance (the forecast buffer minus the already-swept area). This is then combined with cumulative observed exposure up to that issuance to give **total exposure** = fcastonly + cumulative observed, which does not double-count.

2. **Historical coverage:** IBTrACS wind-buffer polygons (`storms.ibtracs_wind_exposure`) provide exposure estimates for historical storms (2002–2025) not covered by NHC track files.

3. **Rainfall fallback:** IMERG observed 2-day q80 rainfall over Cuba (same metric as the endorsed version) is retained as an OR component, so a storm that does not meet the wind-exposure threshold can still trigger if rainfall is sufficient.

4. **Optimization:** For each wind level, the analysis sweeps all possible exposure thresholds. For each exposure threshold, the rainfall threshold is set deterministically as the (n − n_exp)-th largest q80 among storms not already triggered by exposure. The target is n=10 storms triggering over 2002–2025 (RP ≈ 2.6 yrs, 24 seasons). The best option per wind level maximises CERF storm count, then Total Affected, then uses the lowest exposure threshold.

5. **64-kt exposure-only option:** Also tested is a pure wind-exposure trigger with no rainfall component (64 kt only), which simplifies communication at the cost of removing the rainfall safety net.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** NHC wind-buffer population exposure (fcastonly + cumulative observed) and/or NASA IMERG 2-day q80 observed rainfall over Cuba.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** A storm triggers if either (a) the population exposed to hurricane-force winds (forecast + already observed) at the time of an NHC advisory exceeds a threshold, OR (b) the 80th spatial quantile of 2-day IMERG rainfall over Cuba exceeds a rainfall threshold. Option (b) only applies to storms not already triggered by (a). A pure wind-exposure option (no rainfall component) is also being evaluated. Exact threshold values are not finalised.
- **Activation structure:** OR logic — wind exposure OR observed rainfall. No readiness / action / observational window split is described in the dev notebooks; the optimization treats each storm as a binary trigger. The endorsed three-window structure may or may not be preserved in the final design.
- **Calibration:** Return-period optimization over all Atlantic-basin storms affecting Cuba, 2002–2025 (24 seasons), including Melissa 2025. Target n=10 storms (RP ≈ 2.6 yrs). Objective: maximise CERF storm count, then EM-DAT Total Affected. Melissa 2025 impact data hardcoded as placeholder values pending EM-DAT inclusion. Correlation analysis performed across wind levels and rainfall indicators to identify best discriminating indicator.
- **Authoritative source:** No published framework PDF exists. The trigger design is in `exploration/wsp_trigger.py` (Marimo app) on `source_branch: 2026-trigger`. The operational monitoring code (`pipelines/`, `src/constants.py`) still reflects the endorsed 2025-08-26 design.
- **Operated by:** Monitoring continues to be run by OCHA CHD (automated pipeline); the production monitoring system has not been updated to the new design.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Switch from ZMA wind speed to wind-exposure:** The endorsed trigger used NHC maximum wind speed within the ZMA geographic buffer as the sole forecast indicator. Hurricane Melissa's activation highlighted a desire to improve early warning specificity by distinguishing storms that threaten populated areas from those passing through uninhabited parts of the ZMA. Population wind-exposure (GHSL intersected with NHC wind buffers) directly captures the humanitarian risk signal.
- **OR logic (exposure OR rainfall):** Retaining IMERG rainfall as an OR fallback preserves the endorsed framework's empirical safety net (all historical observational activations coincided with forecast activations, but the fallback reduces risk of missing a storm with unusual forecast behaviour).
- **64-kt-only option:** A pure wind-exposure trigger at the 64-kt level removes the rainfall component entirely, simplifying the trigger and improving communicability. Its performance is comparable to the OR options at 64 kt.
- **Shift to RP ≈ 2.6 yr (n=10):** The development analysis uses a 2002–2025 window (24 seasons) including Melissa 2025, and targets n=10 storms. This is a lower RP than the endorsed 3.7-year target. No formal justification for the change is recorded in the repo — this is an open question for the design review.
- **Fcastonly exposure, not total forecast:** To avoid double-counting, the forecast component uses only the *future* portion of the NHC forecast cone (the cone area not yet swept by the observed track). The total trigger signal is fcastonly + cumulative observed, which represents total expected exposure without counting already-affected areas twice.

## Changes from previous version

The endorsed 2025-08-26 version uses:
- **Forecast trigger:** NHC maximum wind speed in ZMA ≥ 120 kt (two lead-time windows: readiness at 120 h, action at 72 h)
- **Observational trigger:** NHC observed wind in ZMA ≥ 105 kt AND IMERG 2-day q80 ≥ 98.2 mm

The proposed 2026 design replaces:
- **Forecast indicator:** ZMA wind speed → GHSL population exposed to NHC wind buffer (34/50/64 kt), forecast-only cone plus cumulative observed exposure
- **Trigger logic:** AND (wind AND rainfall for observational) → OR (wind exposure OR rainfall)
- **Return period target:** 3.7 yr (n=7 over 2000–2024) → ~2.6 yr (n=10 over 2002–2025)
- **Window structure:** three windows (readiness/action/early response) → not yet defined; the dev optimization is single-stage

The rainfall fallback (IMERG 2-day q80) is retained but demoted to an OR fallback rather than a required AND condition.

## Open questions / known issues

- **Which wind level to use:** Three options (34/50/64 kt) are explored; no preferred option has been selected. 64-kt performance is best for CERF capture; 34-kt catches more storms but may have higher false-alarm rate.
- **Rainfall component:** Whether to retain IMERG rainfall as an OR fallback or drop it entirely (64-kt-only option) is undecided.
- **Return period target:** The shift from 3.7 yr to 2.6 yr has not been formally justified or agreed with CERF/stakeholders.
- **Window structure:** How to map the new OR-logic trigger back onto the three-window (readiness/action/early response) structure of the endorsed framework — or whether to redesign the windows — is not settled.
- **Dev DB tables:** `storms.nhc_tracks_fcastonly_exposure`, `storms.nhc_tracks_obsv_exposure` are used by the app but their producers are not documented.
- **Melissa impact data:** Hardcoded placeholder values in `wsp_trigger.py` for Melissa 2025 (Total Affected=2,200,017; CERF=$7,500,016) need to be replaced with final EM-DAT validated figures before the optimization is finalised.
- **Stakeholder/INSMET review:** No evidence in the repo of stakeholder engagement on the new design. The endorsed framework involved INSMET and INRH in threshold selection — the same process would be needed before endorsement.
- **No runbook for operationalising the new trigger:** The endorsed trigger's monitoring pipeline would need to be rewritten to use wind-exposure tables rather than ZMA wind speed.
