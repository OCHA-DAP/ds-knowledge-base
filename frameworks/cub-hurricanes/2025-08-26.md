# Cuba Tropical Cyclone — 2025-08-26

## Summary

OCHA-facilitated anticipatory action framework for hurricanes in Cuba, endorsed by the Emergency Relief Coordinator on 27 August 2025. It pre-arranges USD 4 million in CERF pooled funding covering the 2025 and 2026 hurricane seasons (June–December each year) to protect up to 170,000 people. The trigger is a three-window mechanism: two forecast-based windows (Readiness at 120 h, Anticipatory Action at 72 h) using only NHC wind speed, plus one observational window (Early Response) combining NHC observed wind speed with NASA IMERG 2-day precipitation. CHD monitors the triggers automatically and notifies INSMET, OCHA, and the Resident Coordinator, who validates with Cuban national authorities before formally activating. Monitoring is conducted by OCHA CHD using an automated email pipeline.

## Method

1. **Data ingestion**: NHC forecast tracks (global, filtered to Atlantic basin) are pulled every 3–6 hours. NHC observed best-track data is pulled daily. NASA IMERG v7b daily raster is downloaded for rainfall observation.
2. **Spatial filtering**: Storm track points are interpolated to 30-minute intervals and tested for intersection with INSMET's Zona de Máxima Atención (ZMA) — a buffer of roughly 230 km around Cuba. Only ZMA-intersecting track segments trigger evaluation.
3. **Forecast triggers**: For each NHC forecast issuance, maximum wind speed is extracted from all ZMA-intersecting forecast points within the lead-time window. If that maximum meets the wind threshold, the trigger fires.
4. **Observational trigger**: For storms that pass through the ZMA at sufficient wind speed, the 80th spatial quantile of the 2-day rolling-sum IMERG rainfall over Cuba is computed for the storm period. Both conditions (wind AND rainfall) must be met simultaneously.
5. **Notification**: When a trigger fires, CHD sends a provisional activation email to INSMET, OCHA, and the Resident Coordinator. The RC jointly validates with MINCEX and EMNDC before confirming activation.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** NHC official forecast/observed track (wind speed) and NASA IMERG v7b daily raster (rainfall, observational window only)
- **Decision rule (plain language):** A storm must be forecast (or observed) to enter Cuba's ZMA at or above a wind speed threshold. For the two forecast windows the thresholds are identical (120 kt) but the time horizon differs. For the observational window the wind threshold is lower (105 kt) but a simultaneous rainfall condition must also be met: at least 98.2 mm 2-day precipitation must be observed over 20% of the country (operationalised as the 80th spatial quantile of the IMERG pixel distribution).
- **Activation structure:**
  - Readiness → Anticipatory Action → Early Response (sequential in design, but windows can fire independently)
  - Early Response only activates if the Anticipatory Action window did NOT previously fire
  - RC retains a discretionary override for atypical cases where the trigger fires but impact is clearly minimal
- **Calibration:** Optimised over all storms that entered the ZMA in 2000–2024 (Atlantic basin, IBTrACS). Objective: maximise total EM-DAT "Total Affected" and CERF allocations for triggered storms. Constraint: exactly 7 triggered years (≈ 1-in-3.7-year return period), matching CERF guidance. 3,897,600 threshold combinations tested; after RP and anti-overfitting filters, the final combination was selected in consultation with INSMET and INRH.
- **Authoritative source:** The endorsed PDF at `framework_doc` is the authoritative trigger specification. The repo (`code_ref`) implements and monitors it.
- **Operated by:** Monitoring is run by OCHA CHD (automated pipeline); the live trigger is in this repo, not an external agency system.

## Trigger windows

| window | basis | indicator | threshold | lead time | return period | releases |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Readiness | forecast | NHC forecast max wind in ZMA | ≥ 120 kt (Cat. 4) | up to 120 h (5 days) | ~3.7 yr (shared) | Readiness activities; CERF sends approval letters for readiness % of budget |
| Anticipatory Action | forecast | NHC forecast max wind in ZMA | ≥ 120 kt (Cat. 4) | up to 72 h (3 days) | ~3.7 yr (shared) | Full anticipatory action activities; 100% CERF budget released |
| Early Response | observational | NHC observed wind in ZMA AND IMERG 2-day precip (q80 Cuba) | ≥ 105 kt (Cat. 3) AND ≥ 98.2 mm | post-landfall | ~3.7 yr (shared) | Early response activities; 100% CERF budget released; fires only if Anticipatory Action did not previously fire |

**Note on historical performance (PDF Annex 1):** Historically (2000–2024), all observational trigger activations also had forecast trigger activations. The observational window is thus empirically redundant but retained as a safety net for rapid-intensification events where forecast triggers may be missed.

**Note on Readiness:** The readiness window uses the same wind threshold as the Anticipatory Action window; it triggers at a longer lead time but historically would not have captured additional events. A readiness false alarm (120 h trigger that does not progress to 72 h) is theoretically possible.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **ZMA as geographic scope:** INSMET's pre-existing Zona de Máxima Atención (~230 km buffer around Cuba) was used as the spatial trigger boundary, leveraging Cuban institutional definitions and enabling alignment with government protocols.
- **Wind speed only for forecast triggers:** Rainfall forecasts (CHIRPS-GEFS) were explored but excluded from the forecast trigger because all historical hurricanes meeting the ZMA + wind criterion would have reached the minimum rainfall threshold — making the rainfall parameter uninformative for forecasts. The observational trigger retains rainfall because observed (IMERG) data is more reliable than forecast rainfall, and the rainfall condition adds specificity.
- **120 kt (Cat. 4) for forecast windows:** Chosen via optimization to meet the 3.7-year return period while maximising EM-DAT affected population and CERF allocations. Both readiness and anticipatory action use the same wind threshold; only the lead time differs.
- **105 kt (Cat. 3) for observational window:** Lower threshold than forecast windows because the observation is certain and the combined rainfall condition provides the discriminating specificity. Historically all observational activations overlapped with forecast activations (empirically redundant), but retained as a safety net.
- **80th spatial quantile for rainfall:** Optimisation showed that the 80th percentile (20% of pixels exceeding the threshold) best captured high-impact storms while meeting the RP constraint. This corresponds to "at least 20% of the country" receiving ≥ 98.2 mm over 2 days.
- **2-day rainfall window:** 2-day sums were selected (over 1-day or total event) to ensure rainfall peaks occurring around midnight are fully captured.
- **Observational trigger fires only if action trigger did not:** Design decision to prevent double-funding; the early response is a backstop for events that bypassed the forecast trigger.
- **Return period target 3.7 years (7 years in 2000–2024):** Aligned with CERF guidance. Multiple-activations in a year count as a single event in return-period calculations.
