# Niger Flood — 2025-11-04

## Summary

Niger's collective anticipatory action framework targets severe riverine flooding on the Niger River, triggering pre-arranged CERF funds when observed water levels at the ABN Niamey gauge station exceed defined thresholds. The 2025 version (approved 4 November 2025) supersedes the 2024 pilot and introduces a three-tier trigger structure: a buffer alert at 530 cm (no funds, agencies prepare), a mobilisation threshold at 592 cm (~3-year return period, partial funds), and a full-action threshold at 604 cm (~5.1-year return period, $5 million released). The framework covers 13 communes in the Dosso and Tillabéri regions, targeting 109,156 vulnerable people, and can activate once per year in either the "crue locale" (Jul-Sep) or the "crue guinéenne" (Nov-Feb) season. The framework confirmed its first real activation in November 2024 under the 2024 pilot design (threshold 580 cm, guinean flood season).

## Method

The Centre for Humanitarian Data (CHD) at OCHA and OCHA Niger jointly monitor the ABN SATH website daily for the Niger River water level at Niamey. The ABN (Autorité du Bassin du Niger) is a nine-country intergovernmental body headquartered in Niamey that publishes real-time hydrology readings. When the observed level crosses a threshold, OCHA Niger triggers the CERF disbursement chain and alerts agencies within hours. No forecast model is involved in the endorsed trigger — it is purely observational. The repo contains additional GloFAS-based analysis for upstream Garbe-Kourou that was explored as a potential early-warning indicator but has not been endorsed.

Historical calibration uses river discharge from GRDC stations (Niamey GRDC ID 1234150, Garbe-Kourou GRDC ID 1234130), converted to water levels via a fitted rating curve (quadratic polynomial coefficients in `src/utils/rating_curve.py`). Return periods are calculated from peak annual levels split by flood phase (local vs Guinean). The 2025 PDF notes that historical years are identified from discharge data converted to levels using the current rating curve, which may differ slightly from the ABN raw gauge records used in the 2024 analysis.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** ABN-observed water level (cm) at Niamey gauge station, read from SATH-ABN website (http://www.sath.abn.ne/Hydrology_FR.html)
- **Decision rule:** If the level exceeds 592 cm during the active flood season, CERF releases mobilisation funds and agencies begin preparedness. If it subsequently exceeds 604 cm, the full $5M is released and all anticipatory activities begin immediately. The framework can fire only once per year, in whichever flood season (local or Guinean) first crosses the threshold.
- **Activation structure:** Three tiers — (1) 530 cm yellow buffer: OCHA alerts agencies to prepare, no funds; (2) 592 cm mobilisation: partial CERF funds for preparedness activities (~3.0-year return period); (3) 604 cm action: full CERF envelope released, all activities execute within 7 days (~5.1-year return period).
- **Calibration:** Return periods computed from 1979–2024 historical data (46 flood seasons), split by flood phase. At 604 cm the overall return period is ~5.1 years (9 activations in 46 seasons). The 2025 threshold was raised from the 2024 pilot level of 580 cm (~2.7-year RP) in part because a levee (digue) was constructed near Niamey that reduced flood risk at lower water levels.
- **Authoritative source:** 2025 framework PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo (`code_ref`) contains the calibration analysis and monitoring notebooks.
- **Operated by:** OCHA Niger + CHD (not an external platform — no IRI Maproom or equivalent).

Note: The two rows represent the mobilisation (592 cm, partial funds) and action (604 cm, full funds) tiers within the same observational mechanism — not geographically or temporally distinct windows. The 530 cm buffer is a pre-alert, not a funding trigger, so it is not a window row (see `extra.schema_strain`).

## Monitoring

CHD and OCHA Niger monitor the ABN SATH website daily during flood seasons (approximately July–February). The `src/datasources/abn.py` module loads ABN monitoring data from blob storage (manually recorded from SATH-ABN into a Google Sheet/CSV). The `exploration/2025/activation_plot_2025.md` notebook tracks current-season water levels against historical context. No automated pipeline: monitoring is manual, with CHD as the trigger certifier.

## Historical activations

**2026 activation (first under this endorsed version):** The framework activated in **January 2026** during the crue guinéenne (Guinean flood) window — the first activation of the revised 2025-11-04 framework. **No public communications were issued**, so there is no ReliefWeb/CERF announcement to cite; it is recorded here from internal/team knowledge.

**2024 activation (pilot framework):** The framework activated on or around 28 November 2024 during the crue guinéenne (Guinean flood) season, per the repository README. This was under the 2024 pilot framework (single threshold at 580 cm) and predates the current version. The 2025 framework document confirms that 2024 was the only year in the historical record (1979–2024) where the action threshold (604 cm) was reached for BOTH local and Guinean floods, suggesting the Guinean flood in January 2025 (winter 2024/2025 season) also exceeded 604 cm. CERF released $5M for the activation.

The 2024 pilot PDF annex lists all backtest trigger dates since 2005 at 580 cm: local floods — 19 Aug 2012, 30 Aug 2013, 13 Sep 2016, 7 Sep 2017, 25 Aug 2018, 27 Aug 2019, 8 Aug 2020; Guinean floods — 3 Jan 2019 (2018 season), 12 Jan 2021 (2020 season). These are simulated, not real activations.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Why observational (not forecast)?** The 7-day window between threshold crossing at Niamey and peak flood at downstream communes (the 13 targeted communes per Tableau 3: Namaro, Karma, Sambera, Tillabéri, Say, Tanda, Gaya, Bitinkodji, Kourtheye, Kollo, N'Dounga, Dessa, Dargol — matching `ADM3_AOI_PCODES_2025` in `src/constants.py`) is sufficient for the anticipatory activities selected. Note: Tounouga and Liboré were 2024-pilot communes and are NOT in the 2025 list. GloFAS or upstream gauge forecasts were explored (sirba-forecast branch) but not adopted because the operational benefit did not justify the additional complexity and the GRDC/ABN system is already trusted by government partners.

**Why raise the threshold from 580 to 604 cm?** The 2025 PDF explicitly cites a dike (digue) constructed near Niamey since the 2024 pilot that reduces flood exposure at lower water levels. This made the 580 cm threshold too conservative post-construction. The new 604 cm level remains in the orange alert band.

**Why a mobilisation tier (592 cm)?** Learning from the 2024 activation: agencies needed prep time before the full action trigger fired. The 592 cm mobilisation tier releases a portion of funds to allow field positioning and targeting before the 604 cm threshold.

**Why reduce from 8 to 5 agencies?** The 2025 framework consolidated from 8 agencies (2024 pilot) to 5 (FAO, IOM, WFP, UNICEF, WHO) to maximise impact per dollar and retain only activities feasible within 7 days.

**Why expand from 6 to 13 communes?** The 2024 pilot was conservative on geography. Post-activation learning and multi-criteria analysis of flood impact, vulnerability, and access enabled expansion, with the 2025 communes chosen using a weighted multi-criteria scoring (flood impact, operational access, security, food insecurity vulnerability).

## Changes from previous version

Supersedes the 2024 pilot (approved 2024-10-24, `framework_doc` at https://www.unocha.org/attachments/7cfb4890-2eca-4984-8bd8-34c15fabe3d9/Niger%20Floods%20AA%20Framework%20%28FINAL%20-%2015.11.2024%29.pdf).

Key changes:
1. **Trigger raised:** Single threshold 580 cm → three-tier system (530 cm buffer / 592 cm mobilisation / 604 cm action). The action threshold increased from ~2.7-year to ~5.1-year return period.
2. **New mobilisation tier:** Pre-action CERF disbursement introduced to fund agency preparedness before the full action threshold fires.
3. **Agencies reduced:** 8 UN agencies (FAO, IOM, WFP, UNICEF, WHO, UNFPA, UNHCR, UNDP) → 5 (FAO, IOM, WFP, UNICEF, WHO). Protection mainstreamed as cross-cutting rather than standalone.
4. **Geographic expansion:** 6 communes → 13 communes; target population 455,080 (2024) → 109,156 vulnerable people within 308,219 total (2025, using refined vulnerability estimates).
5. **Historical data extended:** Calibration now uses 1979–2024 (46 seasons, GRDC-derived) vs 2005–2024 (ABN raw data). Historical thresholds re-derived using rating curve to convert discharge to level.
6. **Rationale for threshold change:** New levee constructed near Niamey reduces flood risk at lower levels; learning from 2024 activation.

- The framework note indicates CERF funding is contingent on the Niger drought framework's first window not having activated. The interaction logic between the two frameworks (flood and drought) should be verified each season.
- Sirba river analysis (sirba_selection.md in repo) identifies the Sirba as a major tributary influencing flooding downstream — potential for a future separate monitoring station or composite trigger.
