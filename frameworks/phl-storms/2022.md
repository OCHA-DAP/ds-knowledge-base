# Philippines Tropical Cyclone — 2022

> No code repo covers this version (`source_repo: null`) — reconstructed entirely from the public framework document and OCHA/ReliefWeb/Anticipation Hub reporting. This page is a sourced institutional summary, not a derivation from code.

## Summary

The Philippines anticipatory action (AA) framework for tropical cyclones was one of CERF's earliest AA pilots, endorsed in 2021 and revised in October 2022 to extend geographic coverage. This 2022 Revision pre-arranged US$7.5 million from CERF to deliver unconditional multi-purpose cash and complementary livelihood/protection assistance ahead of a severe typhoon, restructuring the design into two geographic scenarios — Scenario 1 (Region 5 Bicol + Samar Island provinces of Region 8) and Scenario 2 (Leyte Island provinces of Region 8 + Region 13 Caraga) — to cover a wider set of the country's most typhoon-exposed areas than the original 2021 pilot (which covered only Regions 5 and 8). Implementing agencies were FAO, IOM, UNFPA, UNICEF and WFP, with OCHA coordinating. The framework was never activated under this version; it was later revised again (2023 Revision, then 2024-09-13, then the current 2025-10-03 endorsed version — see that page for the framework's first confirmed activation, in November 2025).

## Method

Once a tropical cyclone forms with the potential to reach category 4 or higher (>200 km/h maximum 1-minute sustained wind speed) and is tracking toward Region 5, 8, or 13, the readiness trigger fires 4–7 days before forecast landfall as a pre-activation notice. As the storm approaches, the Netherlands Red Cross (NLRC) 510 Initiative runs a probabilistic impact model — using ECMWF track/wind ensembles — to estimate the number of houses that will be totally damaged, updated every 6–12 hours. At or before the 72-hour mark before forecast landfall, if the model's output falls within an agreed probability×damage matrix, the action trigger fires and CERF funds are released for the scenario area under threat.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** ECMWF tropical-cyclone forecast tracks/wind speed (readiness); NLRC 510 probabilistic impact model output for houses totally damaged (action).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** If a forecast tropical cyclone can reach Category 4 or higher (>200 km/h maximum 1-minute sustained wind speed) and is projected to hit Region 5, 8, or 13, a readiness notice goes out 4–7 days ahead. At or before 72 hours before landfall, if the 510 model's predicted houses-totally-damaged estimate falls within the agreed probability matrix (ranging from 50% probability of ≥80,000 houses to 95% probability of ≥5,000 houses), the action trigger fires and CERF releases pre-arranged funds for unconditional multi-purpose cash and complementary assistance in the affected scenario area.
- **Activation structure:** Two-stage — readiness (pre-activation, no funds released) then action (funds released) — applied per scenario (Scenario 1: Region 5 + Samar/R8; Scenario 2: Leyte/R8 + Region 13).
- **Calibration:** Sources describe a single probability×damage matrix (50%@80,000 houses ... 95%@5,000 houses) without stating an explicit return period in the document itself; the same matrix values persisted through the 2023–2024 revisions (see discrepancies/gap note — could not confirm from primary text whether return-period analysis underpinned this specific 2022 calibration).
- **Authoritative source:** "Philippines: Anticipatory Action Framework, 2022 Revision" (`framework_doc`, ReliefWeb/OCHA, dated 2022-10-13). No code repo implements or derives this version.
- **Operated by:** OCHA Philippines with OCHA CHD support; the NLRC 510 Initiative (Netherlands Red Cross) runs the impact model. No production system in a DS repo for this version.

## Monitoring

OCHA Philippines and OCHA CHD monitored ECMWF forecasts once a tropical cyclone formed, with the NLRC 510 Initiative running the probabilistic impact model every 6–12 hours as a storm approached. No standalone monitoring pipeline or app exists for this version.

## Historical activations

**Never activated under this version.** The framework had a "near activation" in December 2021 (Typhoon Rai/Odette) that predates this 2022 Revision and was monitored but did not trigger; a 31 March 2022 After Action Review examined that near-miss and fed into this revision's design changes. The Philippine Red Cross separately activated its own Early Action Protocol (EAP) for Typhoons ahead of Tropical Storm Nalgae/Paeng in October 2022 — that is an IFRC-run framework, not this OCHA/CERF one, and is not recorded as an activation here. This OCHA/CERF framework's first confirmed activation occurred years later, in November 2025, under the 2025-10-03 version (see that page).

## Key decisions & rationale

**Extension to Region 13 and the Scenario 1/2 split:** The original 2021 pilot covered only Regions 5 and 8. To "extend the geographic coverage of the pilot," the 2022 Revision added Region 13 (Caraga) and split the design into two geographically distinct scenarios — Scenario 1 (Region 5 + Samar Island provinces of Region 8) and Scenario 2 (Leyte Island provinces of Region 8 + Region 13) — so that a single fixed CERF envelope could still be targeted precisely regardless of which part of the exposed area a given typhoon threatened.

**Learning-pilot framing:** As an early CERF AA pilot, the framework's design explicitly built in an after-action review cycle (31 March 2022) to revise the forecasting model/triggers, delivery model, cash interventions, and cross-cutting issues based on the 2021 season's near-miss experience — a deliberate "no regrets" iterative approach rather than a one-off fixed design.

## Changes from previous version

The 2022 Revision (supersedes the original 2021-09-15 pilot doc) introduced:

1. **Region 13 (Caraga) added** to the framework's geographic scope, alongside the original Regions 5 and 8.
2. **Scenario 1 / Scenario 2 structure introduced** — replacing a single national design with two geographically distinct activation scenarios, each with its own household/people targets (62,000 HH / 312,000 people vs. 37,000 HH / 187,000 people).
3. **Target population roughly doubled** in design terms — from 270,500 people (Regions 5+8 only, 2021) to a combined ceiling of 499,000 across the two new scenarios (only one of which activates per event).

This structure (Scenario-based geography, not yet region-disaggregated) persisted through the 2023 Revision and into the 2024-09-13 version, before being replaced by full region-level disaggregation in the 2025-10-03 version (see that page's "Changes from previous version").
