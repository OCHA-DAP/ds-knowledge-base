# Philippines Tropical Cyclone — 2023

> The canonical trigger is the 2023 Revision framework doc (`framework_doc`); the calibration analysis lives in the source repo (`code_ref`). This page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

The Philippines CERF Anticipatory Action (AA) Framework, 2023 Revision (published 2023-10-03), pre-arranges a US$7.5 million CERF envelope to fund collective, multi-agency anticipatory action ahead of severe tropical cyclones threatening Region V (Bicol), Region VIII (Eastern Visayas), and/or Region XIII (Caraga). Activation is a **two-stage** trigger. A **readiness** stage fires 4–7 days before forecast landfall when an ECMWF forecast shows a cyclone with the potential to reach Category 3+ (>178 km/h 1-minute / 158 km/h 10-minute maximum sustained wind) projected to directly impact the pilot regions — this begins preparatory measures but releases no funds. An **action** stage fires on or before 72 hours before landfall when the IFRC/Netherlands Red Cross 510 Global probabilistic impact model (applied to ECMWF forecast tracks) predicts houses totally damaged within a band running from a 50% probability of 80,000 houses to a 95% probability of at least 5,000 houses — this releases the CERF envelope. Depending on which regions the storm threatens, one of two mutually-exclusive geographic scenarios activates: Scenario 1 (Region V + Samar-island Region VIII provinces, ~378,000 people) or Scenario 2 (Leyte-island Region VIII provinces + Region XIII/Caraga, ~179,000 people). FAO, IOM, UNFPA, UNICEF, and WFP deliver the pre-agreed actions, roughly two-thirds as unconditional multi-purpose cash. This version supersedes the October 2022 revision and was itself superseded by the "Anticipatory Action Framework Philippines 2024" (published 2024-09-13). Note: this version has **no observational trigger** — that pathway was added in the 2025 revision.

## Method

OCHA Philippines, working with HDX, monitors ECMWF forecasts for tropical cyclones threatening the pilot regions (Regions 5, 8, 13). The framework activates in two stages. First, a **readiness** stage (pre-activation) fires 4–7 days before forecast landfall when a cyclone is forecast with the potential to reach Category 3 or higher (>178 km/h 1-minute / 158 km/h 10-minute maximum sustained wind) and is projected to directly impact Region 5, 8 and/or 13 — this triggers preparatory/pre-positioning measures but does not release funds. As soon as ECMWF forecasts are available for a candidate storm, the 510 Global probabilistic impact model (developed by the Netherlands Red Cross/IFRC 510 initiative for the Philippines) is applied to the ensemble of forecast tracks and wind speeds to estimate the number of houses that will be totally damaged, producing an impact map updated every 6–12 hours as the forecast evolves. Second, the **action** stage fires on or before 72 hours before forecast landfall if the modeled damage estimate crosses the threshold band (see Trigger logic): the CERF anticipatory-action trigger fires for whichever geographic scenario the storm threatens, and the pre-agreed disbursement to FAO/IOM/UNFPA/UNICEF/WFP proceeds so that cash and in-kind assistance reach households before landfall.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** (readiness) tropical-cyclone category / maximum sustained wind forecast (ECMWF); (action) ECMWF forecast tracks run through the 510 Global probabilistic impact model to estimate houses totally damaged. Both stages are forecast-based and gated on projected landfall in Region V, VIII, and/or XIII.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** *Readiness* — 4–7 days before a cyclone's forecast landfall, if the forecast shows the storm can reach Category 3+ (>178 km/h 1-minute / 158 km/h 10-minute maximum sustained wind) and is projected to directly impact the pilot regions, pre-activation begins (preparatory measures, no funds). *Action* — on or before 72 hours before forecast landfall, if the 510 Global model's damage estimate falls within the band from a 50% probability that 80,000 houses will be totally damaged to a 95% probability that at least 5,000 houses will be totally damaged, and the storm is projected to directly impact the pilot regions, the action trigger fires for the affected scenario (Scenario 1 or 2) and the pre-arranged CERF envelope is released to the five implementing agencies.
- **Activation structure:** Two sequential stages — readiness (4–7 days out, pre-activation) then action (≤72h out, funds released). The geographic scenario (1 or 2) that activates depends on which set of regions the storm threatens, not on a separate stage. **No observational trigger** in this version (that pathway was added in the 2025 revision).
- **Calibration:** A modeled-impact probability band (not a plain historical return period) — the 50%/80,000-house and 95%/5,000-house bounds come from the 510 Global model's damage-function calibration for the Philippines. Public sources for this version do not state an underlying return period; the source repo's 2024 trigger review carries the same "current" action-matrix endpoints (80k houses @50% … 5k houses @95%).
- **Authoritative source:** the 2023 Revision PDF (`framework_doc`). The calibration analysis (return periods, thresholds) lives in the source repo (`code_ref`), whose HEAD reflects the later 2024/2025 revision.
- **Operated by:** OCHA Philippines, together with HDX, runs the forecast-to-impact-map modeling; no external platform (IRI, INSIVUMEH, etc.) operates this trigger.

## Trigger windows

**n_windows = 2** — a readiness stage and an action stage. The two geographic "scenarios" (which regions the storm threatens) are a spatial split of the SAME action-stage disbursement, not a separate window/stage — see the scenario detail in `extra` and in Method above.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Impact-based (not just hazard-based) trigger:** Using the 510 Global model's probabilistic house-damage estimate for the action stage, rather than wind speed/category alone, ties the funding release to expected humanitarian impact rather than storm intensity in isolation — consistent with the broader shift in AA design toward impact-based forecasting.

**Two-stage readiness → action design:** A readiness stage (4–7 days out, keyed off the coarser category/wind forecast) buys lead time to pre-position without committing funds, while the action stage (≤72h, keyed off the 510 damage estimate) commits the CERF envelope only once the forecast impact is firm — separating "get ready" from "spend the money".

**Two geographic scenarios instead of a single fixed target area:** Splitting delivery into Scenario 1 (Region V + Samar-island Region VIII provinces) vs. Scenario 2 (Leyte-island Region VIII provinces + Region XIII/Caraga) lets the same pre-arranged CERF envelope follow whichever set of regions the specific storm actually threatens, rather than committing to one fixed geography regardless of the storm's track. (The 2025 revision later abandoned this sub-region scenario logic in favour of full per-region thresholds — see the 2025 page.)

**Heavy reliance on cash:** Roughly 64–65% of the overall pilot budget is delivered as cash (multipurpose transfers plus sectoral top-ups, e.g. UNICEF's top-up to the government's 4Ps social protection program), reflecting a preference for flexible, dignity-preserving assistance ahead of landfall.
