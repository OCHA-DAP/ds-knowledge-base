# Philippines Tropical Cyclone — 2025-10-03

## Summary

This framework triggers CERF anticipatory action (up to USD 6M + USD 4M from partners) ahead of destructive typhoons threatening Region 5 (Bicol), Region 8 (Eastern Visayas), or Region 13 (Caraga). Three trigger types exist — readiness, action, and observational — structured so that both readiness and action must fire for full pre-landfall AA delivery; the observational trigger is a fallback if the forecast-based windows are not met. The 510 probabilistic damage model (houses totally damaged, run every 6-12 hours on ECMWF forecasts) is the core decision instrument for the action trigger. One region at a time is activated.

## Method

OCHA Philippines monitors TC formation beginning 6-10 days before forecast landfall. When a typhoon is projected to reach ≥177 km/h (10-min sustained) or ≥200 km/h (1-min sustained) wind speed AND is projected to directly impact Region 5, 8, or 13, the readiness trigger is assessed. OCHA CHD runs the Netherlands Red Cross 510 probabilistic impact model on ECMWF forecast tracks every 6-12 hours, producing region-level probability distributions over totally damaged houses. At the 72-hour cut-off, predicted damage is compared against per-region threshold matrices (varying by probability level). If the threshold is met for a region, the action trigger fires. If no forecast-based trigger fires, the observational trigger can be activated up to landfall if PAGASA reports a super typhoon (≥185 km/h 10-min at landfall) affecting ≥50% of the target region.

The 510 model is deterministic-to-probabilistic: it ingests ECMWF ensemble forecast tracks plus historical impact data (relationship between wind speed, rainfall, and houses totally damaged) to produce a probability distribution over damage scenarios by region.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** 510 model impact estimates (houses totally damaged, probability-weighted) from ECMWF forecast tracks; PAGASA/ECMWF wind speed forecasts for readiness; PAGASA observed wind speed for observational.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** 3-7 days before landfall, OCHA checks whether a TC is forecast to hit one of the three regions at ≥177 km/h (10-min). If yes, that region is activated for readiness. At 72 hours before landfall, the 510 model estimate is checked against the per-region matrix: if the predicted damage at any probability level meets or exceeds the threshold for that probability, the action trigger fires for that region. If these thresholds are not met and a super typhoon (≥185 km/h 10-min) is approaching with ≥50% of the region in its damaging wind field, the observational trigger may activate from 0 to 72 hours before landfall.
- **Activation structure:** Three sequential/fallback stages. Readiness + Action can co-activate simultaneously at the 72-hour cutoff. Action can also fire without prior readiness (direct activation at ≤72h). Observational is fallback only (no prior readiness/action activation required). Only one region activates at a time; if two regions meet thresholds simultaneously, the region with higher predicted house damage activates.
- **Calibration:** Return periods individually: readiness RP ~5.7 years, action RP ~3.4 years, observational RP ~4.5 years. Overall framework RP stated as ~3.5 years. Thresholds calibrated against regional damage vulnerability differences (Region 5, 8, 13 have different damage profiles, hence separate matrices).
- **Authoritative source:** Framework PDF (October 2025). The repo implements the 2021-2022 era calibration using a combined scenario design; the 2025 per-region thresholds are not yet implemented in code.
- **Operated by:** OCHA Philippines and OCHA CHD jointly run the live trigger monitoring and model runs. This repo contains analysis/calibration code, not the production monitoring pipeline.

## Monitoring

OCHA Philippines monitors TC formation 6-10 days before forecast landfall. Once a storm is identified as potentially meeting readiness criteria, OCHA CHD runs the 510 model every 6-12 hours on ECMWF forecast tracks, producing impact estimates and probability maps. Model runs continue from 3-5 days until the 72-hour decision cut-off. OCHA Philippines participates in government Pre-Disaster Risk Assessment (PDRA) meetings convened by NDRRMC. Impact maps are distributed to CERF AA agencies by email.

Data sources used in monitoring: PAGASA forecasts, ECMWF ensemble forecasts, GEFS, 510 model (NL Red Cross). For the observational trigger: PAGASA official wind speed classification.

The `src/datasources/imerg.py` module in the repo shows IBTrACS and IMERG ingestion pipelines (via `ocha-stratus`), used for post-event and calibration analysis rather than operational monitoring.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Shift from scenarios to per-region activation (2025):** The 2023-2024 framework grouped Regions 5+8 as Scenario 1 and Regions 8+13 as Scenario 2. The 2025 framework disaggregates to individual regions, motivated by the finding that the three regions have different vulnerability profiles warranting separate thresholds.
- **Only one region at a time:** If multiple regions simultaneously meet the action threshold, only the highest-damage region activates. This constrains fund deployment within the USD 6M CERF envelope.
- **Observational trigger introduced:** The 2024 after-action review identified that rapid intensification (RI) causes forecast-based triggers to miss high-impact events (all historical missed activations in the performance report were attributable to RI underestimates). The observational trigger acts as a safety net for events that intensify faster than ECMWF predicts.
- **510 model chosen:** After 2021 review of multiple models used by organizations in PHL (IFRC/510, others), the 510 probabilistic impact model was selected. It was further revamped from deterministic to probabilistic using ECMWF ensemble data.
- **Stronger TC focus:** 2025 framework targets only stronger TCs (≥177 km/h 10-min wind) to focus resources on highest-impact events; this narrows the activation pool compared to a lower wind threshold.
- **Wind threshold in PAGASA convention:** The 10-minute sustained wind convention (177/185 km/h) aligns with PAGASA's operational reporting. The 200 km/h 1-min equivalent is provided for international reference (WMO convention).

## Changes from previous version

The PDF states the 2025 framework replaces the 2023-2024 design. Key documented changes:

1. **Scenario grouping eliminated:** Region-pair scenarios replaced by individual-region activation with separate calibrated thresholds.
2. **Observational trigger added:** New fallback mechanism for super typhoons that intensify rapidly after the 72-hour forecast window.
3. **Trigger refinement:** Regional thresholds recalibrated to reflect per-region vulnerability differences; overall framework RP stated as 3.5 years.
4. **Validity extended:** 2025-2026 framework covers until January 2027 (two typhoon seasons), with Jan coverage to catch anomalous late-season TCs.
5. **Legislation milestone:** Philippine RA 12287 (State of Imminent Disaster Act) enacted September 2025; future iterations to align with implementing rules.
