# Philippines Tropical Cyclone — 2025-10-03

> The canonical trigger is the 510 probabilistic model run by OCHA CHD / OCHA Philippines; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

The OCHA Philippines anticipatory action framework for tropical cyclones pre-arranges CERF funding to deliver multi-sectoral cash and in-kind assistance to vulnerable households in Regions 5 (Bicol), 8 (Eastern Visayas), and 13 (Caraga) before a destructive typhoon makes landfall. Activation requires one of three triggers: a readiness trigger (wind forecast ≥177 km/h, 3–7 days before landfall), an action trigger (NLRC 510 model predicting houses totally damaged across a per-region probability matrix, ≤72 hours before landfall), or an observational trigger (PAGASA confirms super typhoon ≥185 km/h at or within 72 hours of landfall). The 2025 version endorses US$6 million from CERF, complemented by US$4 million from other partners. This 2025 framework was activated in November 2025 for Super Typhoon Fung-Wong/Uwan via the observational trigger (~US$5.95M released to five UN agencies) — see Historical activations.

## Method

OCHA Philippines, supported by OCHA CHD, monitors ECMWF tropical cyclone forecasts 6–10 days before projected landfall. Once a TC enters a potentially impactful track, CHD runs the NLRC 510 probabilistic impact model (which predicts the number of totally damaged houses using ECMWF track ensembles, rainfall, and wind speed) every 6–12 hours. The results are compared against a per-region probability threshold matrix. PAGASA reports are also monitored directly. Three activation pathways can fire independently or simultaneously:

1. **Readiness** fires if a forecast shows the TC can reach ≥177 km/h 10-min sustained wind speed and is projected to impact Region 5, 8, or 13 — this is a pre-activation notice, not yet full AA release.
2. **Action** fires at or before the 72-hour cutoff if the 510 model output at that snapshot meets the regional threshold matrix — this releases funds.
3. **Observational** fires if the forecast and action triggers were not met but the storm subsequently intensifies to super typhoon (≥185 km/h, 10-min PAGASA) at or up to 72 hours before landfall, with ≥50% of the target region within damaging winds.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** ECMWF ensemble TC tracks + NLRC 510 probabilistic impact model (houses totally damaged); PAGASA sustained wind speed reports (observational pathway).
- **Decision rule:** At the 72-hour cutoff before forecast landfall, if the 510 model predicts a number of totally damaged houses that falls within the regional threshold matrix (e.g. 50% probability of ≥25,000 for Region 5, OR 80% probability of ≥6,000, etc.) the action trigger fires and the RC activates AA for that region. If two regions hit thresholds simultaneously, the one with the higher damage estimate is activated; only one region activates per event.
- **Activation structure:** Three sequential/parallel pathways — readiness (pre-activation, no funds released), action (funds released), observational (fallback for intensifying storms the forecast missed). Readiness and action can co-fire at the 72-hour mark.
- **Calibration:** Per-region thresholds calibrated to a return period of >3 years per region, with an overall cross-trigger return period of 1 in 3.5 years. Readiness alone: 1 in 5.7 years. Action: 1 in 3.4 years. Observational: 1 in 4.5 years. GEV-fitted return period analysis on historical houses-totally-damaged records (NDRRMC/EM-DAT) was conducted in `analysis/04_trigger_thresholds.md` and `analysis/03_compute_return_period.md`.
- **Authoritative source:** 2025 framework PDF (see `framework_doc`). The 510 model is maintained by Netherlands Red Cross / NLRC 510 Initiative; OCHA CHD runs it operationally.
- **Operated by:** OCHA Philippines and OCHA CHD (live trigger monitoring and 510 model runs); `pa-aa-phl-storms` repo contains the calibration analysis, not the production system.

## Monitoring

OCHA CHD runs the NLRC 510 probabilistic model every 6–12 hours during TC events. Updates are shared with CERF AA agency focal points via email and meetings. No standalone monitoring pipeline is in this repo. Monitoring relies on ECMWF data access maintained by NLRC 510 / OCHA CHD outside this repository.

## Historical activations

**This 2025 framework** was activated on **6 November 2025** when the observational trigger fired for Super Typhoon Fung-Wong (local name: Uwan), roughly three days before forecast landfall. Forecast 10-min sustained wind speed exceeded the observational threshold of 185 km/h (PAGASA). CERF released **~US$5.95 million** within minutes to five UN agencies (FAO, IOM, WFP, UNICEF, UNFPA). The activation **initially targeted northern Luzon — an area outside the framework's three standard CERF regions (5/8/13)** — illustrating the observational pathway's discretion to follow a super typhoon wherever it threatens. Oxfam, the Philippine Red Cross and WFP also triggered their own AA protocols for their respective coverage areas.

(Note: the specific "~195 km/h at landfall" figure carried in an earlier draft is not corroborated by the PDF or repo; sources state the forecast exceeded 185 km/h. The exact at-landfall maximum is not recorded here.)

The 2025 PDF references "previous AA activations within the country" generically as context for the framework refinements (the 2024 after-action review folded in lessons from the successive 2024 typhoons), but does not name a specific prior CERF AA activation. Earlier near-activations (e.g. Typhoon Rai/Odette, December 2021 — monitored, not triggered) predate this version; treat any pre-2025 *triggered* activation as unconfirmed from these sources.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Shift from scenario-based to region-based (2025 change):** The 2023–2024 framework grouped Region 8 provinces into Scenario 1 (Samar Island provinces) and Scenario 2 (Leyte Island provinces + Region 13 Caraga) to reflect different TC track directions. The 2025 framework abandons this sub-region logic and disaggregates by full administrative region (5, 8, 13). Rationale: the different vulnerability profiles of the three regions are better captured at the regional level; this also simplifies targeting and regional planning.

**Addition of observational trigger (2025 change):** The 2024 and earlier frameworks used only two stages (readiness + action). The 2025 framework adds an observational fallback triggered by PAGASA wind reports, reflecting lessons from rapid TC intensification events (like Fung-Wong) where the forecast model may underestimate intensity at 72 hours.

**Focus on stronger typhoons (2025 change):** The readiness wind threshold was raised from ≥154 km/h (2024: Category 2 equivalent, 1-min) to ≥177 km/h (2025, 10-min sustained) — equivalent to a Philippine Typhoon category 4 or higher. This narrows the activation aperture to fewer, more severe events.

**Per-region thresholds replacing single-national matrix (2025 change):** The 2024 single activation range (50% @ 50k houses, 85% @ 8k houses nationally) was replaced by separate calibrated thresholds for each region (5, 8, 13), reflecting differing vulnerability and exposure profiles.

**Only one region activates per event:** Even if multiple regions breach thresholds simultaneously, only the one with higher estimated damage activates. This is a resource constraint decision given the fixed CERF envelope (~US$5–6M).

**WFP extension to Regions 2 and 3:** WFP uses internal funding to activate AA in Regions 2 and 3 guided by the same CERF framework triggers. Regional thresholds for Regions 2 and 3 were developed separately and included in the CHD model output, but these regions are outside CERF funding scope.

## Changes from previous version

The 2025 framework (supersedes 2024-09-13) introduces:

1. **Observational trigger added** — a third activation pathway for super typhoons (≥185 km/h, PAGASA) not captured by the action trigger at 72 hours; requires ≥50% of target region within damaging winds.
2. **Scenario → region disaggregation** — replaced Province-grouped Scenario 1 (R5 + Samar R8) and Scenario 2 (Leyte R8 + Caraga R13) with individual Region 5, 8, 13 thresholds.
3. **Readiness wind threshold raised** — from ≥154 km/h (1-min, Category 2+) to ≥177 km/h (10-min, Category 4+), targeting only stronger typhoons.
4. **Regional activation matrix** — new per-region probability×damage matrix replacing single national range.
5. **Budget reduction** — CERF envelope reduced from US$7.5M (2023/2024) to US$6M; total envelope including co-financing is ~US$10M.
6. **Framework becomes government law** — RA 12287 signed September 2025 institutionalizes AA in the Philippine government framework.
7. **MPC transfer value updated** — PHP 4,000 (2025) vs PHP 3,300 (2024), aligned with updated Minimum Expenditure Basket.
