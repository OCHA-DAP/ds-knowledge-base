# Nigeria Flood — 2026-06-18 (development)

> In-development successor to the 2025-08-11 endorsed version; not yet endorsed, no published framework doc. Trigger design content dates to the 2026-06-18 session; this sync reflects the `feat/glofas-country-reforecast-download` branch (SHA `fcbe009`, 2026-07-07). The canonical trigger is the code/notes at `code_ref`; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

This is the in-development 2026 revision of Nigeria's flood anticipatory action framework, focused on **Adamawa state** along the Benue river. The core change is a redesign of the riverine **action trigger** from the 2025 **single-station OR logic** (GloFAS OR Google GRRR at one Wuroboki gauge) to a **multi-gauge consensus trigger** — fires when ≥6 of 10 selected Google GRRR gauges simultaneously exceed their individual 4-year return period threshold. A new GloFAS **readiness trigger** has been selected (Option A: GloFAS ensemble mean > 3,132 m³/s at Wuroboki, lead time ≤ 13 days) to give partners advance notice ahead of the action trigger. The 2025 **NHF flash-flood** component (observational, BAY states) is retained as a separate funding stream; on this branch its 2026 LGA-retargeting work is at an earlier, exploratory stage (comparing the 2025 LGAs against CERF-prioritized LGAs and raw NiHSA risk-community counts, with no finalized shortlist). Following the Adamawa analysis, **Benue state was dropped** from the 2026 framework. No funding envelope, implementing agencies, or target population are committed for this development version. This branch's own distinguishing contribution is a **new whole-Nigeria GloFAS reforecast bulk-download pipeline**, not yet used by the trigger itself (see Monitoring).

## Method

The 2026 analysis follows a structured seven-step workflow (`exploration/2026/cerf/workflow/`):

1. **Reporting points** (`01`) — identify all Google GRRR gauges whose catchments intersect the target LGAs, plus the GloFAS reference station (≈42 GRRR gauges + Wuroboki for Adamawa).
2. **Floodscan benchmark** (`02`) — aggregate Floodscan SFED to a daily mean over a 10 km river buffer; compute wet-season (Aug–Nov) annual maxima and flag event years at 3-, 4-, and 5-year RP via the Weibull plotting position (RP = (n+1)/k). The **4-year RP** is the primary design target (event years 1999, 2012, 2015, 2018, 2022, 2023).
3. **Forecast correlation** (`03`) — rank every candidate gauge by best lag-adjusted Spearman ρ vs Floodscan SFED.
4. **Gauge selection** (`04`) — retain the top 10 gauges by Spearman ρ from the combined GRRR+GloFAS pool (purely performance-based; in Adamawa all 10 happen to be GRRR, with Wuroboki GloFAS just below the cutoff).
5. **Trigger assessment** (`05`) — grid search over (per-gauge RP threshold, minimum gauge fraction); score against Floodscan event years with POD/FAR/F1.
6. **Trigger definition** (`06`) — fix the configuration; evaluate reanalysis (1998–2023) and GRRR multi-gauge reforecast (2016–2022, 7 yrs) performance.
7. **Readiness trigger** (`07`) — evaluate a single-station GloFAS readiness trigger across lead times vs Floodscan event years.

The write-up for this workflow lives in the dated session notes (`notes/2026-06-18.md` and the earlier `notes/2026-05-*.md` sessions) and the notebooks themselves. **Note:** the prior sync of this page cited consolidated write-ups `notes/trigger_development.md` and `notes/methodology.md` as the primary narrative sources — neither file exists in this branch's checkout (see discrepancies); all design facts below are re-verified directly against the notebooks and session notes instead.

The selected Adamawa action trigger: **≥6 of 10 GRRR gauges simultaneously exceed their individual 4-yr empirical Weibull RP threshold on at least one wet-season day** (per-gauge thresholds ~1,101–1,117 m³/s for the 9 main-channel gauges, 142.7 m³/s for the outlier). The readiness trigger (GloFAS reforecast at Wuroboki) is confirmed selected as Option A in both `notebook 07` and `exploration_peak_timing.ipynb`. A separate observational NHF flash-flood window (BAY states) remains at an exploratory LGA-comparison stage on this branch.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** Google GRRR reanalysis (calibration) and reforecast (operational) streamflow at 10 gauges in Adamawa (action); GloFAS ensemble reforecast at Wuroboki (readiness); FloodScan SFED per-LGA observation (NHF flash flood).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** The **action** trigger fires if at least 6 of the 10 selected GRRR gauges simultaneously report streamflow above their individual 4-year return-period threshold on the same wet-season day. The **readiness** trigger fires when the GloFAS ensemble-mean discharge at Wuroboki exceeds 3,132 m³/s at a lead time of ≤13 days — an earlier, lower-bar pre-positioning signal that does not guarantee an action activation will follow. A GloFAS-reanalysis (near-real-time, LT=0) "safety net" OR-condition was evaluated in the 2026-06-18 session notes and explicitly **not recommended** for 2026 (no real-time GloFAS feed is available), and it does not appear in the canonical readiness notebook — so this page treats the readiness trigger as reforecast-only. The **NHF flash-flood** window targets high-risk BAY-state LGAs and fires on observed flash flooding (FloodScan SFED, mechanism carried from 2025) for a separate NHF funding stream; the 2026 LGA set is unresolved on this branch (see discrepancies).
- **Activation structure:** Two riverine windows for Adamawa — readiness (GloFAS, lower RP ~3.0 yr, fires first) and action (GRRR multi-gauge, ~4.5 yr RP) — plus the parallel NHF flash-flood window. No funding or release logic is fixed for this development version.
- **Calibration:** Empirical Weibull RP from wet-season (Aug–Nov) annual maxima, 1998–2023 (n=26). Action trigger RP ~4.5 yr; readiness RP ~3.0 yr (Option A). All thresholds anchored to Floodscan SFED satellite flood observations, not modelled discharge.
- **Authoritative source:** `trigger_source: repo` — no published framework doc exists for this version (`framework_doc: null`); no PDF extract was available for this re-sync either, so this page is drafted entirely from the design notebooks and session notes. The consolidated design write-ups referenced by the prior sync (`notes/trigger_development.md`, `notes/methodology.md`) are not present in this branch's checkout — the authoritative sources here are `notes/2026-06-18.md` and notebooks `06`/`07`.
- **Operated by:** null — analysis by OCHA CHD; no deployed trigger system for the 2026 design.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Multi-gauge consensus over single-gauge OR logic.** The 2025 trigger's single Wuroboki station was a single point of failure — gauge malfunction or a flood peaking away from Wuroboki causes misses or false alarms. Requiring 6 of 10 gauges to agree simultaneously is a strong spatial quality filter that eliminates local noise and ensures the signal represents a basin-wide flood. The trade-off: it replaces the 2025 design's two-model (GloFAS+GRRR) redundancy with spatial redundancy inside a single model, removing GloFAS as an independent cross-check — which is part of why GloFAS was redeployed as a separate readiness trigger.

**Activation frequency held constant.** The ≥6/10 (60%) configuration was chosen partly to keep **6 fire years in 26** (RP ~4.5 yr), matching the intervention frequency endorsed in 2025. A ≥8/10 configuration scored a higher F1 in the grid search but fires less often, raising the framework RP and reducing activation frequency — so it was not selected.

**4-year RP target, better calibrated.** At the 4-yr design target the 2026 trigger fires in 2018 (a genuine 4-yr event) instead of 2003 (only a 3-yr event), improving POD 0.50→0.67, FPR 0.15→0.10, F1 0.50→0.67. Performance at 3-yr and 5-yr benchmarks is identical to 2025.

**GloFAS as readiness, Option A selected.** Option A (3,132 m³/s, LT≤13d, RP~3.0 yr) was chosen over Option B (3,250 m³/s, LT≤12d, RP~3.5 yr) because Option B fires *after* the action trigger in 2012, while Option A keeps a (marginal, +2d) early signal there. Both miss 2018 (GloFAS underforecast at LT≤13d). A GloFAS-reanalysis "safety net" component was evaluated and **not recommended** (adds no new detections in 2003–2022; needs an unavailable real-time feed) — this is stated plainly in the 2026-06-18 session notes; the notebooks implement only the reforecast-only version.

**Benue dropped.** Benue was initially scoped as a second target state but, after the Adamawa analysis, the decision was to keep focus on Adamawa for 2026. The workflow is transferable (notebooks accept `STATE = "Benue"`), but no Benue trigger is part of the 2026 framework.

**NHF flash flood re-targeting still exploratory.** The observational flash-flood mechanism is carried from 2025 (6 BAY-state LGAs). On this branch, the retargeting work compares those 2025 LGAs against 16 CERF-prioritized LGAs and raw NiHSA high-risk-community counts, but has not yet produced a finalized 2026 shortlist.
