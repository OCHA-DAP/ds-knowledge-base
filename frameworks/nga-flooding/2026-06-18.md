# Nigeria Flood — 2026-06-18 (development)

> In-development successor to the 2025-08-11 endorsed version; not yet endorsed, no published framework doc. Trigger design from the `govt-2026-prep` branch (re-synced at SHA `a1858fb`, 2026-07-04; design content dates to the 2026-06-18 session), subject to change. The canonical trigger is the code/notes at `code_ref`; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

This is the in-development 2026 revision of Nigeria's flood anticipatory action framework, focused on **Adamawa state** along the Benue river. The core change is a redesign of the riverine **action trigger** from the 2025 **single-station OR logic** (GloFAS OR Google GRRR at one Wuroboki gauge) to a **multi-gauge consensus trigger** — fires when ≥6 of 10 selected Google GRRR gauges simultaneously exceed their individual 4-year return period threshold. A new GloFAS **readiness trigger** has been added and, since the prior sync, **selected** (Option A: GloFAS ensemble mean > 3,132 m³/s at Wuroboki, lead time ≤ 13 days) to give partners advance notice ahead of the action trigger. The 2025 **NHF flash-flood** component (observational, BAY states) is retained as a separate funding stream, with the 2026 work re-targeting its LGA set from the NiHSA 2026 at-risk-communities dataset. Following the Adamawa analysis, **Benue state was dropped** from the 2026 framework. No funding envelope, implementing agencies, or target population are committed for this development version.

## Method

The 2026 analysis follows a structured seven-step workflow (`exploration/2026/cerf/workflow/`, written up in `notes/methodology.md` and `notes/trigger_development.md`):

1. **Reporting points** (`01`) — identify all Google GRRR gauges whose catchments intersect the target LGAs, plus the GloFAS reference station (≈42 GRRR gauges + Wuroboki for Adamawa).
2. **Floodscan benchmark** (`02`) — aggregate Floodscan SFED to a daily mean over a 10 km river buffer; compute wet-season (Aug–Nov) annual maxima and flag event years at 3-, 4-, and 5-year RP via the Weibull plotting position (RP = (n+1)/k). The **4-year RP** is the primary design target (event years 1999, 2012, 2015, 2018, 2022, 2023).
3. **Forecast correlation** (`03`) — rank every candidate gauge by best lag-adjusted Spearman ρ vs Floodscan SFED (lag range −3 to +14 days).
4. **Gauge selection** (`04`) — retain the top 10 gauges by Spearman ρ from the combined GRRR+GloFAS pool (purely performance-based; in Adamawa all 10 happen to be GRRR, with Wuroboki GloFAS just below the cutoff).
5. **Trigger assessment** (`05`) — grid search over (per-gauge RP threshold, minimum gauge fraction); score against Floodscan event years with POD/FAR/F1.
6. **Trigger definition** (`06`) — fix the configuration; evaluate reanalysis (1998–2023) and reforecast (2003–2022) performance.
7. **Readiness trigger** (`07`) — evaluate a single-station GloFAS readiness trigger across lead times vs Floodscan event years.

The selected Adamawa action trigger: **≥6 of 10 GRRR gauges simultaneously exceed their individual 4-yr empirical Weibull RP threshold on at least one wet-season day** (per-gauge thresholds ~1,101–1,117 m³/s for the 9 main-channel gauges, 142.7 m³/s for the outlier). The readiness trigger (GloFAS reforecast at Wuroboki) has now been selected as Option A. A separate observational NHF flash-flood window (BAY states) is being re-targeted via NiHSA at-risk-community scoring.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** Google GRRR reanalysis (calibration) and reforecast (operational) streamflow at 10 gauges in Adamawa (action); GloFAS ensemble reforecast at Wuroboki (readiness); FloodScan SFED per-LGA observation (NHF flash flood).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** The **action** trigger fires if at least 6 of the 10 selected GRRR gauges simultaneously report streamflow above their individual 4-year return-period threshold on the same wet-season day. The **readiness** trigger fires when the GloFAS ensemble-mean discharge at Wuroboki exceeds 3,132 m³/s at a lead time of ≤13 days — an earlier, lower-bar pre-positioning signal that does not guarantee an action activation will follow. (The repo's own design write-up, `trigger_development.md`, also lists a GloFAS-reanalysis "OR" branch (LT=0, near-real-time) in its summary/spec tables, but that same document's Appendix and the 2026-06-18 session notes explicitly recommend **against** adding it for 2026 — no real-time GloFAS feed is available — so this page treats the readiness trigger as reforecast-only; see `discrepancies`.) The **NHF flash-flood** window targets a set of high-risk BAY-state LGAs and fires on observed flash flooding (FloodScan SFED, mechanism carried from 2025) for a separate NHF funding stream.
- **Activation structure:** Two riverine windows for Adamawa — readiness (GloFAS, lower RP ~3.0 yr, fires first) and action (GRRR multi-gauge, ~4.5 yr RP) — plus the parallel NHF flash-flood window. No funding or release logic is fixed for this development version.
- **Calibration:** Empirical Weibull RP from wet-season (Aug–Nov) annual maxima, 1998–2023 (n=26). Action trigger RP ~4.5 yr; readiness RP ~3.0 yr (Option A). All thresholds anchored to Floodscan SFED satellite flood observations, not modelled discharge.
- **Authoritative source:** `trigger_source: repo` — no published framework doc exists for this version (`framework_doc: null`); no PDF extract was available for this re-sync either, so this page is drafted entirely from the design write-ups and code. The design write-ups are `notes/trigger_development.md` and `notes/methodology.md` on `govt-2026-prep`.
- **Operated by:** null — analysis by OCHA CHD; no deployed trigger system for the 2026 design.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Multi-gauge consensus over single-gauge OR logic.** The 2025 trigger's single Wuroboki station was a single point of failure — gauge malfunction or a flood peaking away from Wuroboki causes misses or false alarms. Requiring 6 of 10 gauges to agree simultaneously is a strong spatial quality filter that eliminates local noise and ensures the signal represents a basin-wide flood. The trade-off: it replaces the 2025 design's two-model (GloFAS+GRRR) redundancy with spatial redundancy inside a single model, removing GloFAS as an independent cross-check — which is part of why GloFAS was redeployed as a separate readiness trigger.

**Activation frequency held constant.** The ≥6/10 (60%) configuration was chosen partly to keep **6 fire years in 26** (RP ~4.5 yr), matching the intervention frequency endorsed in 2025. A ≥8/10 configuration scored a higher F1 in the grid search but fires less often, raising the framework RP and reducing activation frequency — so it was not selected.

**4-year RP target, better calibrated.** At the 4-yr design target the 2026 trigger fires in 2018 (a genuine 4-yr event) instead of 2003 (only a 3-yr event), improving POD 0.50→0.67, FAR 0.15→0.10, F1 0.50→0.67. Performance at 3-yr and 5-yr benchmarks is identical to 2025.

**GloFAS as readiness, Option A selected.** Option A (3,132 m³/s, LT≤13d, RP~3.0 yr) was chosen over Option B (3,250 m³/s, LT≤12d, RP~3.5 yr) because Option B fires *after* the action trigger in 2012, while Option A keeps a (marginal, +2d) early signal there. Both miss 2018 (GloFAS underforecast at LT≤13d). Extending to LT≤16d catches 2018 but adds a false positive (2010) and drops the RP to 2.3 yr — an unacceptable activation frequency. A GloFAS-reanalysis "safety net" component was evaluated and **not recommended** (adds no new detections in 2003–2022; needs an unavailable real-time feed) — this is stated plainly in `trigger_development.md`'s own Appendix, which makes it odd that the same document's earlier summary/spec tables list the reanalysis OR-branch as part of the adopted "2026 readiness trigger." Treat the not-recommended verdict as authoritative until the repo's docs are reconciled.

**Benue dropped.** Benue was initially scoped as a second target state but, after the Adamawa analysis, the decision was to keep focus on Adamawa for 2026. The workflow is transferable (notebooks 05/06 run with `STATE = "Benue"`), but no Benue trigger is part of the 2026 framework.

**NHF flash flood re-targeted, not redesigned.** The observational flash-flood mechanism is carried from 2025; 2026 re-selects the BAY-state LGAs using the NiHSA at-risk-communities composite score, retaining Maiduguri and Ngala from 2025 and dropping Bade, Bama, Karasuwa, Madagali.

## Changes from previous version

The 2025-08-11 endorsed version uses a **single-station OR trigger** (GloFAS ≥3,132 m³/s OR Google GRRR ≥1,195 m³/s at Wuroboki) for the riverine action, plus a flash-flood observational trigger (FloodScan SFED per-LGA for 6 BAY LGAs). The 2026 development:

1. **Action trigger** — replaced single-station OR logic with a multi-gauge consensus trigger (≥6 of 10 GRRR gauges at 4-yr RP). Fires in 2018 (missed by 2025) but misses 2003 (caught by 2025); both miss 2015 and 2023. Same ~4.5-yr activation frequency by design.
2. **Readiness trigger** — new GloFAS reforecast component, **now selected** (Option A, 3,132 m³/s, LT≤13d, ~3.0 yr RP). Not present in the 2025 design.
3. **NHF flash flood** — retained as a separate window; LGA set re-targeted via the NiHSA 2026 dataset (Maiduguri/Ngala retained; Bade/Bama/Karasuwa/Madagali dropped). *(In the prior sync this component was recorded as absent from the 2026 analysis; it is now present at the targeting stage.)*
4. **Geographic scope** — Benue state, briefly scoped as a second target state in the prior sync, was **dropped**; the 2026 riverine framework covers the 7 Adamawa LGAs only.

## Open questions / known issues

1. **2026 design not in code.** The multi-gauge action design and the selected readiness option exist only in notebooks/notes. When finalised they must be written into `STATE_CONFIG` (`glofas_leadtime_readiness` etc. are still `None`) and the monitoring pipeline rebuilt before the 2026 trigger can go operational.

2. **NHF LGA set not fixed.** 7 candidate BAY-state LGAs are ranked; 4 are to be selected after an ongoing review. The observational trigger thresholds for 2026 are not re-derived in the notebooks (the 2025 mechanism is assumed).

3. **hybas_1120840690 outlier gauge.** 4-yr threshold 142.7 m³/s (≈8× lower than the other nine), positive lag (+2d), contributed to only 4 of 6 trigger years — likely a small tributary. Retained (one vote of ten can't dominate ≥6/10) but flagged for review in any future gauge-set revision.

4. **2018 not detectable by readiness within LT≤13d.** GloFAS underforecast the 2018 event at short lead times (only crosses 3,132 m³/s at LT=14d); the readiness trigger cannot reliably serve 2018-type events.

5. **Short reforecast evaluation period.** GloFAS reforecast covers 2003–2022 (20 yrs); the GRRR multi-gauge reforecast only 2016–2022 (7 yrs). All reforecast lead-time figures carry substantial uncertainty.

6. **Both triggers miss 2015 and 2023.** Genuine 4–5-yr Floodscan flood years that neither the 2025 nor 2026 action trigger detects (possible causes: Lagdo-dam release timing, upper-Benue vs tributary pathways, gauge-coverage gaps). Investigating these is a priority before deployment.

7. **Readiness trigger spec needs reconciling within the repo itself.** `trigger_development.md`'s "Proposed trigger — summary" table and "Trigger specifications" section describe the 2026 readiness trigger as reforecast **OR** GloFAS reanalysis (LT=0) — but the same document's own Appendix says that reanalysis addition "was evaluated and not recommended" for 2026, and the 2026-06-18 session notes reach the same conclusion. Before this design is finalised, someone needs to either strike the OR-reanalysis wording from the summary tables or explicitly re-adopt it (and, if re-adopted, implement it in `07_readiness_trigger.ipynb`, which currently only uses the reanalysis for threshold calibration, not as a live trigger branch).
