# Nigeria Flood — 2026-06-18 (development)

> In-development successor to the 2025-08-11 endorsed version; not yet endorsed, no published framework doc. Trigger design from the `govt-2026-prep` branch (SHA f4df116) as of 2026-06-18, subject to change. The canonical trigger is the code at `code_ref`; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

This is the in-development 2026 revision of Nigeria's flood anticipatory action framework. The core change is a redesign of the Adamawa riverine action trigger from a **single-station OR logic** (GloFAS OR Google GRRR at one gauge, as in the 2025 endorsed version) to a **multi-gauge consensus trigger** (≥6 of 10 selected GRRR gauges must simultaneously exceed their individual 4-year return period threshold). The analysis also extends the geographic scope to include **Benue state** (Makurdi station) alongside Adamawa, though no thresholds have been defined for Benue yet. A GloFAS-based **readiness trigger** is being evaluated as a new early-warning component ahead of the action trigger. The 2025 flash-flood NHF component (BAY states, FloodScan SFED) is not addressed in the 2026 analysis to date. No funding envelope, implementing agencies, or target population are committed for this development version.

## Method

The 2026 analysis follows a structured workflow pipeline (`exploration/2026/cerf/workflow/`) with seven steps:

1. **Reporting points** — identify GloFAS and Google GRRR gauges within the target LGAs.
2. **Floodscan benchmark** — compute wet-season (Aug–Nov) Floodscan SFED annual maxima and flag event years at 3-, 4-, and 5-year RP using the Weibull formula.
3. **Forecast correlation** — rank all gauges by Spearman ρ (correlation with Floodscan SFED, lag range −7 to +14 days).
4. **Gauge selection** — select top 10 gauges by Spearman ρ.
5. **Trigger assessment** — grid search over (N gauges, RP threshold) pairs, score against Floodscan event years.
6. **Trigger definition** — select the final (N, RP) configuration; evaluate reanalysis and reforecast performance.
7. **Readiness trigger** — evaluate GloFAS station threshold at multiple lead times vs Floodscan event years.

The selected action trigger for Adamawa: **≥6 of 10 GRRR gauges simultaneously exceed their 4-yr empirical Weibull RP threshold on at least one wet-season day**. Thresholds are per-gauge (not a single shared value), derived from wet-season annual maxima 1998–2023. The readiness trigger (GloFAS reforecast at Wuroboki) is still being evaluated; two candidate options are documented but no selection has been made.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** Google GRRR reanalysis and reforecast streamflow at 10 selected gauges in Adamawa state (action trigger); GloFAS ensemble reforecast at Wuroboki / Makurdi (readiness trigger).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** The action trigger fires if at least 6 of the 10 selected GRRR gauges simultaneously report streamflow exceeding their individual 4-year return period threshold on the same wet-season day. The readiness trigger (design not finalised) fires if the GloFAS ensemble mean discharge at Wuroboki exceeds a calibrated threshold within a defined maximum lead time.
- **Activation structure:** Two windows being designed — a readiness trigger (GloFAS, fires first, lower RP ~3–3.5 yr) and an action trigger (GRRR multi-gauge, ~4.5 yr RP). No funding or release logic is defined yet for this version.
- **Calibration:** Empirical Weibull RP from wet-season annual maxima (1998–2023, 26 years). Action trigger RP ~4.5 yr (reanalysis). Readiness options A/B: ~3.0–3.5 yr.
- **Authoritative source:** `trigger_source: repo` — no published framework doc exists for this version. The design is in `exploration/2026/cerf/workflow/` on `govt-2026-prep`.
- **Operated by:** null — analysis is by OCHA CHD. No deployed trigger system for this version.

## Key decisions & rationale

**Multi-gauge consensus over single-gauge OR logic:** The 2025 trigger's single Wuroboki station was a single point of failure — gauge malfunction or a flood event peaking away from Wuroboki causes misses or false alarms. Requiring 6 of 10 gauges to agree simultaneously is a strong spatial quality filter that eliminates local noise and ensures the signal represents a basin-wide flood pattern rather than a station artefact.

**GRRR-only action trigger (no GloFAS in action window):** At the 4-yr calibration level, the GRRR multi-gauge design is better calibrated than the 2025 GloFAS OR design — it fires in 2018 (a genuine 4-yr event) rather than 2003 (a 3-yr event). However, the maximum GRRR reforecast horizon is ≤7 days, which is a tighter operational window than GloFAS's ≤5-day window (especially for large events with longer upstream signal). GloFAS is moved to the readiness trigger.

**GloFAS as readiness trigger:** GloFAS can provide earlier warning in some years (e.g. 46 days in 2019) but is unreliable across years — it provided no meaningful early warning in 2012 (a major event). A readiness trigger at a lower RP threshold (~3–3.5 yr) is intended as a low-cost pre-positioning signal that does not guarantee a full action activation will follow.

**4-year RP target:** Consistent with the 2025 endorsed design's CERF requirement. The 2026 action trigger more faithfully achieves the 4-yr RP target (Weibull ~4.5 yr) than the 2025 design.

**10-gauge selection by Spearman ρ:** Gauges ranked by their correlation with Floodscan SFED (the observational ground truth for flood impact) across a range of lags. This ensures selected gauges are those whose streamflow signal best predicts actual flooding in the target LGAs.

## Changes from previous version

The 2025-08-11 endorsed version uses a **single-station OR trigger** (GloFAS ≥3,132 m³/s OR Google GRRR ≥1,195 m³/s at Wuroboki, ~4.5 yr RP combined) covering 7 Adamawa LGAs, plus a separate flash-flood observational trigger (FloodScan SFED per-LGA thresholds for 6 BAY LGAs). The 2026 development redesigns the action trigger as follows:

1. **Action trigger** — replaced the single-station OR logic with a multi-gauge consensus trigger (≥6 of 10 GRRR gauges simultaneously at 4-yr RP). Key performance difference: the 2026 trigger fires in 2018 (missed by 2025) but misses 2003 (caught by 2025). Both designs miss 2015 and 2023.
2. **Readiness trigger** — new component being evaluated. Not present in the 2025 endorsed design. Uses GloFAS reforecast at Wuroboki as an early-warning signal ahead of the GRRR action trigger.
3. **Geographic expansion** — Benue state (Makurdi station, 5 LGAs along the Benue river) is being added as a second target state. Not in the 2025 endorsed design.
4. **Flash-flood NHF component** — the 2025 observational trigger (FloodScan SFED for BAY states) is not yet addressed in the 2026 analysis.

## Open questions / known issues

1. **Readiness trigger option selection:** Options A (3,132 m³/s, LT≤13d, RP~3yr) and B (3,250 m³/s, LT≤12d, RP~3.5yr) have been evaluated. The key trade-off is whether to accept the marginally earlier warning in 2012 (Option A, +2d) at the cost of higher FPR (25% vs 19%). GloFAS completely misses 2018 under both options. Neither option has been selected as of 2026-06-18.

2. **Benue state threshold definition:** `glofas_thresh`, `google_thresh`, `glofas_leadtime_action`, and `google_leadtime_readiness` are all `None` in `STATE_CONFIG["Benue"]`. Notebook `17_threshold_selection.ipynb` provides the framework for the grid search, but no thresholds have been chosen. The Benue Floodscan pixels parquet (1998–2025) has been processed and is available on the blob.

3. **hybas_1120840690 outlier gauge:** This Adamawa gauge has a 4-yr threshold of ~143 m³/s (vs ~1,100 m³/s for the other nine), a positive lag (+2d, peaking after Floodscan), and contributed to only 4 of 6 trigger years. It is likely a small tributary rather than the main Benue channel. Whether to exclude it before finalising the gauge set is unresolved — its inclusion is not harmful in the ≥6-gauge logic since it contributes one vote of ten, but its outlier threshold dilutes the design's internal consistency.

4. **Flash-flood NHF component:** The 2025 design included an NHF-only reprogramming trigger for 6 BAY-states LGAs (FloodScan SFED). This component is not discussed in the 2026 development analysis. It is unclear whether it will be retained as-is, redesigned, or dropped.

5. **GRRR reforecast evaluation period:** Only 2016–2022 (7 years) have complete GRRR reforecast coverage. This is too short to draw robust conclusions about reforecast lead times at the action trigger level. All reforecast performance figures carry substantial uncertainty.

6. **Both triggers miss 2015 and 2023:** These are genuine 4–5-yr Floodscan flood years that neither the 2025 nor 2026 action trigger detects. Investigating the cause (different flood pathway, upstream conditions, gauge coverage gaps) is a priority.

7. **CODE_REF not updated:** The 2026 multi-gauge design exists only in analysis notebooks. When thresholds are finalised, they must be written back into `src/constants.py` and the monitoring pipeline updated before this version can go operational.
