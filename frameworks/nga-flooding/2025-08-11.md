# Nigeria flooding — 2025-08-11

## Summary
Two independent flood triggers. **Riverine** (CERF + NHF, primary): activates on forecast/reanalysis river discharge on the Benue River exceeding thresholds at two gauge stations. **Flash** (NHF reprogramming, monitoring-only): per-LGA satellite flood-exposure thresholds. Endorsed by the ERC 11 Aug 2025; valid 2025–2026; CERF $5M + NHF $2M.

## Method
OCHA/CHD runs automated daily monitoring (cron 20:00 UTC) of two redundant riverine forecast sources (GloFAS, Google Flood Hub) plus FloodScan flash exposure. On a riverine threshold crossing, CERF disburses on a no-regrets basis via pre-signed approval letters.

## Trigger logic
- **Keys off:** river discharge (m³/s) — riverine; population-exposed-to-flooding — flash.
- **Decision rule (riverine, OR):** GloFAS forecast (≤5d) ≥ 3,132 m³/s at Wuroboki **OR** GloFAS reanalysis ≥ 3,132 **OR** Google forecast ≥ 1,195 m³/s at Kangli (any lead). Flash: per-LGA 3-day-rolling exposure exceeds an LGA-specific threshold.
- **Calibration:** riverine ~1-in-4.5-yr; flash per-LGA thresholds set for equal triggering probability (~1-in-4-yr); historical lead time 14–25 days.
- **Authoritative source:** 2025 endorsed PDF; the repo code matches it exactly.

## Trigger windows

| window | basis | indicator | threshold | source | return period | releases |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Riverine (Wuroboki) | forecast | discharge | ≥ 3,132 m³/s | GloFAS fcast ≤5d / reanalysis | ~1-in-4.5yr | CERF $5M + NHF $2M |
| Riverine (Kangli) | forecast | discharge | ≥ 1,195 m³/s | Google Flood Hub (any lead) | — | CERF + NHF |
| Flash (per-LGA ×6) | observational | pop exposed | LGA-specific (e.g. Ngala 126,086) | FloodScan SFED ×WorldPop | ~1-in-4yr | NHF reprogramming only |

(Repo-only WARNING tier: GloFAS 2,800 / Google 1,000 → informational emails, not activation.)

## Monitoring
OCHA/CHD daily monitoring pipeline (GHA `monitoring.yml`, cron 20:00 UTC) → emails to the government-chaired Trigger Group; CHD confirms activation. Apps: flood-exposure return periods + monitoring overview (marimo).

## Key decisions & rationale
Dual independent triggers separate CERF-eligible riverine from NHF-only flash. Two redundant riverine sources OR'd for outage resilience and max lead time. GloFAS reanalysis allowed as a lead-time-0 trigger. Flash thresholds per-LGA (not comparable across LGAs due to FloodScan geographic bias). Mid-September cutoff pivots NHF from riverine to flash.
