# Somalia Drought — 2019

> No DS code repo and no standalone framework-design PDF were located; the trigger is reconstructed from public OCHA/CERF documents (`framework_doc` + the CERF allocation/report PDFs and the Centre for Disaster Protection evaluation). This page is a sourced institutional summary, not a derivation from code.

## Summary

Somalia's anticipatory action (AA) pilot for drought/famine was one of OCHA's first AA frameworks, designed and endorsed in 2019 by humanitarian partners under the Humanitarian Coordinator together with the Federal Government of Somalia, OCHA, the World Bank, donors and technical partners. It combined four components: a forecast/trigger built on **FSNAU/IPC food-security projections as a proxy for drought**, pre-agreed anticipatory actions, pre-arranged CERF finance, and a learning agenda. The framework activated **twice**: in June 2020 (USD 15M, the first OCHA-facilitated collective AA allocation, reaching 638,718 people) and in February 2021 (USD 20M, targeting 661,166 people). The defining lesson from the pilot was that it lacked a drought-specific trigger — the 2020 activation fired on food insecurity caused by locusts, flooding and COVID-19 rather than drought itself.

## Method

The framework keys off forward-looking **IPC food-security projections** produced by the Somalia Food Security and Nutrition Analysis Unit (FSNAU, hosted by FAO) and FEWSNET. When the projected share of the population in IPC Phase 3+ (Crisis or worse) reaches the trigger threshold — reported as **≥20%** — the framework activates: the Emergency Relief Coordinator releases pre-arranged CERF funds against a set of pre-agreed anticipatory actions, implemented by UN agencies and partners ahead of the projected food-security crisis. Because IPC projections look several months ahead (the projection horizon), the trigger provides lead time over a reactive response, but it measures food insecurity (the impact) rather than the drought hazard directly.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** FSNAU/FEWSNET IPC food-security **projections** (forward-looking), used as a proxy indicator for drought-driven famine risk.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** When FSNAU/FEWSNET project that the population in IPC Phase 3+ (Crisis or worse) will reach or exceed the agreed threshold (reported ≥20%) over the coming projection period, the trigger is met and the ERC releases pre-arranged CERF funds for the pre-agreed anticipatory actions.
- **Activation structure:** Single national-level food-security window; activated at most as projections are published. No staged/multi-window AND-OR logic was used in the pilot.
- **Calibration:** Absolute IPC-phase population-share threshold set by stakeholder consensus, not return-period calibrated. The choice of IPC projections as the indicator was pragmatic — IPC is the authoritative, regularly-updated food-security signal for Somalia — but it is an impact proxy, not a hazard (rainfall/vegetation) measure.
- **Authoritative source:** Public OCHA "Learning from 2020" material and CERF allocation/report PDFs (`framework_doc`). No standalone framework-design PDF was located, and there is no code repo.
- **Operated by:** OCHA/CHD-facilitated; the trigger was monitored against routine FSNAU/FEWSNET IPC analyses rather than an automated pipeline.

## Monitoring

The trigger was monitored against the regular FSNAU/FEWSNET IPC analysis cycle for Somalia (post-Deyr assessment ~January–February; post-Gu assessment ~July–August), cross-checked with food-security projections. No automated monitoring pipeline or app is associated with this framework.

## Historical activations

- **19 June 2020 (first activation):** USG/ERC Mark Lowcock released **USD 15M** from CERF — the first OCHA-facilitated collective AA allocation. The food-insecure population was projected to rise from ~1.1M to ~3.5M (July–September 2020). The activation reached **638,718** people (cash, livestock vaccination, desert-locust control) across Gedo, Hiraan, Middle Juba and Middle/Lower Shabelle, with IOM additionally in Bakool, Lower Juba, Lower Shabelle, Galgaduud and Mudug. It helped prevent ~500,000 people from sliding into IPC Crisis/Emergency (Jul 2020–Jan 2021) and mobilised an additional USD 181M. **Key caveat:** this fired on food insecurity driven by the "triple threat" of locusts, floods and COVID-19, **not drought** — the pilot had no drought-specific trigger ([CERF report 20-RR-SOM-44036](https://cerf.un.org/sites/default/files/resources/20-RR-SOM-44036_Somalia_CERF_Report.pdf); [OCHA: Learning from 2020](https://www.unocha.org/anticipatory-action-somalia-learning-2020)).
- **February 2021 (second activation):** A high probability of drought combined with FSNAU forecasts of deteriorating food security met the threshold; **USD 20M** from CERF was allocated (April 2021, allocation 21-RR-SOM-47081) to **seven UN agencies** (FAO, IOM, UNFPA, UNHCR, UNICEF, WFP, WHO), targeting **661,166** people, against a broader projection that up to 2.65M Somalis (~21.5% of the population) could reach IPC3+ in April–June 2021. This was the first time the trigger fired on a genuinely drought-driven deterioration ([CERF report 21-RR-SOM-47081](https://cerf.un.org/sites/default/files/resources/21-RR-SOM-47081_Somalia_CERF_Report.pdf); [CERF allocation summary](https://cerf.un.org/what-we-do/allocation/2021/summary/21-RR-SOM-47081)).

## Key decisions & rationale

The pilot deliberately used **IPC food-security projections** as the trigger because IPC is the authoritative, regularly-updated, consensus-based food-security signal for Somalia, giving a defensible and widely-trusted basis for releasing CERF funds ahead of a projected crisis. The trade-off — flagged sharply in the 2020 evaluation — is that IPC is an **impact proxy, not a hazard indicator**: it does not isolate drought, so the 2020 activation fired on a food-security deterioration driven by locusts, floods and COVID-19. The recommended fix was to add a drought-specific (rainfall/vegetation) indicator alongside the IPC proxy, which later OCHA AA frameworks in the region (e.g. Ethiopia) moved toward.
