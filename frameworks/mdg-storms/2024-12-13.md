# Madagascar Tropical Cyclone — 2024-12-13

## Summary

This pilot anticipatory action (AA) framework for Madagascar targets intense and very intense tropical cyclones (Scenario 3 of the national contingency plan: sustained winds ≥ 166 km/h forecast within 100 km of the Malagasy coast). A two-step activation — readiness trigger (120–72 h before impact) and action trigger (≤ 72 h before impact) — releases up to USD 3 million in CERF pre-arranged funds to six UN agencies (IOM, UNICEF, UNFPA, WHO, WFP, FAO) covering shelter, WASH, education, health, food security, and protection. The Centre de Données Humanitaires (CHD) monitors Météo-Madagascar bulletins every 3–6 hours during cyclone season (1 November – 30 April) and activates when thresholds are met. The framework also provides for an observational indicator (IDP-based and/or rainfall-based) to accelerate CERF rapid response for sub-threshold events; this indicator was not yet finalized at the time of publication. The framework was first activated for Cyclone Gezani on 9 February 2026.

## Method

Météo-Madagascar (Direction Générale de la Météorologie, DGM) issues bulletins every 6 hours (3 hours as landfall approaches) covering a 5-day forecast window. Each bulletin includes the projected track, forecast time, and maximum wind speed at each track point. The CHD reads each bulletin and checks whether any forecast point meets the Scenario 3 conditions: (a) forecast maximum wind speed ≥ 166 km/h and (b) distance of the forecast position from the Malagasy coast ≤ 100 km. If conditions are met, the CHD — with confirmation from BNGRC where possible — sends an activation message to all partners and the CERF secretariat, which immediately issues letters of approval to agency headquarters.

The analytical basis for the thresholds was developed in this repo using IBTrACS best-track data (South Indian Ocean basin), La Réunion RSMC historical forecast data (2010/11–2023/24), BNGRC historical cyclone damage database (1997–2024), and IMERG daily precipitation data. Return-period analyses across three candidate scenarios (Scenario 2: 64 kt/~118 km/h; Scenario 2.5: hybrid; Scenario 3: 90 kt/~166 km/h) informed the final choice of Scenario 3 to align with the national contingency plan and target a roughly 1-in-3 year event frequency.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** Météo-Madagascar operational cyclone bulletin; indicator is forecast maximum wind speed and forecast distance to the Malagasy coast.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** If any forecast track point in a Météo-Madagascar bulletin shows a cyclone forecast to be within 100 km of Madagascar's coast AND at or above intense tropical cyclone intensity (≥ 166 km/h sustained 10-minute winds), the relevant trigger tier is activated. The readiness trigger fires when such a point appears at lead time 72–120 hours; the action trigger fires when such a point appears at lead time ≤ 72 hours. Lead time is measured as the hours between the bulletin's publication and the cyclone's predicted landfall (PDF footnote 2). The action trigger can fire even if the readiness trigger was not previously activated.
- **Activation structure:** Two sequential but independent windows — readiness (preparation phase) and action (implementation phase). On readiness activation, CERF immediately issues approval letters; agencies begin preparatory activities. On action activation (if not preceded by readiness), the same letters are issued and interventions deploy. Both stages release funds from the same USD 3 million envelope.
- **Calibration:** The PDF frames Scenario 3 as affecting Madagascar "environ une fois tous les trois ans" (~once every 3 years), aligning with the CERF mandate that only non-annual extraordinary crises can be funded. The repo's own notebook (`analysis/04.0_combined_return_period.ipynb`) computes this more precisely per window and finds the two windows diverge: using the PDF's exact readiness definition (La Réunion RSMC forecasts, 2010/11–2023/24, MG_distance_km ≤ 100, max_wind_kt ≥ 90, lead time 72–120h) the readiness window fires in 7 of 14 seasons (**~1-in-2 yr**); using IBTrACS best-track observations (2001–2024, wind ≥ 90 kt, no lead-time/distance filter — a looser proxy, not the exact action definition) the action window fires in 8 of 24 seasons (**~1-in-3 yr**, matching the PDF's headline figure). See `discrepancies`. The combined analysis incorporating the observational IDP/rainfall back-trigger is also computed in notebook 04.0.
- **Authoritative source:** The framework PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo (`code_ref`) provides the analysis backing the threshold choice.
- **Operated by:** CHD/OCHA — the live trigger is monitored manually by the Centre de Données Humanitaires reading Météo-Madagascar bulletins. No automated trigger system is deployed in this repo.

## Monitoring

During cyclone season (1 November – 30 April), the CHD reads every Météo-Madagascar bulletin (issued every 6 h, or 3 h near landfall) as soon as it is published (within ~1 hour of validity time). Each bulletin is assessed against the Scenario 3 thresholds. If thresholds are met, an activation message is sent immediately via email and WhatsApp to the BNGRC, HC/RC, OCHA, UN agency focal points, Start Network, and IFRC/Red Cross. No automated pipeline is deployed; the repo's monitoring notebooks (`analysis/05.x`) are retrospective post-event analyses for learning purposes, not a live system.

## Historical activations

The framework triggered once — its first activation:

- **2026-02-09 — Cyclone Gezani (Madagascar's first cyclone AA activation):** Météo-Madagascar forecasts met the Scenario 3 threshold (intense TC, ≥166 km/h, within 100 km of the coast) on 9 Feb 2026. The **full US$3M pre-arranged CERF envelope was released ~1 day ahead of landfall** to six UN agencies (IOM, UNICEF, UNFPA, WHO, WFP, FAO), targeting **more than 93,000** vulnerable people. Gezani made landfall near **Toamasina on 10 Feb 2026** as an intense TC (winds up to 250 km/h), causing severe damage across eastern/central Madagascar. Confirmed via OCHA Gezani flash updates (Feb 2026) and the [Anticipation Hub](https://www.anticipation-hub.org/news/governments-and-un-agencies-act-ahead-of-tropical-cyclone-gezani-in-madagascar-and-mozambique). (A separate US$4.5M CERF AA envelope was released for Mozambique for the same system.) The 2024/2025 season (Cyclones Honde and Jude, Feb–Mar 2025 — repo notebooks 05.0/05.1) did **not** trigger the framework.

Note: The Start Network ran a separate 2021/2022 pre-activation for Cyclones Batsirai and Emnati (~USD 470,000 / 250,000 people) under its own lower threshold (166 km/h, 3 days), which is not an activation of this OCHA/CERF framework.

## Key decisions & rationale

1. **Scenario 3 only (≥ 166 km/h):** The CERF mandate requires non-annual extraordinary crises. Scenario 2 (~1-in-2 yr) was deemed too frequent for CERF and would exhaust the envelope in most seasons. Scenario 3 (~1-in-3 yr) fits the CERF constraint while still covering the most destructive events (≥ 400,000–550,000 people in need).

2. **100 km coastal buffer:** This distance buffer ensures the trigger can fire with sufficient lead time for the action window to be meaningful and encompasses the typical track uncertainty at 72–120 h lead.

3. **Météo-Madagascar (DGM) as the sole official forecast source:** Madagascar's DGM is the WMO-affiliated national meteorological authority and the official source for national contingency plan activation. Using DGM bulletins directly aligns the AA trigger with the government's own emergency management protocols (BNGRC) and avoids ambiguity from multiple competing forecasts (e.g., ECMWF or NHC).

4. **Two-step (readiness + action) structure:** The readiness window (72–120 h) gives agencies 2–5 days to mobilize teams, pre-position stocks, and begin community sensitization. The action window (≤ 72 h) confirms the threat and authorizes full implementation. The action trigger can fire without a preceding readiness trigger to handle rapid-intensification scenarios.

5. **Observational indicator as a CERF rapid-response accelerator (not an AA trigger):** Recognizing that sub-threshold cyclones can still cause significant humanitarian impact (see Cyclone Gamane, March 2024), the framework includes an annex noting that an observational indicator (IDP displacement and/or rainfall exceedance) will be defined to support accelerated CERF rapid-response for events that do not meet Scenario 3. This is distinct from the pre-arranged AA trigger — it does not release pre-arranged AA funds, but facilitates faster post-impact CERF disbursement. The indicator had not been finalized at publication.

6. **Alignment with Start Network and Red Cross thresholds:** The Start Network uses a 166 km/h / 100 km / 3-day threshold (matching this framework's action window intensity and distance criteria). The Red Cross uses a lower 118 km/h threshold. By aligning with the Start Network, the OCHA framework can coordinate activation communications and avoid confusion.

## Open questions / known issues

- The observational indicator (IDP / rainfall back-trigger for sub-threshold CERF rapid response) was not finalized at the time of publication. Its definition and any eventual code should be captured here when completed.
- The framework covers the 2024/2025 and 2025/2026 cyclone seasons under the current CERF approval. Renewal and any design changes for 2026/2027 and beyond should be tracked as a new version.
- No automated monitoring pipeline is deployed in the repo. If CHD develops one, it should be registered here and in `pipelines/`.
- The `rsmc.py` copy-paste path artefact (`'moz'` raw data directory) should be corrected in a future repo cleanup — it is harmless but confusing.
- The return-period analysis in `04.0_combined_return_period.ipynb` was computed against data through 2024 and includes both the forecast-based trigger and the observational components. The return period for the combined trigger (forecast + obs IDP + obs rainfall) was computed but is not cited in the endorsed PDF, which focuses only on the forecast trigger.
- The repo's own readiness-window RP (~1-in-2 yr, exact forecast definition) and action-window RP (~1-in-3 yr, looser IBTrACS-observation proxy) diverge, while the PDF cites a single "~once every 3 years" figure for Scenario 3 overall. A future version should either compute the action-window RP under its exact forecast definition (lead time ≤72h, as in notebook 01.6.1) or clarify which figure is the calibration standard.
