# Malawi Drought — 2021

> No DS framework repo exists for this pilot; the authoritative source is the OCHA/CERF framework document (`framework_doc`). This page is a sourced summary from public OCHA/CERF/Anticipation-Hub material.

## Summary

Malawi's anticipatory action framework for **dry spells** was finalized in October 2021 as an OCHA/CERF pilot for the **2021/2022 rainy season**, designed by humanitarian partners under the Resident Coordinator with the Government of Malawi and OCHA/technical support. It uses a **hybrid two-stage trigger** — a **predictive** (forecast) component and an **observational** component — covering three drought-exposed districts in the Southern Region: **Balaka, Chikwawa and Nsanje**. CERF pre-arranged **up to US$7 million** (20% / US$1.4M to the predictive component, 80% / US$5.6M to the observational component), released only if and when the respective triggers were met. The framework **did not activate**: the trigger was not reached during the 2021/2022 season.

## Method

A dry spell is defined as **14 consecutive days with 0–2 mm of cumulative rainfall**. The framework pairs a forecast-based readiness leg with an observation-based action leg:

- **Stage I — predictive.** In **mid-November** (looking at January) or **mid-December** (looking at February), if the subseasonal forecast predicts a dry-spell event, funds are released to begin **Protection and WASH** activities ahead of the predicted shock (UNICEF, IOM, UNFPA).
- **Stage II — observational.** Starting in **January**, if a dry-spell event is **observed** (in the pilot districts) during January and/or February, funds are released to begin **Food Security & Livelihoods and Nutrition** activities (WFP, FAO, UNICEF).

The predictive component draws on the **ECMWF subseasonal (S2S)** forecasting system, which shows skill for these events at roughly a **10–30 day lead time** over the region; the observational component is computed from **CHIRPS** rainfall raster data.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** (Stage I) ECMWF subseasonal forecast of a dry spell; (Stage II) CHIRPS-observed dry spell (14 consecutive days, 0–2 mm).
- **Decision rule (plain language):** In mid-November and mid-December, check the subseasonal forecast for an upcoming dry spell in January / February respectively — if predicted, release the predictive-component funds for Protection/WASH early actions. Then, through January and February, check observed rainfall — if a dry spell has actually occurred in the pilot districts, release the (larger) observational-component funds for food-security, livelihoods and nutrition response.
- **Activation structure:** Two stages with **independent budgets** (predictive 20% / observational 80%), so each can fire on its own — this is a SPLIT (`all_in: false`) framework, not a single all-in envelope.
- **Calibration:** **Bespoke / absolute** — a fixed dry-spell definition (14 days, 0–2 mm cumulative rainfall) rather than a return-period calibration. Trigger development used a historical dry-spell record for Malawi (2000–2021).
- **Authoritative source:** the OCHA/CERF framework document (`framework_doc`). No DS framework repo implements it.
- **Operated by:** OCHA/CHD with national partners; no separate external operator identified.

## Trigger windows

**Counting rule:** two distinct activation windows — the predictive (Stage I) and observational (Stage II) components, each with its own budget. `n_windows = 2`.

Predictive decision points at **mid-November** and **mid-December** (subseasonal forecasts for Jan / Feb dry spells); observational monitoring through **January and February** using CHIRPS rainfall. No automated DS monitoring pipeline is documented for this pilot; monitoring was conducted by the framework partners during the season.

## Historical activations

**Never activated.** The trigger was **not reached during the 2021/2022 season** — process-learning reporting notes the trigger had not been met as of February 2022, which limited any performance assessment. `activations: []`.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Hybrid predictive + observational design.** A two-stage structure lets partners act early on a forecast (Protection/WASH readiness) while reserving the bulk of funding (80%) for confirmed, observed dry spells (food security, livelihoods, nutrition) — balancing the value of acting ahead of a shock against the limited skill of subseasonal forecasts.
- **Absolute dry-spell definition (14 days, 0–2 mm).** A locally meaningful, transparent definition tied to crop stress during the planting season, calibrated against a historical Malawi dry-spell record rather than a statistical return period.
- **Three pilot districts.** Balaka, Chikwawa and Nsanje were chosen as among the most dry-spell-exposed and vulnerable in the Southern Region.
- **Split budget (20/80).** Genuinely independent component envelopes so the readiness leg can fire without spending the larger response envelope.

## Open questions / known issues

- **Exact framework-doc date / PDF URL.** Finalized October 2021; the precise OCHA posting date and a direct PDF attachment URL were not confirmed — `framework_doc` points to the OCHA publications landing page.
- **Observational threshold wording.** Summaries say the observational trigger requires a dry spell "in at least three districts"; with exactly three pilot districts in scope this may mean all three. Needs the primary doc to confirm.
- **Target population.** Sources give "more than 185,000 families"; the equivalent individual count (`target_people`) is not stated.
- **Renewal / supersession.** No confirmed renewal of the CERF dry-spell framework after 2021/2022. A separate FAO/Germany-funded dry-spell AA project (OSRO/MLW/088/GER, 2023/24) exists but is not this framework — verify whether the OCHA/CERF framework was renewed, retired, or superseded.
