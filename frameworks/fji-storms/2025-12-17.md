# Fiji Tropical Cyclone — 2025-12-17

## Summary

The Fiji National Anticipatory Action Framework for Tropical Cyclones (Phase 2, 2025–2027)
activates pre-arranged financing and humanitarian action when RSMC Nadi forecasts a tropical
system whose wind field is expected to expose at least 5,000 people to ≥ 64-knot winds
(readiness: within the 120-hour forecast; action: within the 72-hour forecast), OR when IMERG
satellite rainfall data observes ≥ 190 mm average over two consecutive days across Fiji while
a tropical cyclone centre is within 250 km. On activation, CERF pre-arranged funds (~USD 2.4M),
agency co-financing, and IFRC DREF flow to clusters for cash transfers, protection kits, health/WASH
supplies, shelter kits, and early warning messaging. NDRMO leads; FMS provides the operational
forecast; OCHA CHD operates the automated trigger monitoring pipeline.

## Method

FMS produces a 120-hour TC track forecast (including category, position, and asymmetric wind-radii
by quadrant) every ~6 hours during a TC event. FMS emails the CSV to OCHA CHD, which runs an
automated Python script (GitHub Action `run_monitor_forecast_trigger.yml`). The script:

1. Parses the FMS CSV, building a GeoDataFrame of forecast positions.
2. Computes hourly-interpolated asymmetric wind-field buffers at 34-, 50-, and 64-knot thresholds
   using the FMS-regression model for wind radii (quadrant-radius columns from the CSV when
   available, falling back to a regression on MeanWind and latitude from `WIND_RADIUS_PARAMS_FMS`
   in `constants.py`).
3. Clips WorldPop 2020 1 km UN-adjusted population to each buffer to calculate exposed population.
4. Checks threshold: population exposed to ≥ 64-knot wind ≥ 5,000 (`EXP_THRESHOLD_64_KNOTS`).
5. Fires trigger emails (readiness if the condition is met within ≤120 hr leadtime; action if ≤72 hr)
   via Listmonk to pre-agreed distribution lists.
6. Additionally calculates perturbed-track exposures (36 directions × uncertainty cone) to provide
   best/worst-case scenario plots in the information email.

For the observational trigger (rainfall), a separate daily OCHA CHD script (not yet deployed as of
the monitoring-2025 branch) reads IMERG Late v07b data, computes the 2-day national average,
and checks ≥ 190 mm while a TC is within 250 km ± 1 day.

Active monitoring runs 1 October–31 May each year.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** (1) FMS 120/72-hour TC forecast CSV (wind field, quadrant radii) + WorldPop 2020
  population grid; (2) NASA IMERG Late daily satellite rainfall for observational window.
- **Decision rule (plain language):** A tropical system (depression, storm, or cyclone) triggers the
  framework if EITHER (a) its forecasted wind field will expose ≥ 5,000 people to hurricane-strength
  winds (≥ 64 knots, equivalent to Category 3+ Australian scale) within 5 days (readiness) or 3 days
  (action), OR (b) it has already caused national average rainfall ≥ 190 mm over two consecutive days.
- **Activation structure:** Three sequential/independent stages:
  - *Readiness:* exposure condition met anywhere in the ≤120-hr forecast.
  - *Action:* exposure condition met in the ≤72-hr forecast (independently evaluated).
  - *Observational:* IMERG 2-day average ≥ 190 mm while TC is within 250 km ± 1 day.
  Any stage can activate independently. CERF funds become available at readiness or action trigger;
  observational trigger can be activated concurrently or stand-alone.
- **Calibration:** Target return period ~3.1 years. Optimised over all combinations of wind-exposure
  (34/64 knot) × rainfall thresholds to: (i) hit the target RP; (ii) capture all three historical CERF
  storms (Winston 2016, Harold 2020, Yasa 2021); (iii) ensure the forecast trigger fires more often
  than the observational trigger; (iv) maximise total people affected among qualifying combinations.
  Selected: 5,000 exposed to 64 knots (OR) 190 mm rainfall.
- **Authoritative source:** 2025 framework PDF (endorsed 17 December 2025 by ERC).
- **Operated by:** OCHA CHD runs the automated monitoring pipeline; FMS (RSMC Nadi) is the
  forecast provider; the RC may pause an activation in outlier cases.

## Monitoring

FMS emails each 120-hour TC forecast CSV to data.science@humdata.org during active TC events.
A Power Automate flow POSTs the CSV (base64-encoded) as a parameter to the `Check Trigger`
GitHub Action (`run_monitor_forecast_trigger.yml`) on the `pa-aa-fji-storms` repo. The workflow
runs `pipelines/monitor_forecast_trigger.py`, which evaluates the trigger, sends Listmonk emails
(trigger alerts to `TRIGGER_LIST_IDS`; informational emails to `INFO_LIST_IDS`), and uploads
forecast data to blob storage. **Note: the production workflow is wired to the DEV blob slot**
(`DSCI_AZ_BLOB_DEV_SAS` / `DSCI_AZ_BLOB_DEV_SAS_WRITE` in the workflow `env:` block), so the live
trigger reads inputs from and writes outputs to the dev storage account — see `discrepancies`.
For the observational trigger, a separate daily OCHA CHD process
is described in the 2025 PDF (Section 5.3) but has not been automated in the current branch.
Active monitoring season: 1 October–31 May each year.

## Historical activations

**Never activated.** The Phase 1 pilot (2023–2025) ran through two full cyclone seasons without
triggering. Cyclone Rae (February 2025) was tracked (`exploration/ray_2025_check.md`) but its
forecast did not meet the population-exposure trigger conditions. An independent Process Learning
by the University of the South Pacific confirmed that operational readiness was built even in the
absence of activation. This is recorded as a substantive fact: `activations: []`.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Shift from category/distance to wind-field/population exposure (2023 → 2025):** The 2023
  framework used a simple categorical/distance rule (Cat 4 within 250 km OR Cat 3 making landfall).
  The May 2025 Framework Revision Workshop agreed to strengthen the trigger by incorporating
  actual forecast wind radii and population data. This makes the trigger directly impact-informed
  (people exposed to damaging winds) rather than proxy-based (category/distance). The 64-knot
  threshold (~Category 3 minimum on the Australian scale) was chosen as the wind speed above
  which significant damage occurs.
- **Addition of observational rainfall trigger:** Cyclone Cody (2022) caused ~70,000 affected
  despite being only Category 1, driven by heavy rainfall (200 mm+). The 2025 framework adds an
  IMERG-based observational trigger to capture heavy-rainfall events that the forecast wind trigger
  would miss. This introduces a mixed basis (forecast + observational).
- **Target return period 3.1 years:** Set to ensure the framework covers storms of the severity
  of historical CERF events (Winston 2016, Harold 2020, Yasa 2021) while not triggering too
  frequently. Calibration required all three CERF storms to be captured.
- **Monitoring season: Oct 1–May 31:** Extended one month beyond the official cyclone season
  (Nov–Apr) on each side as a precautionary buffer.
- **Broad TC scope:** The 2025 trigger applies to "tropical systems" (including depressions and
  tropical storms), not only named cyclones. This is a widening from the 2023 scope.
- **RC override retained:** The Resident Coordinator (in consultation with NDRMO) may pause
  an activation in theoretically possible outlier cases. This discretion is not encoded in the pipeline.
- **Budget:** Total pre-arranged financing ~USD 3.9M (CERF ~USD 2.4M pre-arranged + agency
  co-financing). Cash transfers via government social protection registry (WFP + MWCSP) target
  15,000 households (75,000 people) at FJD 200/household.

## Changes from previous version

The previous version was the **2023-11-13 framework** (endorsed 13 November 2023 by ERC;
approved 3 November 2023 by RC; Cabinet endorsed February 2024).

Key changes from 2023 to 2025:

1. **Trigger redesign:** Category/distance rule (Cat 4 within 250 km OR Cat 3 landfall) →
   wind-field/population-exposure rule (≥ 5,000 people in ≥ 64-knot forecast wind field) plus
   new observational rainfall trigger (IMERG ≥ 190 mm two-day national average).
2. **Third trigger stage added:** 2023 had two stages (readiness + action); 2025 adds an
   observational trigger for rainfall-driven events.
3. **TC scope broadened:** 2023 specified "tropical cyclone"; 2025 says "tropical system"
   (including depressions and tropical storms).
4. **Monitoring season formalised:** 2025 introduces explicit Oct 1–May 31 monitoring window.
5. **Activities updated:** Fishing-boat/water-tight drum kits (FAO/FRCS) removed; new voucher
   approach for selected health/protection; community-based anticipatory activities formalised
   with NDRM Act 2024 backing.
6. **Governance strengthened:** Now explicitly anchored in the National Disaster Risk Management
   Act 2024 (which replaced the 1998 Act); NDRMO rebranded from NDMO; CERF raised to
   USD 2.4M pre-arranged.
7. **Evaluation protocol added:** Johns Hopkins University multi-stakeholder evaluation protocol
   funded by FAO to assess the framework when activated.
