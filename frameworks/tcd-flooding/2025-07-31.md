# Chad Flood — 2025-07-31

> The canonical trigger is the code at `code_ref`; this page explains it, it does not redefine it.

## Summary

The Chad anticipatory action framework for riverine flooding uses daily GloFAS v4 ensemble-mean discharge forecasts at the N'Djamena station on the Chari River to trigger two sequential windows: a "readiness" (mobilisation) window at ≤14 days leadtime and an "action" window at ≤10 days. Both windows use the same discharge threshold of 4,542 m³/s (approximately a 4-yr return period in the reforecast). Either trigger activating releases up to USD 4 million CERF pre-arranged funds for UNHCR, UNICEF, FAO, and WFP to intervene in two provinces: N'Djamena and Mayo-Kebbi Est (Bongor area). The framework was first activated in 2024; this July 2025 version is a revised edition based on lessons from that activation, covering the 2025 and 2026 flood seasons.

## Method

Each day, OCHA's Centre for Humanitarian Data (CDH) automatically reads the GloFAS ensemble forecast (CDS API, `cems-glofas-forecast`, product type: ensemble perturbed forecasts) for the N'Djamena station (Chari River downstream of Logone confluence, coords ~12.125°N, 15.025°E). The ensemble mean discharge over each lead-day is compared against the threshold. If the mean exceeds 4,542 m³/s within the relevant leadtime window, the trigger fires. CDH notifies OCHA, DRE (Direction des Ressources en Eau), and ANAM (Agence Nationale de Météorologie) by email and WhatsApp; DRE/ANAM must confirm within 24 hours. The CERF secretariat then sends approval letters immediately, 100% of funds are disbursed at the readiness trigger, and agencies may begin mobilisation activities. The remaining "action" portion of each agency's budget is unlocked only if the 10-day trigger also fires during the same season.

The threshold was derived from a reforecast skill analysis (`analysis/glofas_reforecast_2025.md`): the 4-yr RP level in the GloFAS reanalysis (2003–2023) is ~4,542 m³/s. At ≤10 days leadtime this corresponds to a ~4.0-yr RP in the reforecast; at ≤14 days it corresponds to ~3.0-yr RP — the lower RP satisfies the CERF requirement of ≥3-yr RP for readiness.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** GloFAS v4 ensemble mean river discharge (m³/s) at N'Djamena, Chari River ("Chari A Ndjamena - Ex Fort-Lamy-", downstream of Logone confluence)
- **Decision rule (plain language):** Each day from July onwards, check whether any forecast day within the relevant lead-time window has an ensemble-mean discharge ≥ 4,542 m³/s. If yes within 14 days → readiness trigger. If yes within 10 days → action trigger. Either trigger launches both provinces simultaneously.
- **Activation structure:** Two-stage sequential. Readiness fires first (≤14 days); action may fire independently or after readiness (≤10 days). CERF funds are released at readiness; the action portion is unlocked only when action also fires. If action fires without a prior readiness (possible if forecasts only cross the threshold close to the deadline), both windows are treated as activated simultaneously.
- **Calibration:** The 4,542 m³/s threshold is the interpolated 4-yr return-period level derived from GloFAS reanalysis discharge peaks (2003–2023) at N'Djamena. The reforecast analysis showed this threshold captures 5 out of 5 high-severity years (TPR=100%) at ≤10 days leadtime, and 7 years including moderate years at ≤14 days.
- **Authoritative source:** The July 2025 revised framework PDF (`framework_doc`). The repo (`analysis/glofas_reforecast_2025.md`, `src/monitoring/etl.py`) implements and validates it.
- **Operated by:** OCHA CHD runs the automated monitoring script. DRE and ANAM provide operational confirmation.

## Monitoring

CDH runs an automated daily pipeline (GitHub Actions, `cron: '0 20 * * *'`) that:
1. Downloads the daily GloFAS ensemble forecast for N'Djamena from the CDS API
2. Saves raw data to Azure Blob Storage (`pa-aa-tcd-flooding/raw/glofas/monitoring/`)
3. Saves processed values to the DB table `projects.pa_aa_tcd_flooding_monitoring`
4. Generates a forecast chart and uploads to blob
5. Sends emails: always an "informational" email on Mondays or when forecasts are near or above the threshold; trigger-specific emails (readiness or action templates) when thresholds are exceeded

The monitoring pipeline was developed on the `add-quarto` branch and runs daily (emails are sent in French). Note: the `STAGE` env var (`monitoring.yml`) only switches the **email distribution list** between a test list and the real one in `send_emails.py` — it does NOT route the data writes. The blob/DB pipeline (`etl.py`, `check_forecasts.py`, `plot.py`) hardcodes the `dev` slot, so forecast data and plots are always saved to dev infrastructure regardless of `STAGE`. See the dev-slot discrepancy above.

## Historical activations

The framework was **activated in the 2024 flood season**. The repo README records "_Update 2024-09-28_: framework has been activated based on GloFAS forecasts", dating the activation to on/around 28 September 2024 (which window fired and the disbursement amount are not documented). The 2025 revision explicitly states: "À la suite de l'activation de cadre en 2024, sur la base des leçons apprises, le Groupe de Travail Action Anticipatoire du Tchad a jaugé la pertinence de réviser le document." The 2024 activation was against the initial 2024-09-26 version of the framework (which covered only N'Djamena province and included 7 agencies including WHO, UNFPA, and IOM). The 2025 revision expands geographic scope, rationalises implementing agencies, and improves targeting strategies based on those lessons.

Note: The GloFAS reforecast analysis shows the trigger would have fired historically in approximately 2010, 2022, 2003, 2012, and 2005 for the action window; and additionally 2018 and 2007 for the readiness window (based on reforecast data 2003–2023). The 2024 activation is not in this backtested window as that analysis ran through 2023.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **Single station, single threshold for both provinces:** The N'Djamena GloFAS station (on the Chari downstream of the Logone confluence) is used for both N'Djamena and Mayo-Kebbi Est provinces. A Bongor station (Logone River) was investigated for the 2025 revision but including it did not improve performance; the single-station approach is kept for simplicity. Mayo-Kebbi Est is upstream of N'Djamena so flooding there can occur before the Chari peaks at N'Djamena — this is an accepted trade-off.
- **Same threshold for readiness and action:** Both windows use 4,542 m³/s. The CERF requirement for the readiness/mobilisation trigger is a ≥3-yr return period; the analysis showed that applying the 4-yr RP threshold to a 14-day window produces ~3.0-yr RP (due to forecast variability at longer leadtimes). Using a lower threshold for readiness was considered but rejected in favour of keeping a unified, simple threshold.
- **Ensemble mean as trigger indicator:** The trigger uses the ensemble mean (sometimes called the "control forecast" in the PDF, though this is imprecise — the actual GloFAS "control" is the unperturbed member; the PDF footnote clarifies it means the ensemble mean). This is simple, reproducible, and directly comparable to the reforecast analysis.
- **CERF disbursement design:** All funds disburse at readiness; the action portion is escrowed within agency budgets. This allows mobilisation to begin immediately but provides a check: if the flood does not materialize into the 10-day window, the unspent action funds are returned.
- **GloFAS calibration bias noted but accepted:** The known ~31-day early bias in GloFAS reanalysis vs. observed DRE levels means the forecasted trigger date leads the actual flood peak by ~1 month. This is considered a feature, not a bug, for AA purposes: it provides real lead time even if the trigger formally fires at "1-day leadtime" in forecast terms.

## Changes from previous version

**Previous version:** 2024-09-26 (the initial endorsed framework)

Major changes in the 2025-07-31 revision:
1. **Geographic expansion:** Added Mayo-Kebbi Est Province (Bongor area) as a second target province. The original framework covered only N'Djamena Province. The Bongor GloFAS station investigation confirmed that the N'Djamena station alone captures Mayo-Kebbi Est flood years correctly.
2. **Agency changes:** WHO, UNFPA, and IOM were removed from the framework. Remaining: UNHCR, UNICEF, FAO, WFP. Budget allocation unchanged at USD 4 million total.
3. **Improved targeting strategies:** More detailed geographic and household targeting criteria defined for each agency and sector (Shelter/NFI, WASH, Food Security).
4. **Trigger renewal:** The framework is renewed to cover the 2025 and 2026 seasons (two-year CERF approval).
5. **Trigger thresholds unchanged:** 4,542 m³/s, 14-day readiness, 10-day action — these were confirmed by the 2025 reforecast reanalysis.

## Open questions / known issues

- **2024 activation date and outcome not documented in repo:** The exact trigger date, which window(s) fired, and the documented outcomes from the 2024 AAR are not in the repo or textually extractable from the PDF. A dedicated activation record (or link to the AAR report) would strengthen the KB entry.
- **Target beneficiary count not stated:** The framework specifies agency budgets and geographic targeting zones (specific arrondissements in N'Djamena, Bongor zone in Mayo-Kebbi Est) but does not state a total target beneficiary or household count in the document.
- **GloFAS interface deprecation:** The PDF footnote 7 notes "il n'est plus possible de vérifier le déclencheur directement sur l'interface GloFAS, car l'interface ne montre pas les valeurs numériques des prévisions." The pipeline correctly reads from the CDS API, not the deprecated interface.
- **`CERF_YEARS` constant not updated:** `CERF_YEARS = [2012, 2022]` in `src/constants.py` was likely set during early calibration to identify historical CERF-relevant years. It has not been updated to reflect the confirmed 2024 activation. Low priority.
- **Additional donor co-financing:** The PDF (Section 5, "Autres donateurs") notes that WFP, UNICEF, and UNHCR have identified additional resource opportunities for the 2025 season, but no amounts are committed. Co-financing is explicitly NOT part of the pre-arranged CERF envelope.
