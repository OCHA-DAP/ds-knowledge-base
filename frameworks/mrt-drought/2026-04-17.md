# Mauritania Drought — 2026-04-17

## Summary

The Mauritania drought anticipatory action framework (endorsed by the Emergency Relief Coordinator on 17 April 2026) triggers on severe national-level drought — events with approximately a 1-in-3- to 1-in-4-year return period — and releases USD 2.5 million CERF to five agencies (FAO, IOM, WFP, UNICEF) for pre-agreed multisectoral activities in the three moughataas of Wompou, Selibaby, and Ould Yengé in the Guidimakha region. The framework has two independent, non-cumulative triggers corresponding to two seasonal windows: a forecast-based trigger (February–May, based on ONM seasonal forecasts displayed in the NCDP Maproom) and an observational trigger (August–September, based on cumulative CHIRPS dekadal rainfall). The first activation of either trigger releases 100% of the CERF budget; subsequent triggers in the same season are ignored.

## Method

**Data chain:**
1. **Window 1 (forecast):** ONM produces seasonal rainfall forecasts for July–August–September (JAS), calibrated specifically for Mauritania in collaboration with Columbia University's National Center for Disaster Preparedness (NCDP, formerly IRI). These forecasts are displayed as tercile probabilities in the NCDP Maproom for Mauritania (JAS fcst v8). OCHA/CHD monitors the Maproom monthly from February through May.
2. **Window 2 (observational):** CHIRPS dekadal rainfall data (final version, sourced from HDX) is aggregated as cumulative national-average precipitation from the first dekad of July onwards. OCHA computes the cumulative total against a set of static dekad-specific thresholds and validates jointly with ONM.

The trigger mechanism was developed jointly by ONM, NCDP/Columbia University, OCHA/CHD, and the GTSAA. The analysis code is in the `ds-aa-mrt-drought` GitHub repository on the `initial-analysis` branch.

## Trigger logic

- **Keys off:** (1) ONM JAS seasonal forecast probability in the NCDP Maproom (top-45% drought signal); (2) CHIRPS cumulative national-average dekadal precipitation against static thresholds.
- **Decision rule (plain language):**
  - *Window 1:* Each month from February to May, check whether the ONM forecast places Mauritania in the top 45% of worst drought years for the JAS season. If this condition is met in **two consecutive months**, the forecast trigger fires. Possible firing dates: end-March (Feb+Mar), end-April (Mar+Apr), or end-May (Apr+May).
  - *Window 2:* From the 2nd dekad of July through the 3rd dekad of August, check whether national cumulative CHIRPS rainfall since 1 July is below the dekad-specific threshold. If yes at any dekad check, the observational trigger fires.
- **Activation structure:** The two windows are independent and non-cumulative. The first trigger to fire ends the season — all CERF funds are disbursed for the corresponding activity package and subsequent triggers are disregarded. If neither fires, funds roll over to the following season within the two-year validity window (2026–2027).
- **Calibration:** Targeting approximately 1-in-4-year events (the PDF's stated target), implemented as a 45% percentile threshold for Window 1 and static per-dekad thresholds for Window 2. However, the Annex 1 footnote clarifies the "worst years" definition as "rank 12 or worse" in ~25 historical years, implying closer to 1-in-3 years in practice (see discrepancies).
- **Authoritative source:** The 17 April 2026 framework PDF (`framework_doc`). Analysis derivation: `code_ref`.
- **Operated by:** Window 1 — the live trigger is read from the NCDP Maproom (https://iridl.ldeo.columbia.edu/fbfmaproom2/mauritania) with the "Frequency of trigger events" setting at 45% and version "JAS fcst v8." Window 2 — OCHA reads CHIRPS "final" data from HDX. Neither window is automated; both are monitored manually by OCHA with ONM co-validation.

## Monitoring

No automated monitoring pipeline exists in the repo. During the season:
- **February–May:** OCHA staff check the NCDP Maproom monthly for the ONM forecast signal. ONM is responsible for communicating the forecast result; IRI/NCDP and OCHA validate jointly before informing the GTSAA.
- **July–August:** OCHA monitors CHIRPS cumulative national rainfall from HDX against the per-dekad thresholds. Results are validated jointly with ONM within 24 hours of a potential threshold breach.

Both triggers generate regular communications to stakeholders whether or not the threshold is reached, to avoid confusion. Pre-written email templates are prepared for each scenario. The activation protocol has three steps: (1) data validation, (2) activation validation (DCAN convenes GTSAA within 24 hours), (3) execution (DCAN communicates to government, RC office, CERF, and ROWCA).

Never activated. The 2026 season is the first pilot season. Historical back-testing (Annex 1) shows:
- The 9 worst historical years (approximately RP 3 years) identified as: 2001, 2002, 2007, 2011, 2014, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2021.
- With the forecast trigger: would have fired in 4 of those 9 years; 0 false alarms.
- With the observational trigger: would have fired in 5 of those 9 years; 0 false alarms.
- The PDF's Annex 1 also shows that recent historical CERF drought allocations to Mauritania align broadly with the identified worst years (specific allocation years are presented in an image table in the PDF, not in the extracted text; the analysis sources them from `mrt_drought_allocations.parquet`, with the CERF window covering years ≥ 2007).

Note: The PDF (footnote 15) explicitly acknowledges that historical back-test trigger years in its table do not match the Maproom display, due to different methods for historical reconstruction. Only the current-year reading from the Maproom is considered authoritative.

## Key decisions & rationale

- **National scope for the trigger, subnational scope for activities:** The trigger is calibrated on national-average rainfall (IRI Maproom covers all of Mauritania; CHIRPS threshold is for national average), while activities target only Guidimakha. This decouples trigger sensitivity (national signal, better calibrated) from operational targeting (subnational, where impact is highest). The IPC and CERF allocation history confirmed Guidimakha as the priority region.
- **Two independent non-cumulative windows:** A forecast window allows longer lead time for preparedness activities (planting inputs, cash, WASH infrastructure); an observational window allows later confirmation when the forecast missed a drought. Both release the full budget for different activity packages, so the design rewards early action without double-penalising when only the late signal fires.
- **45% worst-years threshold:** Chosen to match a target RP of approximately 3–4 years (see discrepancy note) — a "severe" drought frequency consistent with historical CERF responses and WFP's existing PAA threshold.
- **ONM vs. global models:** The framework explicitly prioritises national sources when equally skilled. The ONM/NCDP system is described as more accurate for Mauritania than global models; it has an existing operational presence through WFP's PAA.
- **WFP PAA co-financing:** The WFP Plan d'Action Anticipatoire adopts the same trigger mechanism, so a CERF activation will top-up existing WFP PAA activities rather than duplicate them. The CRM Protocole d'Alerte Précoce uses a different (more moderate) threshold and different data sources — harmonisation is ongoing.
- **Geographic scope of activities (Guidimakha only):** The three moughataas of Wompou, Selibaby, and Ould Yengé were selected by GTSAA consensus, aligning with WFP PAA geographic targeting criteria (food insecurity recurrence, 30-year drought frequency, access, agropastoral livelihoods, malnutrition recurrence).

## Changes from previous version

This is the first published version of a collective inter-agency AA framework for Mauritania drought. Previous related instruments (WFP PAA, CRM PAP) are agency-specific, not superseded — they continue in parallel with different (more moderate) thresholds.

## Open questions / known issues

- **RP target inconsistency in the PDF** must be resolved before the next version: the body says 1-in-4 years, the Annex footnote says 1-in-3 years. The practical threshold (45% / "rank 12 or worse") implies ~3 years given the historical record length (~25 years).
- **Maproom vs. repo back-test mismatch** (footnote 15): the historical trigger table in the PDF differs from the Maproom display. The current-year reading is correct, but the discrepancy could confuse stakeholders or future analysts. The repo should document the exact back-test method used and why it differs.
- **`full_trigger-Copy1.md` is a Niger analysis file** left in the MRT repo — should be removed to avoid confusion.
- **AOI pcode `MR10` versioning:** In the current CODAB, `MR10` = Guidimakha; in an older CODAB version used in some IPC files, `MR10` = Nouakchott. The ipc.md notebook flags this but the constants.py hardcodes `MR10` without specifying the CODAB version. This is a risk if data pipelines mix CODAB versions.
- **Stale `CERF_YEARS` constant:** `src/constants.py` hardcodes `CERF_YEARS = {2021, 2022, 2023}`, which matches neither the framework's worst-years list nor the real CERF allocation history (sourced from `mrt_drought_allocations.parquet`). Looks like a placeholder leftover; should be removed or corrected before any monitoring code reuses it.
- **Superseded analysis files left in repo:** `iri.md` (1991–2024, `iri_cerf_stats.parquet`, `rp_overall_target=4`) and `iri_rp_modeling.md` / `obsv_chirps_dekad.md` (`rp_overall_target=7`) predate the endorsed v8 design in `full_trigger.md` (2001–2025, `rp_target=3.5`, `q=0.45`). They should be clearly marked as exploratory so a future reader does not mistake them for the final analysis.
- **No deployed monitoring:** OCHA staff must manually query the Maproom and CHIRPS during the season. A lightweight automated monitor (e.g., a GHA workflow or marimo dashboard) would reduce missed-trigger risk.
- **CRM PAP harmonisation:** Discussions are described as ongoing to align the CRM's PAP trigger with the GTSAA framework. Until harmonised, simultaneous activations at different thresholds are possible.
- **2025 pilot season clarification:** The executive summary in the English section refers to "The 2026 season will serve as a pilot phase," while the French section uses "La saison 2025 constituera une phase pilote." The English reference is internally consistent with the approval date (April 2026) and the two-year validity period (2026–2027); the French mention of "2025" appears to be a typo for 2026.
